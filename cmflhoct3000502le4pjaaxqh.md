</p><p>Lyocell is a man-made cellulose fiber derived from sustainably sourced wood pulp, commonly known by the brand name Tencel. This eco-friendly fiber is produced through a closed-loop solvent spinning process that minimizes chemical waste and water consumption. The fiber's unique properties include high absorbency, breathability, and biodegradability, making it suitable for applications in apparel, home textiles, and medical products.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing consumer demand for sustainable textiles and stringent environmental regulations favoring eco-conscious materials. While conventional fibers like cotton require significant water and pesticides, Lyocell's closed-loop production offers a greener alternative. Furthermore, innovations in fiber blending techniques are expanding its use in high-performance fabrics. Key players such as Lenzing AG dominate the market, leveraging their proprietary production technologies to meet growing demand across North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific regions.</p><p>
