</p><p>Sodium percarbonate, a chemical compound with the formula <strong>2Na2CO3·3H2O2</strong>, serves as a powerful oxidizing agent and bleaching component in washing additives. It decomposes into oxygen, water, and sodium carbonate upon dissolution, making it an environmentally preferable alternative to chlorine-based bleaches. The compound is widely used in <strong>powdery and liquid washing products</strong> due to its stain-removing and disinfecting properties.</p><p>
</p><p>While the market shows steady growth, key players like <strong>Solvay, Evonik, and Kemira</strong> are investing in production capacity expansions to meet rising demand. </p><p>Global sodium percarbonate market is experiencing significant growth due to increasing consumer preference for <strong>environmentally friendly cleaning products</strong>. The European market, for instance, has seen a 12% annual increase in demand for eco-friendly cleaning chemicals since 2020, with sodium percarbonate emerging as a preferred choice for household and industrial applications alike.</p><p>

Recent formulation innovations have improved the stability and effectiveness of sodium percarbonate in both powder and liquid detergents, making it more versatile across different product formats. The uncoated form of the chemical has a relatively short shelf life in humid environments, requiring special packaging and storage conditions that increase costs. This limitation particularly affects tropical regions where humidity levels average above 70%, potentially limiting market penetration in these areas without additional protective measures.</p><p>
