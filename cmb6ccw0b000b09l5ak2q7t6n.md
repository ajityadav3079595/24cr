</p><p>Diisononyl Adipate (DINA) is a colorless, oily liquid that is commonly used as a plasticizer in various polymeric systems, such as vinyl, nitrocellulose, and rubber. It is added to these systems to enhance flexibility at low temperatures. DINA is often used as an alternative to regulated, high molecular weight phthalates, which have raised concerns due to their potential health and environmental impacts. Overall, DINA is an important additive in the plastics industry, as it helps to improve the performance of various products, particularly in applications where low-temperature flexibility is critical.</p><p>
