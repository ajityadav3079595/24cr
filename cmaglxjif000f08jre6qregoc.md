</p><p><strong>Glass wool</strong> is a category of fibrous insulating material made from glass fibers. It falls under the broad category of artificial inorganic fibers. The raw materials primarily include <strong>quartz sand, limestone, dolomite, and other natural ores</strong>, which are melted at high temperatures and then converted into fine fibers using a blowing technique.</p><p>
</p><p>These fibers are intricately intertwined to form a <strong>three-dimensional porous structure</strong>, resulting in a product with excellent thermal and acoustic insulation properties. The numerous small air gaps in the material act as barriers to heat flow and sound waves, thus making glass wool a superior insulating material.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Glass Wool Board</strong> refers specifically to the board-form variant of this material. It offers exceptional <strong>thermal resistance, sound absorption, lightweight structure, and high tensile strength</strong>. With service temperatures up to <strong>250Â°C</strong>, glass wool boards are particularly favored in residential and commercial constructions for wall, roof, and HVAC insulation.</p><p>
