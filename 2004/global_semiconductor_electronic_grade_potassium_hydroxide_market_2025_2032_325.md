</p><p><strong>Overview</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Semiconductor and Electronic Grade Potassium Hydroxide (KOH)</strong> refers to a high-purity form of potassium hydroxide used extensively in the electronics industry. It plays a crucial role in several applications, especially in the production of integrated circuits (ICs), very large-scale integrated circuits (VLSIs), solar photovoltaic cells, and liquid crystal displays (LCDs). This specialized form of KOH is characterized by its minimal levels of impurities, making it suitable for the sensitive processes involved in these high-tech industries.</p><p>
</p><p>In the electronics sector, KOH is used primarily for its cleaning, etching, and wafer processing properties. It helps clean the surfaces of silicon wafers during IC manufacturing, ensuring no contaminants remain that could affect the performance of the finished product. KOH’s role in etching processes—whether for semiconductor substrates or LCD glass—also makes it indispensable. Given its high purity, semiconductor and electronic grade KOH ensures minimal damage to the sensitive materials it interacts with, making it an essential material in advanced manufacturing processes.</p><p>

It is used in the manufacturing of silicon wafers for photovoltaic panels, ensuring they are properly cleaned and etched for optimal performance.</p><p><strong>Advances in LCD Technology</strong>: The continued innovation in LCD and OLED technology, where KOH is used in cleaning and etching glass substrates, propels the demand for high-quality electronic-grade potassium hydroxide.</p><p>
