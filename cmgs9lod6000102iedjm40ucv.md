</p><p>C/C composites offer remarkable performance in extreme environments, but their intricate fabrication methods present ongoing restraints to broader market scalability. This complexity not only inflates costs but also extends lead times, making it challenging for industries requiring rapid prototyping or high-volume output. Moreover, the inherent brittleness of C/C materials under certain impact conditions can restrict their use in applications demanding high toughness, prompting engineers to seek hybrid reinforcements or alternative composites.</p><p>
</p><p>These technical limitations are particularly evident in scaling from laboratory to industrial production, where inconsistencies in densification lead to defects like porosity that compromise structural integrity. Addressing these through process optimizations, such as advanced chemical vapor infiltration, is essential but requires substantial technological leaps to fully mitigate the restraint through the forecast to 2032.</p><p>
