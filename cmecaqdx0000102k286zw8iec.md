<strong>MARKET DRIVERS</strong></p><p>

</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Stringent Emission Norms Accelerating Technological Advancements</strong></p><p>

The transition towards low-viscosity oils in modern engines has further increased the importance of effective cooling solutions, as these thinner oils require precise temperature regulation to maintain their protective properties.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Aftermarket Growth Driven by Aging Vehicle Fleet</strong></p><p>

The aftermarket segment benefits from both routine maintenance requirements and the growing DIY culture among vehicle owners, supported by online tutorials and readily available replacement parts.</p><p>
<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Electric Vehicle Adoption Threatens Traditional Market</strong></p><p>
</p><p>While the automotive engine oil coolant market continues to grow, the rapid adoption of electric vehicles presents a significant long-term challenge. Battery electric vehicles (BEVs) accounted for nearly 8% of new vehicle sales in 2023, and this figure is projected to reach 40% by 2030. BEVs either eliminate or drastically reduce the need for traditional engine oil cooling systems, instead focusing on battery and power electronics cooling. This paradigm shift is forcing traditional coolant manufacturers to diversify their product portfolios and develop new solutions for electrified powertrains.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Price Volatility of Raw Materials Impacts Profit Margins</strong></p><p>

This volatility creates challenges for manufacturers in maintaining stable pricing and profit margins, particularly in the competitive aftermarket segment where price sensitivity is high. The situation is further complicated by rising energy costs associated with manufacturing and transportation.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Competition From Alternative Cooling Technologies</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Emerging thermal management technologies, particularly in the area of passive cooling systems and advanced heat pipes, are beginning to challenge traditional liquid-based cooling solutions. These alternatives often promise lower maintenance requirements and better long-term reliability, appealing to both automakers and consumers. While adoption rates remain low currently, continued advancements in materials science could accelerate their market penetration, potentially displacing conventional oil coolants in certain applications.</p><p>

The hybrid market is projected to grow at over 15% annually through 2030, offering coolant manufacturers a strategic avenue for revenue diversification.</p><p>
