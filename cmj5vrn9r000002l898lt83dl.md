</p><p>Neutral polishing enzymes are specialized biocatalysts used to enhance fabric quality in textile finishing processes. These enzymes operate at neutral pH levels (6-8) and are primarily employed for bio-polishing cellulosic fabrics to remove surface fibrils, improve color brightness, and reduce pilling. Key enzyme types include cellulases, pectinases, and amylases, which are increasingly replacing traditional chemical treatments due to their eco-friendly properties and superior fabric-handling characteristics.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by expanding textile manufacturing in Vietnam and Indonesia, coupled with rising demand for sustainable fabric finishing solutions. Furthermore, regulatory pressures to reduce wastewater pollution from textile mills are accelerating adoption. Recent developments include Novozymes' 2023 launch of FiberCare® Ultra, a next-generation neutral cellulase that reduces water consumption by 20% during denim finishing processes.</p><p>
