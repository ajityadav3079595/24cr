</p><p><strong>Dicyclohexylamine (DCHA)</strong> is a colorless, flammable liquid amine primarily utilized as a chemical intermediate in various industrial processes. Belonging to the family of secondary amines, DCHA is produced by the hydrogenation of aniline or cyclohexylamine and exhibits significant solubility in organic solvents. It is widely used in the synthesis of agrochemicals, rubber accelerators, dyes, corrosion inhibitors, and pharmaceutical intermediates. Due to its versatility and chemical reactivity, DCHA plays a vital role across multiple sectors, contributing to enhanced product performance and durability in end-use applications.Market Dynamics (Drivers, Restraints, Opportunities, and Challenges)</p><p>
