</p><p>High-Temperature (High-Temp) Release Agents are specialized chemical substances designed to facilitate the easy removal of molded parts from molds operating under extremely high temperatures. These release agents are primarily utilized in manufacturing processes that involve the molding of materials such as light metals (e.g., aluminum, magnesium) and glass, which require mold temperatures exceeding 1562°F (842°C). The core function of these agents is to prevent materials from sticking to the mold surface, thereby improving surface finish quality, reducing defects, and extending mold life.</p><p>
</p><p>These agents can be water-based or solvent-based and are formulated with materials that can withstand thermal degradation while maintaining consistent release performance. Industries such as automotive, aerospace, foundry, and construction heavily rely on high-temp release agents to ensure efficiency in casting, forging, and compression molding applications.</p><p>
