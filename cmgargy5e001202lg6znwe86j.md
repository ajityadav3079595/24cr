<strong>MARKET CHALLENGES</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>High Production Costs Remain Significant Barrier to Widespread Adoption</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Despite technological advancements, silica aerogel blankets continue to face cost-related challenges that limit market penetration in price-sensitive sectors. The complex supercritical drying process required for production remains energy-intensive, accounting for approximately 60% of total manufacturing costs. While prices have decreased from historic highs, aerogel blankets still command premium pricing - typically 3-5 times higher than conventional insulation materials. This cost differential makes justification difficult for budget-conscious projects, particularly in developing markets where initial cost often outweighs lifecycle savings considerations.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Other Challenges</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Material Fragility Concerns</strong></p><p>
The delicate nature of aerogel structures presents handling challenges during installation. Despite reinforcement with flexible substrates, aerogel blankets can experience performance degradation if improperly installed, requiring trained personnel that may not be readily available in all regions.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Supply Chain Complexities</strong></p><p>
The specialized raw materials and production equipment required contribute to longer lead times compared to traditional insulation. Recent global supply chain disruptions have further exacerbated these challenges, causing project delays in some cases.</p><p>
