</p><p><strong>Methyl 9-decenoate (9-DAME)</strong> is an organic compound classified as an ester, produced by the reaction of methanol with 9-decenoic acid. Belonging to the family of fatty acid methyl esters, it typically presents as a colorless to pale yellow liquid. Methyl 9-decenoate is known for its distinctive floral and fruity aroma, which makes it a valued ingredient in the <strong>flavor and fragrance industry</strong>. Beyond its aromatic applications, this compound has potential in <strong>bio-based chemical synthesis</strong>, <strong>lubricant formulations</strong>, and <strong>biodegradable surfactants</strong>, positioning it as a versatile raw material in multiple industrial domains. Its renewable origin from plant-based oils also supports sustainable chemical manufacturing practices.</p><p>
