</p><p>Indium Tin Oxide (ITO) Conductive Film is a transparent conductive material widely used in electronic displays, touch panels, and photovoltaic applications. It is composed of a thin layer of indium tin oxide deposited on a substrate, typically glass or polyethylene terephthalate (PET). The film exhibits excellent electrical conductivity and optical transparency, making it ideal for applications requiring both properties. Key products in this market include films with varying surface resistance levels, categorized as below 40Ω, 40-80Ω, 80-120Ω, and above 120Ω. These films are essential components in LCD screens, touch panels, solar batteries, and smart glass applications.
