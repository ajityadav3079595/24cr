</p><p>Carbon electrodes are conductive components primarily manufactured from anthracite, petroleum coke, and pitch. These cost-effective alternatives to graphite electrodes are indispensable in high-temperature industrial applications, particularly in electric arc furnaces for steel production. Unlike their graphite counterparts, carbon electrodes skip the energy-intensive graphitization process, giving them both economic and environmental advantages.</p><p>
