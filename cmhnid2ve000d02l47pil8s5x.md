</p><p>Naphthenic base oils are specialized hydrocarbon fluids produced through hydrotreatment of petroleum fractions. These low-pour-point oils contain less than 0.5% wax content and demonstrate superior solvency properties compared to paraffinic alternatives. Their unique molecular structure - characterized by saturated cyclic hydrocarbons - enables excellent performance in low-temperature applications and compatibility with elastomers. These properties make them ideal for transformer oils, industrial lubricants, metalworking fluids, and rubber processing.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from the energy sector, particularly for transformer oils in power grids, and expanding applications in industrial lubrication. While environmental regulations pose challenges for some petroleum products, naphthenic oils maintain demand due to their biodegradability advantages.
