This premium fuel grade exhibits exceptional characteristics including low sulfur content, minimal impurities, and high heat output (approximately 25-30 MJ/kg), making it ideal for specialized industrial applications. Anthracite is primarily classified into standard, high, and ultra-high grades based on carbon content and calorific value.</p><p>

Anthracite's high carbon content and low volatile matter make it particularly valuable for blast furnace injection and sinter production. Emerging economies in Asia and Africa are fueling this demand as they ramp up infrastructure development, with China alone accounting for over 50% of global steel output.</p><p>

This renewed focus on energy independence is expected to sustain demand through 2032, particularly in regions with domestic anthracite reserves.</p><p>

While metallurgical applications remain somewhat insulated, the broader decarbonization trend creates uncertainty for long-term investment in anthracite mining infrastructure.</p><p>
