</p><p>Lanolin is a natural wax secreted by sheep's sebaceous glands, primarily obtained as a byproduct of wool processing. This multifunctional substance consists mainly of sterol esters and possesses unique emollient, waterproofing and protective properties. The material is categorized into two main types: anhydrous lanolin (water-free) and hydrous lanolin (water-containing), each serving distinct industrial applications.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is driven by increasing demand from the personal care and cosmetics sector, where lanolin is prized for its moisturizing properties in products like lip balms and lotions.
