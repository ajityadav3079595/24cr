</p><p>Ultrafine Ground Calcium Carbonate (GCC) is a high-purity, finely ground form of calcium carbonate, primarily used as a filler and coating agent in various industries. It is produced by grinding natural limestone or marble into fine particles, typically below 2 microns in size. The product is widely used in the <strong>paper industry</strong> to improve brightness and opacity, in the <strong>plastics industry</strong> to enhance mechanical properties, and in the <strong>paints and coatings industry</strong> to improve durability and texture. End User Industry Analysis</p><p>
</p><p>The <strong>paper industry</strong> is the largest consumer of ultrafine GCC, accounting for a significant share of the market. GCC is used as a filler and coating agent to enhance paper quality and reduce production costs. The <strong>plastics industry</strong> also relies heavily on GCC to improve the mechanical properties of plastic products, such as tensile strength and impact resistance. In the <strong>paints and coatings industry</strong>, GCC is used to improve opacity, durability, and texture. The <strong>adhesives and sealants industry</strong> uses GCC to enhance bonding strength and viscosity. Other industries, such as construction and pharmaceuticals, also contribute to demand, albeit to a lesser extent.</p><p>
