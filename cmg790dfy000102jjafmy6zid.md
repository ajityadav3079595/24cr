</p><p></p><p>
</p><p>Mold discharging agents (also called release agents) are functional substances applied between molds and finished products to prevent adhesion. These agents demonstrate chemical resistance against various resin chemistries, particularly styrene and amines, while maintaining stability under heat and stress conditions. Key properties include non-transferability to processed parts and compatibility with secondary operations like painting. The market offers two primary formulations: <strong>internal release agents</strong> (mixed directly into materials) and <strong>external release agents</strong> (applied to mold surfaces).</p><p>
</p><p></p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is driven by expanding manufacturing sectors, particularly in automotive and consumer goods, where efficient mold release directly impacts production throughput. Recent developments include Dow Corning's 2023 launch of a high-temperature silicone-based release agent for composite manufacturing. Other key players like Klüber Lubrication and ITW Spraytec are investing in eco-friendly formulations to meet tightening environmental regulations in North America and Europe.</p><p>
