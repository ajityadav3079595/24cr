</p><p>Glucose Monohydrate is the crystallized form of D-glucose, which is a naturally occurring monosaccharide and carbohydrate. This compound is often found in various food and pharmaceutical products due to its versatile nature. Glucose Monohydrate is particularly noted for its sweetness, making it a valuable ingredient in the food industry.</p><p>
</p><p>It functions as a humectant, providing moisture retention, and acts as a fermentation substrate for yeast, an essential process in bread-making and alcohol production. Additionally, Glucose Monohydrate serves as a carrier for other substances in pharmaceutical formulations, enhancing stability and absorption.</p><p>
</p><p>It is typically utilized in various industries, including food and beverages, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics. The main segments include food-grade and medical-grade varieties, both of which serve different functions depending on their purity and intended application.</p><p>
