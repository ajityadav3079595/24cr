These dyes are added to fuel for several reasons, such as for tax or regulatory purposes, identification of fuel types, and to prevent fuel adulteration. The market encompasses both the chemicals used in the dyes (primarily in red, green, and blue variations) and the industries and applications that use these colored fuels. The use of fuel dyes is integral to ensuring proper compliance with laws, traceability, and differentiation between various fuel types. Key sectors involved in this market include refineries, commercial enterprises, and institutional operations.</span></p>

 <p>Historically, the market has seen steady growth, fueled by increasing regulatory frameworks and the rising need for fuel differentiation and monitoring.
