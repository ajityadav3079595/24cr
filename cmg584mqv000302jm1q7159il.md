</p><p>Detergent grade zeolites are microporous crystalline aluminosilicate materials primarily used as water-softening builders in laundry detergents. These synthetic zeolites function through ion exchange, effectively removing calcium and magnesium ions from hard water to improve detergent performance. The most commonly used variant, Zeolite 4A, accounts for over 85% of production due to its optimal pore size and cation exchange capacity.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand for phosphate-free detergents, particularly in developed regions with strict environmental regulations.
