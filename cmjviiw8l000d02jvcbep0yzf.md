</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Higher Material Costs Compared to Conventional Alternatives</strong></p><p>
</p><p>While offering superior performance characteristics, magnesium oxide boards carry a price premium of 15-25% over traditional gypsum and cement boards. Commercial developers frequently opt for conventional materials unless specific building codes or project specifications mandate fire-resistant options. The higher production costs stem from specialized manufacturing processes and quality control requirements for magnesium oxide formulations, limiting price competitiveness in non-regulated applications.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Technical Limitations in High-Moisture Environments</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Despite being water-resistant, magnesium oxide boards can experience performance degradation in continuous wet conditions or high humidity environments. This limitation affects adoption in certain geographic markets and specialized applications like underground construction or coastal developments. Manufacturers are addressing this through advanced coatings and formulation improvements, but market education about proper installation techniques remains an ongoing challenge. In regions with extreme weather conditions, this moisture sensitivity continues to restrain broader market penetration.</p><p>
