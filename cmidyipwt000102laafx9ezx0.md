</p><p><strong>Para-tert-Octylphenol (POTP)</strong>, also referred to as <strong>4-tert-octylphenol</strong>, is a vital chemical compound within the <strong>alkylphenol</strong> category. Structurally, it comprises a benzene ring substituted at the para (1,4) position with a <strong>tert-octyl group</strong> (-C(CH3)3CH2CH2-). The compound is recognized by its molecular formula <strong>C14H22O</strong>. POTP presents as a <strong>white to pale yellow crystalline solid</strong> under standard temperature and pressure conditions. It is <strong>insoluble in water</strong> but dissolves efficiently in a variety of <strong>organic solvents</strong>, which contributes to its versatility across industrial applications.</p><p>
</p><p>POTP is predominantly used as a <strong>precursor in resin production</strong>, specifically <strong>phenolic resins</strong>, and finds utility in the <strong>rubber industry</strong>, <strong>plastics manufacturing</strong>, and as an <strong>intermediate in surfactants and detergents</strong>. Due to its persistent nature in the environment, its use is tightly monitored in certain geographies. However, in many regions, it remains indispensable in <strong>high-performance material applications</strong> due to its chemical stability and unique structural benefits.</p><p>
