</p><p>Proline is one of the twenty standard amino acids used by living organisms for protein synthesis. While it is classified as a non-essential amino acid because the human body can synthesize it, proline plays critical roles in collagen formation, wound healing, and maintaining skin elasticity. The compound exists in two stereoisomeric forms - L-proline (naturally occurring) and D-proline (synthetic) - with L-proline dominating approximately 80% of the market share due to its biological relevance.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by increasing demand from pharmaceutical applications, particularly in wound care and anti-aging formulations.
