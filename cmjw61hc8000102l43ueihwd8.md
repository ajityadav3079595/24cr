</p><p>Thermal greases are thermally conductive compounds used to enhance heat transfer between surfaces in electronic and mechanical systems. These materials fill microscopic air gaps between components and heat sinks, significantly improving thermal conductivity. The product types include silver-based, copper-based, aluminum-based, and silicon-based formulations, each offering distinct thermal conductivity properties ranging from 1-12 W/mK.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from semiconductor and electronics industries, where efficient thermal management is critical for device performance and longevity. The automotive sector's shift toward electric vehicles and advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) has further accelerated demand. Major players like 3M and Dow Corning continue to innovate, with recent developments focusing on non-silicone formulations for high-performance applications.</p><p>
