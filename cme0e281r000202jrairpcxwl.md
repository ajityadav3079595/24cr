</p><p>Antiscorching Agent PVI (CTP), or N-(cyclohexylthio) phthalimide, is a critical rubber additive that prevents premature vulcanization (scorching) during rubber processing. This agent works by delaying the onset of cross-linking in rubber compounds, ensuring optimal processing conditions while maintaining product integrity. It finds extensive applications in both <strong>natural rubber</strong> and <strong>synthetic rubber</strong> manufacturing, particularly in automotive tires, industrial hoses, and footwear production.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is driven by increasing rubber consumption in the automotive and construction industries, particularly in emerging economies. However, regulatory pressures concerning chemical additives and volatile raw material prices pose challenges. The <strong>particles</strong> segment currently dominates the market due to better handling properties, while China remains the largest regional consumer, accounting for over 35% of global demand. Key players such as Eastman Chemical and Dalian Richon Chem are expanding production capacities to meet growing requirements.</p><p>
