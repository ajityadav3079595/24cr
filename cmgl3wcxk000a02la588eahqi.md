</p><p>Methanol Protein, commonly classified as a second-generation single-cell protein (SCP), is produced by utilizing methanol as a carbon source through microbial fermentation. It typically involves the use of specific strains of bacteria or yeast to convert methanol into protein-rich biomass, suitable primarily for animal feed and potentially human consumption. Unlike conventional protein sources, methanol protein production does not rely on agricultural land or climatic conditions, making it a promising alternative in sustainable food and feed systems.</p><p>
</p><p>This innovative biotechnological solution is designed to address the growing global demand for protein while minimizing environmental footprint. Methanol, derived either from fossil fuels or biomass, serves as an economical and widely available substrate. The microorganisms used in the process can grow rapidly and yield protein with a favorable amino acid profile. As such, methanol protein plays an increasingly important role in advancing circular bioeconomy models.</p><p>
