</p><p>High Purity Zinc Antimonide (ZnSb) is a binary compound composed of zinc and antimony, primarily recognized for its semiconductor properties. As a thermoelectric material, ZnSb is utilized to generate electrical energy from temperature gradients, making it valuable for applications in thermoelectric generators (TEGs), infrared detectors, and other optoelectronic devices. High Purity Zinc Antimonide is synthesized to exhibit minimal impurity levels, typically for specialized industrial, research, and defense applications that demand high material purity for optimal performance.</p><p>
</p><p>ZnSb stands out due to its relatively low thermal conductivity and favorable Seebeck coefficient, which are essential for efficient thermoelectric performance. These properties enable it to operate effectively in mid-temperature ranges, bridging the gap between low-temperature materials like bismuth telluride and high-temperature materials like silicon-germanium. Furthermore, its relatively abundant constituent elements compared to rare-earth alternatives contribute to its strategic value.</p><p>
