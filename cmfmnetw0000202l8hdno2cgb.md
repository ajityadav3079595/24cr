</p><p>High Temperature Wear-resistant Steel Plates are specialized steel products engineered to endure extreme thermal conditions and abrasive environments. These steel plates are typically characterized by their high hardness levels (measured in HB or HBW), excellent mechanical properties, and superior resistance to both wear and heat. They are extensively utilized in industries such as cement manufacturing, steel production, power generation, asphalt mixing, and more—where machinery and structural components are subjected to high thermal stress and mechanical wear.</p><p>
</p><p>Common grades of these plates include HBW 400, 400 HB, 450 HB, 500 HB, and above HBW 500. The numbers denote the hardness rating of the material, indicating its resistance level to deformation and abrasion under severe conditions. The higher the HB rating, the more durable the plate against mechanical wear.</p><p>
