As a first-line treatment for tuberculosis and other mycobacterial infections, it works by inhibiting bacterial DNA-dependent RNA polymerase. The API is further processed into various dosage forms including capsules, tablets, and injections. Its effectiveness against gram-positive bacteria and some gram-negative organisms has solidified its position in antimicrobial therapy regimens worldwide.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing tuberculosis cases globally, with WHO reporting 10.6 million new TB cases in 2022. </p><p><strong>Raw Material Volatility</strong></p><p>

</p><p><strong>Stability Considerations</strong></p><p>
Rifampicin's degradation profile requires extensive stability testing and specialized packaging solutions, adding 7-10% to logistics costs. Temperature-controlled storage and transport requirements limit market penetration in developing regions with inadequate cold chain infrastructure.</p><p>
