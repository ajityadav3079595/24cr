</p><p>High Purity Polyaluminum Chloride (PAC) is an advanced, water-soluble inorganic polymer that exists between aluminum chloride (AlCl3) and aluminum hydroxide (Al(OH)3). Commonly referred to by various names including basic aluminum chloride, hydroxy aluminum chloride, and PAC, this compound is characterized by its granular or flake-like appearance, which ranges from white to reddish brown.</p><p>
</p><p>PAC functions primarily as a coagulant due to its high charge density and strong bridging adsorption capacity. During its hydrolysis process, it undergoes a series of physical and chemical transformations such as coagulation, adsorption, and precipitation, which make it an effective solution in water purification, paper manufacturing, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and daily chemical industries.</p><p>
</p><p>Its high purity grade implies low levels of impurities such as iron, heavy metals, and insoluble residues, thereby ensuring minimal secondary contamination in sensitive applications.</p><p>
