</p><p>Polyaluminum Ferric Chloride (PAFC) coagulants are advanced inorganic polymer flocculants that combine aluminum and iron salts. These compounds are widely used in water treatment applications due to their superior coagulation efficiency, rapid sedimentation rates, and reduced sludge production compared to traditional coagulants. PAFC is particularly effective in removing suspended solids, organic matter, and heavy metals from both municipal water supplies and industrial wastewater streams.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing global water treatment demands, stringent environmental regulations, and rapid industrialization in emerging economies. While North America and Europe maintain steady demand due to established infrastructure, Asia-Pacific is emerging as the fastest-growing regional market, accounting for over 40% of global consumption. Recent technological advancements in coagulation chemistry and growing emphasis on sustainable water treatment solutions are further propelling market expansion.</p><p>
