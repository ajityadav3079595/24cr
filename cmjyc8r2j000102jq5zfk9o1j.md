</p><p>N-Acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) is an amino sugar and a derivative of glucose. It is naturally present in the human body and is a fundamental component of various biological structures, including glycoproteins and glycosaminoglycans such as hyaluronic acid and chitin. Industrially, N-Acetylglucosamine is extracted from the hydrolysis of chitin, a polymer found in the exoskeleton of crustaceans like crabs and shrimp. It is widely recognized for its bioactive properties, including anti-inflammatory, skin-repairing, and joint-health-promoting benefits.</p><p>
</p><p>In commercial applications, N-Acetylglucosamine is used in the pharmaceutical, cosmetic, and nutraceutical industries. Its functionality as a skin moisturizer and brightening agent has driven demand in personal care products, while its effectiveness in promoting joint and gut health has made it a valuable ingredient in dietary supplements.</p><p>
