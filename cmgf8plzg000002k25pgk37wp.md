The LTCC silver paste is widely used in multilayer ceramic components, sensors, and high-frequency applications, contributing to its steady market expansion.</p><p>
</p><p>LTCC (Low-Temperature Co-Fired Ceramic) silver paste is a conductive material used in the manufacturing of electronic components. It consists of silver particles dispersed in an organic binder, which allows for screen printing on ceramic substrates. The paste is fired at relatively low temperatures (below 900°C) to form conductive traces, electrodes, and interconnects in multilayer ceramic circuits. LTCC silver paste is preferred for its excellent electrical conductivity, thermal stability, and compatibility with ceramic materials.
