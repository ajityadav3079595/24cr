</p><p>Structural curtain walls are non-load bearing exterior facades designed to resist environmental forces while allowing natural light penetration. These building envelope systems consist of lightweight materials like glass, aluminum, or stone panels supported by aluminum framing. Modern curtain walls incorporate advanced thermal breaks, pressure equalization chambers, and high-performance glazing to meet stringent energy efficiency standards. The technology enables architectural flexibility while providing weather protection and structural stability against wind loads and seismic activity.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing construction of high-rise commercial buildings, rising demand for energy-efficient facades, and urbanization in developing economies. Glass curtain walls dominate the segment due to their aesthetic appeal and daylighting benefits, accounting for over 68% market share in 2024. Recent advancements include smart glass technologies and photovoltaic-integrated facades that contribute to net-zero building initiatives. Major projects like the Jeddah Tower in Saudi Arabia and One Vanderbilt in New York showcase cutting-edge curtain wall engineering.</p><p>
