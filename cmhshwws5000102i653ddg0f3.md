</p><p>Potassium Caseinate is a milk-derived protein formed by the enzymatic treatment of casein with potassium hydroxide. This water-soluble protein finds extensive applications across nutritional products due to its excellent emulsification properties, high protein content (90-95%), and ability to enhance texture and stability in formulations. The product exists in two primary forms - spray-dried and extruded - each offering distinct functional characteristics for food and pharmaceutical applications.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand for protein-fortified foods, rising health consciousness among consumers, and expanding applications in clinical and sports nutrition. While North America currently dominates consumption, Asia-Pacific is emerging as the fastest-growing region due to rising disposable incomes and growing awareness of nutritional supplements. Recent innovations include microencapsulation technologies for improved solubility and bioavailability, with key players like DMV and American Casein Company expanding their production capacities to meet global demand.</p><p>
