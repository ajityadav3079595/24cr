</p><p>Betaine, chemically known as trimethylglycine, is a naturally occurring compound found predominantly in sugar beets and present in a variety of other plants and animals. It exists as a white crystalline powder and is characterized by its zwitterionic nature, containing both positive and negative charges simultaneously. Betaine plays a crucial biological role in methylation reactions and osmoregulation, making it an essential component in animal nutrition, human health, and industrial applications.</p><p>
</p><p>In the commercial landscape, betaine is produced either synthetically or derived from natural sources. Its widespread application spans across animal feed additives, human dietary supplements, personal care and cosmetics, detergents, and pharmaceuticals. In animal feed, betaine is lauded for its ability to provide methyl donors, reduce feed costs by substituting methionine and choline, improve carcass quality, and enhance stress tolerance in livestock and aquaculture.</p><p>
