</p><p>Glutaronitrile (pentanedinitrile) is a versatile nitrile compound appearing as a colorless to light yellow viscous liquid with hygroscopic properties. This specialty chemical serves as a critical intermediate in multiple industries, primarily functioning as a raw material for pesticide formulations and dye production. Furthermore, its emerging application as a performance-enhancing additive in lithium-ion battery electrolytes is gaining traction in the energy storage sector.</p><p>
</p><p>Market expansion is driven by growing agricultural chemical demand and increasing adoption in electrochemical applications. However, stringent environmental regulations on nitrile compounds pose challenges to unchecked growth. The Asia-Pacific region currently dominates production and consumption, with China accounting for over 45% of global capacity. Key manufacturers like Shanghai Jinjinle Industry and Capot Chemical are investing in capacity expansions to meet rising demand, particularly from the battery sector where glutaronitrile improves electrolyte stability and cycle life.</p><p>
