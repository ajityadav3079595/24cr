</p><p>Positive photoresists are light-sensitive materials used in semiconductor photolithography processes to create precise patterns on substrates. These specialized chemicals become soluble when exposed to light, enabling the fabrication of intricate semiconductor lighting components like LEDs and micro-LEDs. The technology plays a critical role in manufacturing high-resolution lighting solutions with superior energy efficiency and miniaturization capabilities.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is being driven by expanding semiconductor fabrication capacities and the global shift toward energy-efficient lighting solutions. <strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Photoresist Material Costs and Supply Chain Vulnerabilities Impact Market Growth</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The positive photoresist market faces significant pressure from rising raw material costs, with key chemical precursors experiencing price volatility of 20-35% annually. </p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Technical Limitations in Extreme Ultraviolet Applications Pose Challenges</strong></p><p>
</p><p>While positive photoresists demonstrate excellent performance in traditional i-line and KrF lithography, their adaptation to emerging extreme ultraviolet (EUV) applications presents technical hurdles. The transition to EUV lithography for advanced micro-LED production requires photoresists with significantly higher sensitivity and resolution capabilities. Current formulations often struggle to achieve the required line-edge roughness below 2nm, creating yield challenges for cutting-edge lighting applications. This limitation has slowed adoption in premium display segments where pixel densities exceed 2000 PPI.</p><p>
