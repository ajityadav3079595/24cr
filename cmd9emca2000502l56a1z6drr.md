</p><p><strong>Chitin</strong> is a naturally occurring, structural polysaccharide composed of modified glucose chains. It is the second most abundant biopolymer after cellulose. Chitin is primarily found in:</p><p>
</p><p>The exoskeletons of insects and crustaceans</p><p>Cell walls of fungi</p><p>Hard structures in invertebrates and fish</p><p>
</p><p>Due to its biodegradable and non-toxic properties, chitin has found extensive use in various industries such as agriculture, pharmaceuticals, food, and biotechnology.</p><p>
