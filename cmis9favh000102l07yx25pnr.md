</p><p>Solid self-lubricating materials are advanced engineering materials designed to reduce friction between moving parts without requiring external lubrication. These materials incorporate solid lubricants like graphite, molybdenum disulfide, or PTFE within their matrix, enabling consistent performance in demanding applications where traditional lubricants fail. The market is segmented into polymer-based, ceramic-based, and metal-based materials, each offering unique advantages for specific industrial requirements.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from automotive and aerospace sectors seeking maintenance-free solutions, coupled with stricter environmental regulations phasing out traditional lubricants.
