</p><p>Friction modifiers are specialized additives designed to reduce friction between moving surfaces in mechanical systems. These chemical compounds form protective boundary layers on metal surfaces, improving fuel efficiency and reducing wear in automotive engines, industrial machinery, and transportation systems. The product segment includes both organic friction modifiers (such as fatty acids and esters) and inorganic friction modifiers (including molybdenum-based compounds).</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing automotive production, stringent fuel efficiency regulations, and growing demand for high-performance lubricants. While Asia-Pacific dominates consumption due to expanding vehicle fleets, North America and Europe maintain strong demand from industrial applications.
