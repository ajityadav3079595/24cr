</p><p>I<strong>ndan (CAS 496-11-7)</strong>, commonly known as indan, is a colorless liquid hydrocarbon used extensively in the chemical and pharmaceutical industries. Chemically represented by the formula <strong>C6H4(CH2)3</strong>, indan is derived primarily from coal tar and typically produced by hydrogenating indene. As a valuable petrochemical intermediate, indan is integral in synthesizing various pharmaceutical and organic compounds. Its distinctive molecular structure contributes to its stability and reactivity, making it a preferred raw material in industrial applications.</p><p>
