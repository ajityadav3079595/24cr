</p><p>Vegetable insulating oils typically command a 15-30% price premium over conventional mineral oils, creating adoption barriers in price-sensitive markets. In developing economies where transformer procurement decisions prioritize upfront costs over lifecycle benefits, this price differential has restricted market penetration to under 15% of total insulating oil sales. While total cost of ownership analyses favor natural esters, the capital-intensive nature of power infrastructure investments continues to favor lower-cost alternatives in many regions.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Supply Chain Vulnerabilities Affect Market Stability</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The vegetable oil market remains susceptible to agricultural commodity fluctuations, with recent geopolitical events causing 18-22% price variations in key feedstock crops.
