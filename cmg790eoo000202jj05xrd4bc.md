<strong>MARKET DRIVERS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Rising Demand in Paper and Textile Industries to Accelerate CHPTAC Adoption</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The paper industry accounts for over 40% of global CHPTAC consumption as the compound is extensively used for starch cationization, which enhances paper strength and printability. With global paper production projected to grow at 2.3% annually through 2030, demand for cationic starch modifiers like CHPTAC continues to rise. In textiles, CHPTAC enables permanent fabric finishing by improving dye receptivity and reducing effluent discharge, meeting tightening environmental regulations worldwide.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Water Treatment Applications Driving Significant Market Growth</strong></p><p>

With over 30% of wastewater globally remaining untreated and tightening discharge standards in regions like Europe and North America, water utilities are increasingly adopting CHPTAC-based solutions.</p><p>

Regional differences in hazardous material regulations further complicate global distribution networks, particularly for Asian manufacturers exporting to Western markets.</p><p>
