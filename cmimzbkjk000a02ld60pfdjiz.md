<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Volatile Crude Oil Prices Impact Production Economics</strong></p><p>
</p><p>As a petroleum derivative, white oil pricing remains directly tied to crude oil market fluctuations. </p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Stringent Regulatory Landscape Increases Compliance Costs</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Food and pharmaceutical applications require white oils meeting NSF, USP, or EP standards, with certification processes adding 15-20% to production costs. Compliance timelines for these regulations average 18-24 months, temporarily restricting market accessibility for some producers.</p><p>
</p><p>Environmental concerns regarding paraffinic hydrocarbons have also led to substitution pressures in certain regions. While white oil remains irreplaceable in many applications, these regulatory hurdles create adoption barriers, particularly for smaller manufacturers serving local markets.</p><p>
