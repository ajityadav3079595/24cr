</p><p>Electrically conductive coatings are specialized surface treatments that provide electrical conductivity while maintaining other functional properties. These coatings typically consist of conductive fillers (such as silver, copper, carbon, or nickel) dispersed in polymer matrices including epoxies, polyurethanes, acrylics, and polyesters. They find extensive applications in EMI/RFI shielding, electrostatic discharge protection, and surface heating elements across industries.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand for consumer electronics with advanced display technologies and the rapid expansion of renewable energy sectors like solar power.
