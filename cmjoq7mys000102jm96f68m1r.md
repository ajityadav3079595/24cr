These adhesives fall into three primary categories: acrylic-based (known for strong bonds to plastics), silicone-based (valued for flexibility and biocompatibility), and other formulations including cyanoacrylates and polyurethanes. Their applications span diagnostic equipment, wearable monitors, and implantable drug delivery systems.</p><p>

</p><p>Medical device adhesives must achieve increasingly difficult performance balances - strong enough for reliable device fixation yet gentle enough for sensitive skin contact.
