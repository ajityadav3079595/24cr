</p><p><strong>Electrotechnical paper is a specialized insulating material primarily designed for use in high-voltage and medium-voltage electrical applications. This paper is manufactured from pure cellulose pulp, derived mainly from wood and cotton, and is processed to remove contaminants and ensure high dielectric strength, mechanical stability, and thermal endurance. Its key function is to serve as an electrical insulator in various electromechanical and electromagnetic systems such as transformers, cables, capacitors, and electric motors.</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>This type of paper exhibits properties such as low conductivity, high resistance to heat and electrical stress, and excellent compatibility with insulating oils. As such, it plays an indispensable role in the safety and efficiency of modern electrical power infrastructure. Over the years, technological advancements in energy distribution and the rising focus on grid modernization have significantly increased the demand for high-performance insulating materials like electrotechnical paper.</strong></p><p>
