</p><p>Aerosol propellants are compressed gases or liquefied gases that enable the dispensing of products in spray form across industries. These propellants create pressure to expel product contents while maintaining formulation stability, playing crucial roles in personal care, household, medical, and industrial applications. Major types include hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), hydrofluoroolefins (HFOs), dimethyl ether (DME), and hydrocarbon propellants.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand for convenience packaging across consumer sectors, particularly in personal care products like deodorants and hairsprays. However, environmental regulations regarding greenhouse gas emissions pose challenges for traditional propellant formulations. Market players are responding with sustainable alternatives - in March 2024, Honeywell announced a new line of low-global-warming-potential HFO propellants for pharmaceutical applications. Other key competitors include Akzo Nobel, Arkema Group, and Chemours Company, who are investing in eco-friendly propellant development to align with global sustainability goals.</p><p>

<strong>GLOBAL AEROSOL PROPELLANTS MARKET TRENDS</strong></p><p>
<strong>Transition Toward Eco-Friendly Propellants Accelerates Market Transformation</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The aerosol propellants industry is undergoing a significant shift driven by stringent environmental regulations and growing consumer demand for sustainable products. Hydrofluorocarbon (HFC) propellants, once dominant, are being phased out in favor of hydrofluoroolefins (HFOs) and dimethyl ether (DME) due to their lower global warming potential. The European Union's F-Gas Regulation has been particularly influential, reducing HFC consumption by over 80% since 2015. This regulatory pressure, combined with corporate sustainability commitments, is reshaping product formulations across personal care, household, and industrial applications.</p><p>
