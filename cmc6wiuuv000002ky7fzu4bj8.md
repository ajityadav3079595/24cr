</p><p>Chrome-plated bars are high-strength, wear-resistant steel bars that are electroplated with a layer of chromium on the surface. This electroplating process enhances their durability, hardness, and corrosion resistance, making them a critical component in a range of precision engineering and industrial applications. The surface hardness typically ranges from 50 to 60 HRC, and the chromium layer possesses a hardness varying from 400 to 1200 HV. These attributes contribute to superior mechanical properties and visual appeal, driving their demand across various sectors.</p><p>
</p><p>The primary purpose of the chromium plating is to improve surface finish and extend the service life of the bar. Due to these benefits, chrome-plated bars are heavily used in applications requiring high performance, such as hydraulic piston rods, linear motion systems, guide rails, automotive components, and precision instruments. The chromium layer also offers exceptional heat resistance, maintaining stability up to 500°C and only beginning to oxidize above that threshold, with a notable drop in hardness occurring beyond 700°C.</p><p>
