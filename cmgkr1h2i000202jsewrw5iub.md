It appears as white granules or crystals and is primarily used as a nitrogen-sulfur fertilizer, accounting for approximately <strong>90% of its applications</strong>. Additionally, it serves as an agricultural spray adjuvant for water-soluble pesticides and in niche industrial processes.</p><p>

<strong>MARKET DRIVERS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Increasing Agricultural Activities to Fuel Ammonium Sulphate Demand</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The global agriculture sector continues to expand rapidly, with fertilizer consumption projected to grow at a steady pace to meet rising food demands. Ammonium sulphate, containing both nitrogen and sulfur - two essential nutrients for plant growth - has seen heightened adoption as a cost-effective fertilizer option. Its effectiveness in alkaline soils and benefits for sulfur-deficient crops make it particularly valuable in key agricultural regions. Emerging economies in Asia-Pacific and Latin America are driving this growth, with fertilizer application rates increasing by approximately 2-3% annually in these regions.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Growth in Food Processing Industry to Accelerate Market Expansion</strong></p><p>

</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Rising Mining Activities to Boost Industrial Applications</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Ammonium sulphate plays a critical role in mining operations, particularly in rare earth element extraction and ore flotation processes.
