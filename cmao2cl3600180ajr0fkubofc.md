</p><p>Benzoguanamine, a derivative of melamine, is a triazine-based compound primarily used in the chemical industry for manufacturing resins, coatings, and plastic additives. With the molecular formula C9H9N5, Benzoguanamine is known for its excellent heat resistance, chemical stability, and crosslinking capabilities. These properties make it especially suitable for applications requiring enhanced durability and thermal performance, such as coatings, paints, fluorescent pigments, molding compounds, and adhesives.</p><p>
</p><p>It is commonly synthesized through the reaction of benzamidine and dicyandiamide, resulting in a white crystalline powder that exhibits high purity levels, typically around 99%. Benzoguanamine is a key intermediate in the production of amino resins, including benzoguanamine-formaldehyde resins, widely used in surface coatings for automotive and furniture industries due to their superior finish and resistance characteristics.</p><p>
