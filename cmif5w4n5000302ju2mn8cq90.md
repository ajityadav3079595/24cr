</p><p>Biomaterial inks are specialized formulations used in 3D bioprinting, composed of biocompatible materials such as hydrogels, polymers, or extracellular matrix components. These inks serve as the foundational medium for fabricating complex biological structures, including tissues, implants, and prosthetics. Key material types include polysaccharides, protein-based bio-inks, synthetic polymers, and other advanced composites, each tailored for specific applications in regenerative medicine and medical device manufacturing.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by rising demand for tissue engineering, increasing adoption of personalized medicine, and advancements in 3D bioprinting technologies. Additionally, collaborations between biotech firms and academic institutions are accelerating innovation.
