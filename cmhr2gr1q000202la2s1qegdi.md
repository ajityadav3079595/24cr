</p><p>Aluminum curtain walls are non-structural exterior building systems designed to provide weather resistance, thermal insulation, and aesthetic appeal. These systems consist of aluminum frames that support various infill materials like glass, metal panels, or thin stone veneers. Modern curtain wall systems are categorized into three main types: stick-built (assembled on-site), semi-unitized (partially prefabricated), and unitized (fully prefabricated off-site).</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is being driven by increasing construction of high-rise commercial buildings, growing emphasis on energy-efficient building envelopes, and rising adoption of sustainable construction materials. While the commercial sector remains the dominant application area, residential adoption is accelerating due to premiumization trends. Key players like Kawneer, Oldcastle BuildingEnvelope, and ALCOA are investing heavily in thermal break technology to meet stringent energy codes, with the latest innovations focusing on pressure-equalized rain screen designs and integrated photovoltaic systems.</p><p>
