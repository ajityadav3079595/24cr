</p><p>Diiodomethane, also known as methylene iodide, is a dense, colorless liquid with the chemical formula CH2I2. It is slightly soluble in water and soluble in organic solvents. Diiodomethane has a variety of applications, including:</p><p>
Determination of density: Due to its high density, Diiodomethane is used to determine the density of mineral and solid samples.</p><p>
Refractive index measurement: It is used as an optical contact liquid to measure the refractive index of certain gemstones.</p><p>
Organic synthesis: Diiodomethane is used as a reagent in organic synthesis for installing the CH2 group.</p><p>
Simmons-Smith reaction: It is used as a source of methylene in the Simmons-Smith reaction.</p><p>
