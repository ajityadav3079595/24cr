</p><p>Despite its excellent properties, aluminum nitride powder faces adoption challenges due to its high production costs. </p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Technical Challenges in Sintering and Machining Hinder Application Scope</strong></p><p>
</p><p>AlN powder processing presents distinct technical challenges that restrain market growth. Achieving dense, pore-free sintered components requires precise control of sintering additives and conditions, as AlN's covalent bonding makes it inherently difficult to densify. The machining of sintered AlN ceramics also proves problematic due to their extreme hardness, necessitating expensive diamond tooling and specialized processes. These technical complexities can deter potential users in cost-sensitive applications, pushing them toward alternative materials despite performance trade-offs.</p><p>
</p><p>Furthermore, the ceramic material's brittleness presents reliability concerns in applications requiring mechanical robustness, limiting its use in environments subject to thermal cycling or vibration stresses.</p><p>
