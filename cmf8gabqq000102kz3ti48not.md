</p><p>The global helium market is experiencing significant growth due to its critical applications in healthcare, particularly in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) systems. Over <strong>90%</strong> of MRI machines rely on liquid helium for cooling superconducting magnets, and with the global MRI market projected to surpass <strong>$9 billion</strong> by 2027, demand for helium continues to rise sharply. Additionally, the semiconductor industry consumes substantial quantities of helium for wafer manufacturing and fiber optics production, with semiconductor applications accounting for approximately <strong>18%</strong> of total helium consumption. As technology advancements drive higher production volumes in these sectors, the need for helium as a cooling and purification agent grows proportionally.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Growth in Aerospace and Leak Detection Applications Fuels Market Expansion</strong></p><p>

Furthermore, leak detection applications across industrial plants and pipelines continue to drive steady demand, given helium's small molecular size and non-reactivity. <strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Supply Chain Vulnerabilities and Geopolitical Factors Limit Market Stability</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The helium market faces significant constraints due to concentrated production and complex supply chains. Currently, over <strong>75%</strong> of global helium supply originates from just three countries - the United States, Qatar, and Algeria - creating vulnerability to geopolitical disruptions. The 2017 Qatar diplomatic crisis demonstrated this fragility, when temporary production halts caused global prices to spike by <strong>135%</strong> within months. Storage and transportation present additional challenges, as liquid helium requires cryogenic containers and loses approximately <strong>1-2%</strong> of volume daily through boil-off, even in optimized conditions.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Other Constraints</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Resource Depletion Concerns</strong></p><p>
