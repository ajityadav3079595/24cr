</p><p>Vapor corrosion inhibitors (VCIs) are chemical compounds that form a protective molecular layer on metal surfaces, preventing oxidation and corrosion in enclosed spaces. These inhibitors sublimate into vapor phase under ambient conditions, creating a protective shield without direct contact with the metal surface. Common types include anode inhibitors, cathode inhibitors, and mixed inhibitors, each with distinct mechanisms of action for different industrial applications.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from industrial and transportation sectors where metal protection is critical. While North America currently dominates the market share, Asia-Pacific is emerging as the fastest-growing region due to rapid industrialization.
