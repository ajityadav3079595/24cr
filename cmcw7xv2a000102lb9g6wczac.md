</p><p>Micronized Polyethylene (PE) Wax refers to ultra-fine powdered polyethylene wax derived from polyethylene through a process of micronization. This wax type features an extremely fine particle size, achieved using advanced equipment like Jet-Micronisers or Microniser/Classifiers. These processes involve high-speed collisions and centrifugal separation that break down coarse polyethylene wax into micron-scale particles. The resulting product offers remarkable properties such as excellent abrasion resistance, chemical stability, cold and heat resistance, and superior lubrication, making it suitable for high-performance applications.</p><p>
</p><p>Micronized PE wax is primarily used as an additive in coatings, inks, adhesives, plastics, and rubber. Due to its ability to enhance scratch resistance, improve slip, and increase gloss retention, it plays a crucial role in industrial formulations. Among its types, oxidized polyethylene wax powder is the most dominant, holding a substantial share of the market.</p><p>
