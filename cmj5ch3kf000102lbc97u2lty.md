Furthermore, the adoption of continuous press technologies has improved energy efficiency, cutting production costs while minimizing waste. These developments, however, require substantial upfront investments, which smaller manufacturers often struggle to afford, leading to consolidation among larger players who can scale these technologies effectively. While the initial transition poses challenges, it positions the industry for long-term resilience against fluctuating raw material prices and evolving consumer preferences for green products. In parallel, digital monitoring systems integrated into production lines are enhancing quality control, ensuring consistent density and strength across batches, which is crucial for applications in high-precision furniture and cabinetry.</p><p>
