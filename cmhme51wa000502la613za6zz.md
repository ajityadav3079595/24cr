</p><p>Hard carbon anodes are non-graphitizable carbon materials used as the anode component in sodium-ion batteries (NIBs). Unlike soft carbon, which can graphitize under high temperatures, hard carbon maintains an amorphous or turbostratic structure, which enhances sodium ion storage through intercalation and pore filling mechanisms.</p><p>
</p><p>These anodes are typically produced from precursors like biomass (e.g., nutshells, wood), synthetic polymers, or pitch. Due to their high specific capacity, structural stability, and wide availability of source materials, hard carbon anodes are considered one of the most promising options for sodium-ion battery technology.</p><p>
</p><p>Sodium-ion batteries, utilizing hard carbon anodes, are an emerging class of rechargeable batteries offering a cost-effective and resource-abundant alternative to lithium-ion counterparts. They are particularly suited for applications such as electric vehicles, stationary energy storage, and backup power systems where lithium supply constraints or price volatility may pose challenges.</p><p>
