</p><p>Foam sandwich panels are composite materials consisting of a lightweight foam core (typically polyurethane, polystyrene, or phenolic) sandwiched between two rigid facings, usually made of metal, fiberglass, or wood. These panels offer superior thermal insulation, structural strength, and fire resistance, making them ideal for applications in <strong>building walls, roofs, and cold storage facilities</strong>. The construction industry accounts for over 65% of global demand, with PUR/PIR panels dominating the market due to their high R-value and durability.</p><p>
</p><p>While North America and Europe remain mature markets with stringent energy efficiency regulations, the Asia-Pacific region is experiencing the fastest growth, fueled by infrastructure development in China and India. Recent innovations include eco-friendly bio-based foam cores and integrated photovoltaic panels, addressing sustainability concerns. However, fluctuating raw material prices and competition from traditional insulation methods pose challenges to market expansion.</p><p>
