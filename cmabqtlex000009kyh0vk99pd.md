</p><p>Graphite electrode billets are intermediate forms of graphite used as electrodes in electric arc furnaces (EAF) and other high-temperature applications. These billets are produced from high-quality graphite scrap that is derived from the graphitization and machining processes of graphite products. They are primarily used in industries that require high thermal conductivity and electrochemical performance.</p><p>
</p><p>These billets serve as essential components in electric arc furnaces used for steel production and are also crucial in electrochemical furnaces used in metallurgical and chemical industries. Graphite's high melting point, electrical conductivity, and resistance to corrosion make it an ideal material for high-temperature environments.</p><p>
</p><p>In steel production, graphite electrodes are used in electric arc furnaces (EAF) for melting scrap steel. The graphite electrodes facilitate the passing of electricity through the furnace, enabling the heat required for melting. As such, graphite electrode billets are vital for the production of steel and other alloys.</p><p>
