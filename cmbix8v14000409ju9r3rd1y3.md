</p><p>N-Propyl Bromide (nPB), with the chemical formula CâHâBr, is a colorless, flammable liquid characterized by a sweet, ether-like odor. It is classified as a brominated hydrocarbon and is primarily used as a solvent in industrial and commercial applications. nPB has gained considerable importance due to its effective degreasing properties and low boiling point, making it suitable for precision cleaning in electronics, aerospace, and metalworking industries. Additionally, it is used as an intermediate in pharmaceutical manufacturing and certain agrochemical syntheses.</p><p>
</p><p>nPB has emerged as a viable alternative to ozone-depleting substances such as trichloroethylene (TCE) and perchloroethylene (PERC). However, despite its environmental benefits over chlorinated solvents, it faces regulatory scrutiny due to its potential health risks, including neurotoxicity and reproductive toxicity. This duality positions nPB at a critical juncture in the global chemicals market, balancing effectiveness with regulatory pressures.</p><p>
