</p><p><strong>D-(+)-Threitol (CAS 2418-52-2)</strong> is a chiral four-carbon sugar alcohol with the molecular formula <strong>C4H10O4</strong>. As a diastereomer of erythritol, it possesses unique stereochemistry that influences its chemical behavior and suitability for specific applications. D-(+)-Threitol is predominantly used as an <strong>intermediate in chemical synthesis</strong>, particularly in pharmaceutical, cosmetic, and specialty chemical industries. The compound’s high purity and controlled stereoisomeric form make it desirable for precise applications, especially where enantiomeric or diastereomeric purity is essential.</p><p>
</p><p>This compound plays a vital role in organic synthesis due to its capability to act as a building block in chiral compound manufacturing. The industry demand is largely influenced by downstream applications in the <strong>cosmetic industry</strong>, the <strong>chemical sector</strong>, and several <strong>niche industrial domains</strong>.</p><p>
