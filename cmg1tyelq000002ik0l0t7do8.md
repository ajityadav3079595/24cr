</p><p>Diisobutylene, a branched alkene with the chemical formula C8H16, is primarily a mixture of isomeric alkenes, notably 2,4,4-trimethylpentene-1 and 2,4,4-trimethylpentene-2. These compounds are derived from isobutene through a dimerization process and are widely used as intermediates in the chemical and petrochemical industries. Diisobutylene serves as a fundamental raw material in the production of high-octane gasoline components such as isooctane and in the manufacture of plasticizers, resins, surfactants, and lubricant additives. Owing to its high purity and favorable chemical properties, diisobutylene is essential for applications that demand stability, high performance, and chemical versatility.</p><p>
</p><p>Its non-polar structure and resistance to oxidation make it ideal for use as a solvent in various industrial applications. Moreover, its role as an intermediate in producing diisobutylene alcohol (used to make isononylol, a crucial plasticizer precursor) underscores its importance in the global chemical supply chain.</p><p>
