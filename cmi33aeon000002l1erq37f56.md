<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>High Production Costs Limiting Mass Market Adoption</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Despite their superior properties, ultralight materials often carry premium pricing that restricts broader adoption. Silica aerogel production currently costs 3-5 times more than conventional insulation materials, while carbon fiber prices remain 10-15 times higher than steel. The energy-intensive supercritical drying process required for aerogels contributes significantly to these costs, accounting for approximately 60% of total production expenses. While prices are gradually declining - aerogel costs have fallen 30% since 2020 - they remain prohibitive for price-sensitive industries and developing markets.</p><p>

</p><p>While ultralight materials excel in specific properties, limitations in other areas constrain their use cases. These performance compromises require careful engineering solutions, often negating some weight-saving benefits. In the automotive sector, concerns about crash performance have limited structural adoption to non-safety-critical components in most vehicle platforms.</p><p>

Regulatory pressure is mounting, with the EU considering classification of certain aerogel formulations under REACH regulations due to potential nanoparticle release.
