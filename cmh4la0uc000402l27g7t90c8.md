</p><p>Needle punched and spunlace nonwovens are engineered fabrics manufactured through mechanical or hydraulic entanglement of fibers without weaving or knitting. These versatile materials find extensive applications across industries due to their superior absorbency, strength, and filtration properties. The two primary types - <strong>spunlace nonwovens</strong> (using high-pressure water jets) and <strong>needle punched nonwovens</strong> (using barbed needles) - cater to diverse end-use requirements from hygiene products to industrial applications.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is driven by increasing demand in healthcare for surgical drapes and wound dressings, coupled with expanding applications in automotive interiors and geotextiles. However, sustainability concerns regarding synthetic fiber disposal present challenges.
