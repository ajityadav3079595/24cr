</p><p>Potassium Tetraborate Tetrahydrate (CAS 12045-78-2) is a white crystalline inorganic compound with the molecular formula B4H8K2O11. It serves as an important boron source with higher solubility than sodium borates at room temperature, making it preferable for applications where sodium content must be minimized. The compound finds extensive use as a buffering agent, welding flux component, lubricant additive, and disinfectant due to its stable pH properties in aqueous solutions.</p><p>

<strong>MARKET DRIVERS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Expanding Application in Flame Retardants to Propel Market Growth</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Global potassium tetraborate tetrahydrate market is witnessing significant growth due to its increasing adoption in flame retardant applications. With stringent fire safety regulations being implemented across construction, automotive, and electronics industries, the demand for effective and environmentally friendly flame retardants has surged. Potassium tetraborate tetrahydrate serves as an excellent alternative to traditional halogen-based additives, offering superior thermal stability while being non-toxic. The compound's ability to release water molecules at high temperatures makes it particularly effective in suppressing flames without emitting harmful gases.</p><p>
</p><p>As environmental concerns drive the shift toward green chemistry solutions, industries are actively seeking safer alternatives. </p><p>Specialty glass production, including laboratory glassware and LCD displays, particularly benefits from potassium tetraborate tetrahydrate's properties.
