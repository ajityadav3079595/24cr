</p><p>Organosilicon polymer, commonly known as polysiloxane, is a class of synthetic polymer consisting of a backbone of alternating silicon and oxygen atoms, often combined with organic side groups such as methyl, phenyl, or vinyl. These materials are prized for their <strong>versatility</strong>, offering properties like high thermal stability, chemical resistance, and flexibility. Key product categories include <strong>silicone oil, silicone rubber, silicone resin, and silicone emulsion</strong>, each serving distinct applications across multiple industries. For example, silicone oil is used as a lubricant and antifoaming agent, while silicone rubber finds application in sealants and gaskets.
