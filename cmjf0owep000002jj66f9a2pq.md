</p><p>Ammonium sulfate is an inorganic chemical compound with the formula (NH4)2SO4. It contains 21% nitrogen in the form of ammonium ions and 24% sulfur as sulfate ions. The purified material takes the form of white granules or crystals. It is commonly used as a fertilizer and as an agricultural spray adjuvant for water-soluble pesticides. It is also used in the preparation of other ammonium salts.</p><p>
</p><p>The market is experiencing steady growth due to several factors, including increased demand for high-efficiency fertilizers, rising agricultural productivity requirements, and the compound's effectiveness in sulfur-deficient soils. Additionally, its use as a flocculant in water treatment and in certain industrial processes contributes to market expansion. The Asia-Pacific region dominates the market due to large agricultural sectors, with China and India being major consumers. Europe and North America follow, with significant usage in specialty crops and turf management.</p><p>
</p><p>For instance, according to the International Fertilizer Association, global ammonium sulfate consumption reached 29.5 million metric tons in 2022. The International Fertilizer Industry Association reports that Asia accounted for 58% of global consumption, while North America and Europe together accounted for 28%.
