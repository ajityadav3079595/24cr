</p><p>Heavy hydrogen, also known as deuterium, is a stable hydrogen isotope containing one proton and one neutron in its nucleus. This atomic structure makes it twice as heavy as protium (ordinary hydrogen), giving it unique properties valuable across scientific and industrial applications. Deuterium finds key uses in nuclear reactors as a moderator, in NMR spectroscopy, and as a tracer in chemical and biological research.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is primarily driven by increasing demand from the semiconductor industry, where deuterium is used in plasma etching processes. The rising adoption of OLED displays in consumer electronics further propels demand, as deuterium-based organic materials enhance device longevity. Key manufacturers including Linde Gas and Cambridge Isotope Laboratories are investing in production capacity to meet this growing demand.</p><p>

The unique properties of deuterium in heavy water make it indispensable for certain reactor designs, particularly CANDU reactors, creating sustained demand in existing and emerging nuclear markets.</p><p>
