</p><p>High level disinfectants are chemical agents capable of killing all microorganisms, including all forms of vegetative bacteria, mycobacteria, fungi, and viruses, with the exception of large numbers of bacterial spores. These antimicrobial agents are primarily used for reprocessing medical instruments and devices that come into contact with mucous membranes or non-intact skin. The most common applications include disinfection of endoscopes, surgical instruments, and other medical devices that cannot withstand the extreme heat of sterilization processes. High level disinfection differs from sterilization in that it may not kill bacterial spores but achieves a higher level of antimicrobial activity than low or intermediate level disinfectants.</p><p>
