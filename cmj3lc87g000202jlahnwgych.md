</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Rising Mental Health Disorders Globally to Fuel Chlorpromazine Demand</strong></p><p>

Chlorpromazine remains a first-line treatment for psychotic disorders, particularly in developing nations where cost-effectiveness is prioritized. The growing recognition of mental health as a global health priority is driving increased diagnosis rates and treatment adoption. Emerging markets in Asia-Pacific and Latin America are experiencing particularly strong growth due to expanding healthcare infrastructure and awareness campaigns.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Cost-Effectiveness of Generic Chlorpromazine Stimulates Market Access</strong></p><p>

</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Side Effect Profile Limits Adoption in Developed Markets</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Chlorpromazine's established side effects, including extrapyramidal symptoms and potential metabolic disturbances, are significantly restraining market growth in advanced healthcare systems. Clinical guidelines in North America and Western Europe increasingly favor newer antipsychotics with improved safety profiles. Recent treatment protocol updates in these regions show chlorpromazine prescriptions declining by approximately 15-20% annually since 2020. The reluctance among prescribers to use first-generation antipsychotics, except in treatment-resistant cases, continues to challenge market expansion in high-income countries.</p><p>
