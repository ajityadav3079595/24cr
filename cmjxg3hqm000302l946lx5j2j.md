</p><p>N-Methyldiethanolamine (N-MDEA) is a tertiary amine produced through the ethoxylation of methylamine and ethylene oxide. This versatile chemical compound serves as a critical component in gas treatment processes, particularly in the removal of hydrogen sulfide and carbon dioxide from natural gas streams during refining. Its selective absorption properties and lower energy requirements make it preferable to other amines in acid gas removal applications.</p><p>
