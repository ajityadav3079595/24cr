</p><p><strong>Aminopropanediol (APD)</strong>, also known as 3-Amino-1,2-Propanediol, is a clear liquid with the molecular formula C3H9NO2. This specialty chemical serves as a pivotal intermediate in the production of non-ionic contrast agents like iohexol and ioversol, which are widely used in medical imaging procedures. Furthermore, APD finds applications as a precursor in pesticide synthesis, highlighting its dual industrial significance in pharmaceuticals and agriculture. Production involves reacting 3-chloro-1,2-propanediol (CPD) with ammonia in an alkaline sodium hydroxide environment, followed by purification.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by increasing global demand for diagnostic imaging procedures utilizing non-ionic contrast media, particularly in developed healthcare systems and emerging economies.
