</p><p>Dirithromycin is a semi-synthetic macrolide antibiotic derived from erythromycin, primarily used to treat respiratory tract infections caused by susceptible organisms. As a prodrug, it converts to its active form erythromycylamine in vivo, demonstrating broad-spectrum activity against gram-positive bacteria and some gram-negative pathogens. The compound shows particular efficacy against Streptococcus pneumoniae and Haemophilus influenzae strains.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is being driven by rising respiratory disease prevalence across Southeast Asia's aging population and improving healthcare infrastructure. However, the emergence of antibiotic-resistant strains presents ongoing challenges. </p><p>The Southeast Asia Dirithromycin market is experiencing significant growth due to the increasing burden of respiratory infections across the region.
