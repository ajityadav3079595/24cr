</p><p>Anhydrous hydrofluoric acid (AHF) is a highly reactive inorganic compound widely used in industrial applications. This colorless liquid or gas is produced through the reaction of fluorite (calcium fluoride) with sulfuric acid. As a crucial chemical intermediate, it finds applications in fluorocarbon production, aluminum smelting, uranium processing, and electronics manufacturing. The market primarily segments product grades into ≥99.90% purity and standard commercial grades.</p><p>
