</p><p><strong>Flame-retardant fiber refers to specially engineered fiber that, when exposed to open flame, smolders without igniting and ceases combustion upon removal from the flame. These fibers are designed to minimize the risk of fire-related hazards, particularly in high-risk environments. In the automotive industry, such fibers are used extensively in vehicle interiors, including seat covers, carpets, insulation panels, and headliners, ensuring passenger safety and compliance with stringent fire safety regulations.</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Automobile fireproof fibers are primarily composed of inherently flame-retardant materials such as aramids, polybenzimidazoles (PBI), and modacrylics. These materials are known for their exceptional thermal stability, chemical resistance, and mechanical strength. Their applications extend beyond private vehicles to include buses, military vehicles, race cars, and public transport systems.</strong></p><p>
