</p><p>Boscalid Technical is a systemic fungicide belonging to the pyrazole-carboxamide class, primarily used to control a wide spectrum of fungal diseases in crops. Its mode of action involves inhibiting succinate dehydrogenase (SDHI) in fungal mitochondria, disrupting energy production. The technical grade material is typically formulated into end-use products like suspension concentrates (SC) and water-dispersible granules (WG) before agricultural application. Major crop applications include cereals, fruits, vegetables, and oilseeds, with particularly strong efficacy against pathogens causing powdery mildew, botrytis, and sclerotinia.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by several factors, including rising global food demand, increasing fungal resistance to conventional fungicides, and the compound's favorable environmental profile compared to older chemistries. However, regulatory scrutiny on pesticide residues and the development of alternative SDHI fungicides present challenges. BASF, Bayer, and Syngenta collectively hold over 60% of the global market share, with recent capacity expansions in China and India significantly influencing regional supply dynamics.</p><p>
