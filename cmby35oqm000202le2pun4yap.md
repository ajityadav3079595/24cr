<strong style="font-size:13px">Allylbenzene (CAS 300-57-2)</strong>, chemically known as <strong style="font-size:13px">prop-2-enylbenzene</strong> or <strong style="font-size:13px">1-Benzylethene</strong>, is a <strong style="font-size:13px">colorless, aromatic liquid</strong> widely used in industrial and research applications.</p><p>
</p><p>Belonging to the <strong>Aromatic Compounds</strong> family, Allylbenzene is soluble in <strong>alcohol</strong>, <strong>ether</strong>, and <strong>benzene</strong>, but remains <strong>insoluble in water</strong>.</p><p>
It is a <strong>stable compound</strong> under normal conditions but reacts adversely with <strong>strong oxidizing agents</strong>.</p><p>
Industrially, Allylbenzene is critical for <strong>organic synthesis</strong>, serving as a precursor in the production of various <strong>specialty chemicals</strong>, <strong>polymers</strong>, <strong>pharmaceuticals</strong>, and <strong>fragrances</strong>.</p><p>
</p><p>Its versatility and chemical stability have made it a highly sought-after compound in various industrial applications where <strong>aromatic intermediates</strong> are essential.</p><p>
