<p>The global zero friction coatings market was valued at <strong>US$ 763 million in 2024</strong> and is projected to reach <strong>US$ 1.3 billion by 2032</strong>, exhibiting a <strong>Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 0.069% </strong>during the forecast period (2025–2032). The market is expected to grow steadily due to increasing demand from industries such as automotive, aerospace, and healthcare, where friction reduction is critical for operational efficiency and longevity of components.</p><p>
</p><p>Zero friction coatings are specialized surface treatments designed to minimize friction between moving parts, thereby reducing wear and energy consumption. These coatings are typically applied to components in high-performance industries where friction can lead to inefficiencies or mechanical failures. The primary types of zero friction coatings include <strong>PTFE-based coatings</strong> and <strong>MoS2-based coatings</strong>. PTFE (Polytetrafluoroethylene) coatings are known for their non-stick and low-friction properties, while MoS2 (Molybdenum Disulfide) coatings are valued for their high load-bearing capacity and durability under extreme conditions.</p><div><b>Download FREE Sample of this Report @
