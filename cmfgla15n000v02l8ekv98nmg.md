</p><p>Polyethylene wax (PE wax) is a low molecular weight polymer derived from ethylene, a monomer of polyethylene. It appears as a white, odorless solid and is widely used across several industries due to its excellent properties such as high melting point, low viscosity, hardness, thermal stability, and chemical resistance. The versatility of PE wax makes it an essential additive in applications ranging from plastics processing to rubber manufacturing, adhesives, inks, and coatings. It functions as a dispersing agent, lubricant, matting agent, and surface modifier.</p><p>
</p><p>Polyethylene wax is primarily available in two types: low-density polyethylene (LDPE) wax and high-density polyethylene (HDPE) wax. It is produced through various methods including polymerization of ethylene, thermal degradation of high molecular weight polyethylene, and by-product recovery during the production of polyethylene.</p><p>
</p><p>PE wax is crucial in improving process efficiency and end-product characteristics. For instance, in plastics manufacturing, it enhances mold release and flow properties. In inks and coatings, it boosts abrasion resistance and surface gloss.</p><p>
