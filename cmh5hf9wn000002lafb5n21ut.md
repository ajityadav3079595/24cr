</p><p>Cocamidopropyl betaine (CAPB) is an amphoteric surfactant derived from coconut oil, widely used as a foaming and thickening agent in personal care formulations. This mild, biodegradable surfactant finds extensive application in shampoos, body washes, liquid soaps, and other cosmetic products due to its excellent compatibility with skin and hair. The compound's unique zwitterionic structure provides both cleansing and conditioning properties, making it particularly valuable in sulfate-free formulations.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by increasing demand for mild and organic personal care products, particularly in developing regions. However, fluctuating raw material prices and regulatory scrutiny concerning potential skin sensitization remain key challenges. Major players are investing in sustainable production methods to capitalize on the growing preference for bio-based ingredients. Recent product innovations include ultra-mild CAPB variants for baby care products and specialized formulations for sensitive skin applications.</p><p>

</p><p>The global Cocamidopropyl Betaine (CAPB) market is witnessing robust growth primarily due to increasing consumer preference for mild and bio-based surfactants in personal care products. The personal care industry accounts for over 65% of CAPB consumption globally, with shampoos and body washes being the largest application segments.
