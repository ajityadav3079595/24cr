</p><p>D-Glucuronic Acid is a uronic acid derivative formed through the oxidation of the C-6 hydroxyl group of glucose into a carboxylic acid group. It predominantly exists in a more stable lactone form, specifically 3,6-lactone of the furan ring, due to instability in its free acid form. This compound plays an essential role in human and animal metabolism by facilitating the detoxification of xenobiotics and metabolic waste through glucuronidation processes in the liver.</p><p>
</p><p>Biochemically, D-Glucuronic Acid forms conjugates with toxins, drugs, hormones, and bile acids to render them more water-soluble for excretion. It is found in several polysaccharides, including glycosaminoglycans such as heparin, chondroitin sulfate, and hyaluronic acid, making it an integral component in connective tissue biology and pharmaceutical development.</p><p>
