It is composed of fatty acids, esters, and hydrocarbons, with its color ranging from white (when purified) to yellow or brown due to the presence of pollen and propolis. This versatile material finds applications across industries, including food, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals, owing to its emulsifying, protective, and moisture-retention properties.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by rising demand in cosmetic formulations, particularly in natural and organic personal care products, as well as increased adoption in pharmaceutical coatings and candle manufacturing.
