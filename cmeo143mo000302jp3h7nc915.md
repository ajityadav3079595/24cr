The <strong>Automatic Die Bonding System</strong> refers to sophisticated equipment and automated processes used to attach semiconductor chips (dies) onto substrates during the semiconductor manufacturing process. These systems are primarily designed for high-precision, high-volume production, ensuring that each die is perfectly aligned with the substrate. The bonding process is crucial in semiconductor packaging, which is integral for the functionality, reliability, and thermal management of electronic devices.</p><p>
</p><p>Die bonding systems are equipped with various technologies such as <strong>pick-and-place</strong>, <strong>pressure application</strong>, and <strong>UV curing</strong> to secure the chips on substrates like <strong>lead frames</strong>, <strong>ceramic substrates</strong>, or <strong>printed circuit boards (PCBs)</strong>. This bonding process is critical for ensuring the overall performance of devices like <strong>diodes</strong>, <strong>MOSFETs</strong>, <strong>power modules</strong>, and other semiconductor components.</p><p>
