</p><p>Ortho-dichlorobenzene (ODCB) is a chlorinated aromatic hydrocarbon with the molecular formula C6H4Cl2, where two chlorine atoms are substituted at adjacent positions on a benzene ring. This chemical compound is primarily produced through the catalytic chlorination of benzene or as a byproduct in chlorobenzene production processes. The compound exists as a colorless liquid with a characteristic aromatic odor and is insoluble in water.</p><p>
</p><p>Key applications of ortho-dichlorobenzene include its use as an intermediate in pharmaceutical manufacturing, dye production, pesticide formulation, and as a solvent for toluene diisocyanate (TDI). The Asia-Pacific region dominates the global market, accounting for approximately 72% of total consumption, followed by Europe (12%) and North America (9%). China represents the largest production base with 44% of global output, while India and Japan contribute 27% and 13% respectively. The market is characterized by the presence of major players such as Jiangsu Yangnong Group, Kureha Corporation, and Aarti Industries, which collectively hold about 58% market share.</p><p>
