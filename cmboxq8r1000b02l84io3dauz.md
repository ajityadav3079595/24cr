<p>The global polyol for polyurethane market was valued at <strong>US$9.87 billion in 2024</strong> and is projected to reach <strong>US$15.3 billion by 2032</strong>, growing at a <strong>compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 5.6%</strong> during the forecast period (2025–2032). The market is driven by increasing demand for polyurethane in various end-use industries, including construction, automotive, and furniture. </p><p>Polyols are key raw materials used in the production of polyurethane, a versatile polymer widely used in foam, coatings, adhesives, and sealants. Polyols for polyurethane are categorized into <strong>polyether polyols</strong> and <strong>polyester polyols</strong>, each offering distinct properties suitable for different applications. Polyether polyols are commonly used in flexible foams, while polyester polyols are preferred for rigid foams and coatings. End User Industry Analysis</p><p>

</p><p>The <strong>automotive industry</strong> is another major consumer, with polyurethane used in seating, dashboards, and lightweight components. The shift toward electric vehicles (EVs) is expected to further boost demand.</p><p>
</p><p>The <strong>furniture industry</strong> relies on polyurethane for flexible foam in mattresses and upholstery. Rising disposable income and urbanization are driving demand in this sector.</p><p>
