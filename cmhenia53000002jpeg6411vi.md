</p><p>Sodium starch octenyl succinate (SSOS) is a modified starch derivative produced through esterification of starch with octenyl succinic anhydride. This white powder serves as an emulsifier, stabilizer, and encapsulation agent in food and pharmaceutical applications. The product's unique amphiphilic structure enables it to stabilize oil-in-water emulsions while providing excellent film-forming properties.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is primarily driven by increasing demand for functional food ingredients and the expanding processed food industry. The Asia-Pacific region dominates consumption, accounting for 45% of global demand, with China being both the largest producer and consumer. Food grade SSOS represents 92% of total production, with applications spanning dairy products, beverages, and nutritional supplements. The pharmaceutical sector is emerging as a significant growth segment, utilizing SSOS for drug encapsulation and controlled release formulations.</p><p>
</p><p>Key manufacturers are investing in production capacity expansion to meet growing demand. In 2023, Guangzhou Fofiber Biological commissioned a new 20,000-ton facility, while RuiLin Chemical announced plans for a 15,000-ton expansion in 2025. The market remains concentrated, with the top five producers controlling 65% of global capacity. Regulatory approvals from FDA, EFSA, and other agencies continue to support market expansion into new applications.</p><p>
