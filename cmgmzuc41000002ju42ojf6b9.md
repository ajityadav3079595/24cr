</p><p>Amorphous iron (Fe Amorphous) refers to a unique metallic alloy with a non-crystalline atomic structure, offering superior magnetic properties compared to traditional crystalline materials. These alloys primarily consist of iron combined with small amounts of silicon, boron, and other elements, forming thin foils or ribbons through rapid solidification techniques. The material's key characteristics include high magnetic permeability, low core loss, and excellent saturation induction, making it ideal for energy-efficient electrical applications.</p><p>
