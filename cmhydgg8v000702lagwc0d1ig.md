</p><p>6-phytase is a specialized enzyme that catalyzes the hydrolysis of phytic acid, an anti-nutritional compound prevalent in plant-based animal feed. This process enhances phosphorus bioavailability for monogastric livestock while reducing environmental pollution from phosphorus excretion. The enzyme finds primary application in poultry, swine, and aquaculture feed formulations, though emerging uses in food processing and pharmaceuticals are gaining traction.</p><p>
</p><p>Market expansion is driven by tightening environmental regulations on phosphorus emissions, particularly in North America and Europe, where sustainable farming practices are prioritized. With over 70 countries implementing some form of phosphorus management policies, feed producers are increasingly incorporating phytase to comply with environmental standards. The growing focus on sustainable agriculture and circular economy principles further accelerates market penetration of these enzymes across all livestock sectors.</p><p>
