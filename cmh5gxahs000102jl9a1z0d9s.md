</p><p>The specialized production process for acicular pitch coke presents significant barriers to market expansion. Creating premium needle coke requires precise temperature control during delayed coking and subsequent calcination, with production cycles taking weeks to complete. The capital intensity of these operations means new facilities require investments exceeding $500 million, deterring smaller players. Furthermore, yield rates for high-quality needle coke typically range between 55-65% of total output, creating inherent production inefficiencies.</p><p>

</p><p><strong>Feedstock Volatility</strong></p><p>
