</p><p>Anticrease agents are chemical formulations applied to textiles during manufacturing to prevent wrinkling and maintain fabric smoothness. These specialized treatments work by cross-linking cellulose fibers or coating fabric surfaces, creating durable press effects while retaining garment quality. The two primary types are dye-bath lubricants (applied during coloration processes) and wet processing lubricants (used in finishing stages).</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by expanding textile production in emerging economies, particularly in Asia Pacific where countries like India and China account for over 65% of global cotton production.
