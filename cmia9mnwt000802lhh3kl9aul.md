</p><p>While RO membranes excel in seawater desalination, treating brackish water presents unique technical hurdles. Many standard membrane configurations struggle with the "salinity sweet spot", becoming either inefficient at lower concentrations or prone to scaling at higher ones. This complexity adds 15-20% to system design costs and extends project timelines, discouraging adoption in regions where brackish water represents the primary source.</p><p>
</p><p>Furthermore, the lack of standardized testing protocols for brackish water applications makes performance comparisons between membrane brands difficult.
