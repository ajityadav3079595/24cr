</p><p>Thiourethane lens monomers are organic compounds primarily used as the foundational materials for the production of polythiourethane lenses. These monomers consist predominantly of reactive chemical structures such as isocyanate groups (-NCO) and thiol groups (-SH), which undergo polymerization reactions to form lenses with unique optical properties. Polythiourethane lenses, manufactured using these monomers, are renowned for their high impact resistance, excellent optical clarity, and UV protection capabilities. The combination of sulfur, nitrogen, and carbonyl functional groups in the monomers provides the necessary chemical attributes to support high refractive index formulations, especially valuable in premium eyeglass lenses, safety goggles, and other optical devices.</p><p>
</p><p>The application of thiourethane lens monomers is primarily in the ophthalmic industry, where they are tailored to produce lenses with specific indices of refraction such as 1.60, 1.67, or 1.74. These variants cater to different consumer needs regarding lens thickness, weight, and optical performance. As demand for lightweight, durable, and optically advanced lenses continues to rise, thiourethane lens monomers have become increasingly integral in modern lens manufacturing.</p><p>
