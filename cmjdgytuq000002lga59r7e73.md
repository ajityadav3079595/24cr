</p><p>Long chain dibasic acids are organic compounds containing two carboxyl functional groups separated by an extended hydrocarbon chain. These specialty chemicals, including DC11, DC12, DC13 and DC14 variants, serve as critical building blocks for high-performance polymers, lubricants and corrosion inhibitors. Their unique molecular structure enables superior thermal stability and chemical resistance compared to shorter chain alternatives.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from the nylon production sector, where these acids enhance polymer durability. While the powder coatings industry represents another key application area, adoption faces challenges from fluctuating raw material costs. Recent capacity expansions by leading producers like Cathay Industrial Biotech and Evonik indicate strong industry confidence, with China emerging as both a major producer and consumer due to its thriving manufacturing sector.</p><p>
