</p><p>Abrasion Resistant Steel Plate refers to high-carbon alloy steel specifically engineered to offer enhanced resistance to abrasion and wear. These steel plates are primarily used in applications where high mechanical stress, friction, and wear are routine, such as mining, construction, agriculture, and heavy-duty manufacturing. The abrasion resistance of these steel plates is usually measured in terms of Brinell Hardness Number (BHN), with common grades including AR400, AR450, AR500, and AR600. Their unique properties stem from alloying elements like carbon, chromium, and manganese, which contribute to their exceptional hardness and durability.</p><p>
</p><p>The steel is generally manufactured using quenching and tempering processes that increase its toughness while maintaining workability. This balance between hardness and toughness makes abrasion resistant steel plates ideal for environments where extended service life and reduced maintenance are critical, such as bulldozer blades, truck beds, crushers, and screen plates.</p><p>
