<p>The global retinoic acid market was valued at <strong>US$ 187 million in 2024</strong> and is projected to reach <strong>US$ 312 million by 2032</strong>, exhibiting a <strong>Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 6.6%</strong> during the forecast period (2025–2032). </p><p>Retinoic acid, a derivative of vitamin A, is a biologically active compound widely recognized for its role in cell growth, differentiation, and immune function. It is primarily used in medical treatments, particularly for dermatological conditions such as acne and psoriasis, as well as in anti-aging skincare products. The compound exists in various forms, including powder, liquid, and crystal, each catering to specific industrial and pharmaceutical needs. Retinoic acid is also utilized in chemical synthesis and laboratory experiments, making it a versatile component across multiple sectors.
