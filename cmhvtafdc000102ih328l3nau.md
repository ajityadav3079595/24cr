</p><p>Betanin, commonly known as beetroot red, is a natural glycosidic food dye extracted primarily from beets. As a betalain pigment, it belongs to a class of compounds including isobetanin, probetanin, and neobetanin. The pigment's aglycone form, betanidin, is obtained through glucose hydrolysis. Recognized as food additive E162, betanin serves as a vibrant red-purple coloring agent with antioxidant properties that make it increasingly popular in clean-label food products.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by rising consumer demand for natural food colors as synthetic alternatives face regulatory scrutiny.
