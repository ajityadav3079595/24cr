<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Higher Production Costs Compared to Synthetic Alternatives to Limit Market Penetration</strong></p><p>
</p><p>While decyl glucoside offers numerous benefits, its higher production costs compared to conventional petroleum-based surfactants remain a significant market restraint. </p><p><strong>Other Restraints</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Performance Limitations in Certain Applications</strong></p><p>
Decyl glucoside exhibits lower foaming capacity and cleansing power compared to traditional surfactants, limiting its adoption in products requiring rich lather. While suitable for mild formulations, this characteristic restricts usage in heavy-duty cleaning applications where powerful detergency is required.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Supply Chain Vulnerabilities for Raw Materials</strong></p><p>
Dependence on agricultural feedstocks makes decyl glucoside susceptible to price fluctuations caused by adverse weather conditions and geopolitical factors affecting crop yields. Recent disruptions in the global supply of coconut oil, a key raw material, have highlighted these vulnerabilities and created challenges for consistent production.</p><p>

</p><p>Decyl glucoside faces increasing competition from newer bio-based surfactants gaining traction in the personal care industry.
