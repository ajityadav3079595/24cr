</p><p>Electrolyte additives are specialized chemical compounds added to the electrolyte solution of batteries, particularly lithium-ion batteries, to enhance performance, stability, and safety. These additives play a critical role in forming a stable solid electrolyte interphase (SEI) layer, preventing dendrite formation, improving thermal stability, increasing cycle life, and enhancing conductivity. The most commonly used electrolyte additives include Vinylene Carbonate (VC), Fluoroethylene Carbonate (FEC), lithium bis(oxalato)borate (LiBOB), and others. These compounds are essential for applications in electric vehicles (EVs), energy storage systems (ESS), consumer electronics, and industrial equipment.</p><p>
