</p><p>The chemical complexity of modern fertilizer blends creates significant technical challenges for dye manufacturers. This variation leads to compatibility issues where dyes may degrade, fade, or react adversely with active ingredients. Field tests reveal that approximately 15% of colored fertilizer products experience some degree of color instability during storage or application, potentially compromising their effectiveness as visual markers.</p><p>
</p><p>Temperature sensitivity poses another persistent challenge, particularly in tropical climates where high heat can accelerate dye decomposition. Manufacturers are investing heavily in stabilizing additives and advanced encapsulation technologies, but these solutions typically add 10-15% to production costs, creating a persistent barrier to widespread adoption.</p><p>

</p><p>The rapid digital transformation of agriculture presents significant growth opportunities for fertilizer dye manufacturers. </p><p>Furthermore, the development of multifunctional dyes with secondary properties such as UV protection or moisture resistance could open new market segments.
