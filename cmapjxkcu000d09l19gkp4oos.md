</p><p><strong>Slab Formwork</strong> refers to a temporary mold or support system used in the construction industry to hold and shape wet concrete until it gains adequate strength and hardens into a self-supporting structure. Commonly used in the construction of floors, ceilings, bridges, and rooftops, slab formwork ensures accurate alignment and quality finish of horizontal concrete slabs. It is composed of various components such as panels (plywood or metal), beams, props (steel or aluminum), and connectors. Slab formwork systems are broadly categorized into traditional timber formwork and modern modular systems. Traditional systems are labor-intensive and manually assembled, whereas modern systems utilize lightweight, reusable, and adjustable components, offering higher efficiency and cost savings. The design and implementation of slab formwork are critical for safety, structural stability, and achieving desired architectural outcomes.</p><p>
