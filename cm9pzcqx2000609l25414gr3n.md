</p><p><strong>Ferro Silicon is a ferroalloy composed primarily of iron and silicon, with silicon content typically ranging from 15% to 90%. It is produced by reducing silica or sand with coke in the presence of iron, often in electric arc furnaces. The resulting material is an essential deoxidizer and alloying agent used in the steel and iron industries. Due to its efficiency in removing oxygen from molten metals and enhancing the strength and quality of steel, Ferro Silicon has become a crucial additive in various metallurgical applications.</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>In addition to its primary use in steel manufacturing, Ferro Silicon is also used in the production of cast iron, where it improves the final product's strength and wear resistance. It serves as a source of silicon in the production of magnesium ferrosilicon alloys used in the nodulization of ductile iron. Moreover, in the semiconductor industry, high-purity silicon derived from Ferro Silicon is instrumental in fabricating electronic devices.</strong></p><p>
