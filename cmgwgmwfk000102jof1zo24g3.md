</p><p>HTCC slurry refers to a specialized ceramic material used in the production of High-Temperature Co-Fired Ceramic substrates. These slurries are formulated with materials like tungsten, molybdenum, or manganese to withstand extreme temperatures (typically above 1,600°C) during sintering. Key applications include semiconductor packaging, multilayer circuit boards, and sensors in harsh environments. The slurry acts as a conductive or insulating layer, enabling miniaturization and enhanced performance in electronic components.
