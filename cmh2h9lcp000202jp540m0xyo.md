</p><p><strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Flame-retardant fiber refers to a type of fiber that does not generate a flame when exposed to fire. Instead, it smolders and extinguishes itself once removed from the flame source. These fibers are widely used in commercial vehicles to enhance fire safety and meet stringent regulatory standards. The flame-retardant properties of these fibers make them crucial for applications in vehicle interiors, upholstery, and insulation materials, reducing the risk of fire-related incidents.</p><p>
