<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>High Production Costs and Complex Manufacturing Processes Limit Market Penetration</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Advanced composite materials typically cost 5-10 times more than conventional alternatives, creating significant adoption barriers outside aerospace and defense sectors. While economies of scale may reduce costs over time, current pricing structures restrict broader industrial adoption across more price-sensitive applications.</p><p>

</p><p>Stringent certification requirements in aerospace, defense, and energy sectors create lengthy approval processes that can delay market entry by 12-24 months. Some jurisdictions are implementing extended producer responsibility policies that may impose additional costs on composite manufacturers.
