</p><p>Gum Base is a non-nutritive, masticatory component used as the core ingredient in chewing and bubble gum formulations. It is an inert and insoluble material that supports the edible and soluble components like sweeteners, polyols, glucose, and flavors. The term "Gum Base" is internationally standardized and recognized by the Food Chemicals Codex and various national food safety legislations. Essentially, it forms the chewable texture that remains after the flavors and sugars dissolve during mastication.</p><p>
</p><p>Gum Base can be synthetic or natural. Synthetic bases are made from food-grade polymers, resins, and waxes, while natural gum bases derive from substances like chicle or jelutong. The versatility and formulation flexibility of gum base make it an essential element in the development of various gum types, including sugar-free, functional, and novelty gums.</p><p>
