</p><p><strong>Food Grade Tetrapotassium Pyrophosphate (TKPP) is a white crystalline or granular powder primarily used as a food additive. Classified under food-grade phosphates, TKPP serves various roles in the food industry, notably as a sequestrant, emulsifier, and buffering agent. It functions by binding metal ions, stabilizing emulsions, and maintaining pH levels in processed foods. TKPP is widely employed in processed meat, seafood, bakery products, dairy items, and beverages. Due to its solubility and stability, it is a preferred choice for maintaining food quality and extending shelf life. Regulatory bodies like the FDA and EFSA recognize food-grade TKPP as safe for consumption within prescribed limits.</strong></p><p>
