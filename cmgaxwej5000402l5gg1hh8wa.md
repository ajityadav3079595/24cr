</p><p>Geopolitical tensions and supply chain vulnerabilities have prompted major pharmaceutical companies to reevaluate their API sourcing strategies. <strong>Dual sourcing and regional supply networks</strong> are becoming standard practice, with manufacturers establishing API production facilities closer to end markets. The International Council for Harmonisation's Q3D guidelines have led to stricter elemental impurity controls, with lead and nickel limits being reduced by up to 80% for certain parenteral formulations.
