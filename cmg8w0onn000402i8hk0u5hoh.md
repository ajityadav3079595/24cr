</p><p>Airlaid nonwoven fabrics are specialized materials produced through an air-forming process that disperses fibers in an airstream before depositing them onto a conveyor belt. These high-performance fabrics combine superior absorption with softness and strength, making them ideal for hygiene products, medical supplies, and industrial wipes. The three primary bonding technologies used include latex-bonded, thermal-bonded, and multi-bonded types, each offering distinct performance characteristics.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand in feminine hygiene products and adult incontinence solutions, particularly in aging populations across developed markets. </p><p>While demand grows, the industry faces significant headwinds from pulp price fluctuations and energy cost instability. </p><p><strong>Production Capacity Limitations</strong></p><p>
The capital-intensive nature of airlaid production creates substantial barriers to rapid capacity expansion. A single 30,000-ton production line requires $80-$100 million investment and 24-30 months for commissioning.
