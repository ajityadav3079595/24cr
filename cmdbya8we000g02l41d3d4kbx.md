End User Industry Analysis</p><p>
</p><p>The oilfield drilling sector accounts for approximately <strong>35%</strong> of global diutan gum consumption, utilizing its superior rheological properties in drilling fluids. The product enhances wellbore stability and fluid loss control, particularly in high-temperature and high-pressure drilling environments.</p><p>
</p><p>In the <strong>food industry</strong>, which represents about <strong>25%</strong> of market share, diutan gum serves as a stabilizer in dairy products, sauces, and gluten-free baked goods. Its ability to maintain texture under varying pH conditions makes it particularly valuable in processed foods.</p><p>
</p><p>The <strong>construction industry</strong> contributes <strong>20%</strong> of demand, where diutan gum improves the workability and water retention of cement-based materials. Its use in self-leveling compounds and tile adhesives has grown significantly in recent years.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Agricultural applications</strong> account for <strong>10%</strong> of consumption, primarily in pesticide formulations and soil stabilization. The mining industry utilizes another <strong>5%</strong> for mineral processing and tailings management. The remaining <strong>5%</strong> is distributed across various specialty applications including personal care products and pharmaceuticals.</p><p>
