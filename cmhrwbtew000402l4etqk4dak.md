</p><p>Redistribution layer (RDL) materials are critical components in advanced semiconductor packaging, enabling the interconnection of chips through fine-line wiring. These materials include polyimides, polybenzoxazoles (PBO), and benzocyclobutenes (BCB), which provide excellent thermal stability, mechanical strength, and dielectric properties. RDL materials facilitate high-density interconnects in fan-out wafer-level packaging (FOWLP) and 2.5D/3D IC packaging solutions.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand for compact electronic devices and the proliferation of 5G and IoT technologies. While polyimide currently dominates the material segment due to its cost-effectiveness, PBO is gaining traction for high-performance applications. Furthermore, leading semiconductor manufacturers are investing heavily in advanced packaging technologies, with recent developments including Samsung's 2024 expansion of its chip packaging facilities to meet growing demand for AI processors.</p><p>
