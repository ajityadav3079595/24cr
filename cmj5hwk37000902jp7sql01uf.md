</p><p>HFC-32 (Difluoromethane) is a hydrofluorocarbon refrigerant widely used as a replacement for ozone-depleting substances in air conditioning and refrigeration systems. As a pure component refrigerant with zero ozone depletion potential (ODP), it offers better energy efficiency and lower global warming potential (GWP) compared to traditional refrigerants like R-410A. While still considered a transitional solution in the refrigeration industry's shift toward more environmentally friendly alternatives, HFC-32 remains critical for meeting current cooling demands across residential, commercial, and industrial applications.</p><p>

</p><p>The global shift toward energy-efficient cooling technologies is significantly driving the HFC-32 refrigerant market. Its thermodynamic properties enable smaller system designs while maintaining cooling capacity, making it increasingly preferred by manufacturers. This regulatory landscape is compelling HVAC OEMs to redesign systems specifically for HFC-32 compatibility.</p><p>
