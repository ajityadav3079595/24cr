</p><p>Gum Acacia, also known as gum arabic, is a natural gum derived from the hardened sap of various species of the Acacia tree, predominantly Acacia senegal and Acacia seyal. This gum is a complex mixture of glycoproteins and polysaccharides, primarily composed of arabinose and galactose. Gum Acacia is a highly soluble dietary fiber and a natural emulsifier used widely in food and beverage industries as a stabilizer in soft drinks, a thickener in confectionery, and a binder in bakery products.</p><p>
</p><p>Beyond food, it is also utilized in pharmaceuticals as a demulcent and excipient, in cosmetics for its emulsifying and film-forming properties, and in industrial applications including printing, ceramics, and textiles. Its natural origin, non-toxicity, and functional versatility make it a preferred additive in formulations seeking organic or plant-derived labeling.</p><p>
