</p><p><strong>P-Nitroaniline (PNA)</strong>, also known as <strong>4-Nitroaniline</strong>, <strong>para-nitroaniline</strong>, or <strong>1-amino-4-nitrobenzene</strong>, is an organic compound with the molecular formula <strong>C6H6N2O2</strong>. It appears as a yellow to orange crystalline solid and is known for its role as a key intermediate in the synthesis of various industrial chemicals. PNA is primarily used to manufacture <strong>p-Phenylenediamine (PPD)</strong>, which is essential in the production of dyes, particularly hair dyes and textile dyes. Additionally, it finds applications in the pharmaceutical, agricultural, and rubber industries, where it acts as a precursor to several drugs, antioxidants, and pesticides.</p><p>
</p><p>Commercially, P-Nitroaniline is produced through the <strong>amination of 4-nitrochlorobenzene</strong>, a process that is widely adopted for its efficiency and cost-effectiveness. Due to its toxic nature and environmental impact, the handling and transportation of PNA are regulated under chemical safety guidelines. Despite this, its widespread applications in multiple end-use industries continue to drive its demand globally.</p><p>
