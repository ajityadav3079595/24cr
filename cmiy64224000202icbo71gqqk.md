</p><p>Nanofibrillar cellulose, often referred to as cellulose nanofibers (CNF) or microfibrillated cellulose (MFC), represents a class of nano-structured cellulose derived primarily from wood pulp or other plant-based sources through mechanical fibrillation processes. These nanoscale fibers, typically measuring 5-20 nanometers in width and several micrometers in length, offer remarkable properties including high tensile strength, flexibility, and biodegradability. As a sustainable alternative to synthetic materials, nanofibrillar cellulose supports applications in composites, packaging, and biomedical fields by enhancing mechanical performance while maintaining environmental compatibility.</p><p>
