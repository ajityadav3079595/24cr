# Refractory High-Entropy Alloys Market, Global Outlook and Forecast 2025-2032

<p>Refractory High-Entropy Alloys (RHEAs) are a category of advanced alloys known for their exceptional high-temperature stability, making them ideal for demanding applications in harsh environments. These alloys are composed of multiple elements, typically five or more, that have high melting points and contribute to the alloy's overall high-temperature resistance and strength. RHEAs combine the unique properties of high-entropy alloys (HEAs)—such as excellent mechanical strength, resistance to oxidation, and resistance to wear—with the inherent high-melting-point characteristics of their constituent metals.</p><p>
</p><p>The primary elements typically used in these alloys include refractory metals such as tungsten, molybdenum, niobium, tantalum, and chromium, all known for their high melting points.
