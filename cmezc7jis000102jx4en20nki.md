</p><p>Succinoglycan is an <strong>exopolysaccharide</strong> produced through microbial fermentation, primarily by soil bacteria like Rhizobium and Agrobacterium. This biopolymer exhibits exceptional rheological properties, including high viscosity at low concentrations, pseudoplastic behavior, and stability across wide pH and temperature ranges. These characteristics make it valuable as a <strong>thickener, stabilizer, and emulsifier</strong> in industrial applications. The market offers three primary grades: <strong>food-grade</strong> (for edible products), <strong>pharmaceutical-grade</strong> (for drug formulations), and <strong>cosmetics-grade</strong> (for personal care products).</p><p>
</p><p>While the market shows promising growth, it faces challenges from synthetic alternatives and regulatory hurdles in food applications.
