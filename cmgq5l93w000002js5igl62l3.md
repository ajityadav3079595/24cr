</p><p>Hot-dip Al-Zn steel, also known as Galvalume, is a corrosion-resistant steel product with an aluminum-zinc alloy coating. This specialized coating composition (typically 55% aluminum, 43.4% zinc, and 1.6% silicon by weight) provides superior protection against corrosion and heat resistance compared to traditional galvanized steel. The material finds extensive applications in roofing, automotive components, HVAC systems, and industrial equipment due to its durability and cost-effectiveness.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is primarily driven by increasing infrastructure development across emerging economies and the material's expanding adoption in automotive lightweighting strategies. However, volatility in aluminum and zinc prices presents ongoing challenges for manufacturers. Recent innovations in coating technologies and sustainability initiatives by major producers like ArcelorMittal and Nippon Steel are creating new opportunities in the renewable energy and electric vehicle sectors.</p><p>
