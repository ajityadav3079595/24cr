</p><p><strong>High Purity Silver Selenide (Ag2Se)</strong> is a semiconductor compound composed of silver and selenium. It is characterized by its high degree of purity, often exceeding <strong>99.99%</strong>, and its distinctive black solid form with a metallic luster. The material has a molecular weight of <strong>328.8 g/mol</strong> and a hexagonal crystal structure, making it suitable for use in high-performance applications.</p><p>
</p><p>Silver selenide is primarily used in thermoelectric devices, infrared detection and imaging systems, and solar cells due to its <strong>low band gap</strong> (approximately <strong>0.1 eV</strong>) and <strong>high Seebeck coefficient</strong> of <strong>300 ÂµV/K</strong>. The compound is valued for its unique electronic and thermal properties, which make it a key material in the development of energy-efficient technologies.</p><p>
