</p><p>Petroleum ether, also known as benzine or ligroin, is a light hydrocarbon mixture derived from petroleum. It is a volatile, colorless liquid composed mainly of alkanes and cycloalkanes and is characterized by a narrow boiling point range. Petroleum ether is primarily used as a non-polar solvent in laboratories, pharmaceutical formulations, industrial processes, and chemical synthesis. Despite its name, petroleum ether is not a true ether but a petroleum-derived solvent with excellent solvency properties, making it ideal for dissolving fats, oils, and waxes. Its low reactivity and high volatility make it suitable for a variety of extraction and purification applications.</p><p>
