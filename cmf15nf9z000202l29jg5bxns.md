</p><p>Silane crosslinking agents are chemical compounds containing two or more silicon functional groups that act as molecular bridges between polymers. These agents facilitate the formation of covalent or ionic bonds between polymer chains, creating three-dimensional network structures essential for enhancing material properties like thermal stability and mechanical strength. Key product types include <strong>deacidified</strong>, <strong>dealcoholized</strong>, and <strong>deketoxime</strong> variants, widely used in rubber products, construction materials, automotive components, and medical applications.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is driven by increasing demand from the construction and automotive industries, where these agents improve material durability. While Asia-Pacific dominates consumption due to rapid industrialization, North America and Europe maintain strong demand for high-performance applications.
