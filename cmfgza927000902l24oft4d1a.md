</p><p>Glass Filled Polyetheretherketone (PEEK) is a type of high-performance engineering thermoplastic modified by the addition of glass fiber. Typically, glass fiber content ranges from 10% to 30% by weight, providing enhanced mechanical properties such as increased rigidity, dimensional stability, and resistance to wear and high temperatures.</p><p>
</p><p>PEEK, by itself, is known for its excellent thermal and chemical resistance, low moisture absorption, and superior creep resistance. When reinforced with glass fibers, its usability extends to more aggressive and structurally demanding environments. This makes Glass Filled PEEK highly suitable for precision components in industries like aerospace, automotive, electronics, and medical, where reliability under extreme conditions is critical.</p><p>
</p><p>Due to its intrinsic properties, Glass Filled PEEK is frequently used in the production of gears, bearings, impellers, structural brackets, electrical insulation components, and seal rings. It is particularly effective in environments requiring high load-bearing capacity, prolonged exposure to elevated temperatures (up to 260°C), and exposure to chemicals or radiation.</p><p>
