<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Higher Production Costs Compared to Regular Fabrics to Limit Market Penetration</strong></p><p>
</p><p>While slub yarn fabrics command premium positioning in the market, their specialized manufacturing process presents cost challenges that can restrict broader adoption. The irregular texture that defines slub fabrics requires careful monitoring during spinning, with production speeds typically 15-20% slower than conventional yarn manufacturing. This productivity gap translates to approximately 30% higher baseline production costs, which must either be absorbed by manufacturers or passed through the supply chain. In price-sensitive emerging markets, these cost differentials can make slub yarn products inaccessible to many consumers, particularly in basic apparel categories where price competition is intense.</p><p>
</p><p>Additional complications arise in downstream processing, where the inherent irregularities of slub yarn fabrics can increase fabric wastage during cutting by as much as 12-15% compared to uniform textiles. These factors collectively create economic barriers that may deter some brands from fully embracing slub yarn fabrics across their product ranges, particularly in mass-market segments where margins are already compressed.</p><p>

Industry surveys reveal that nearly 60% of brands cite inconsistent quality as their primary concern when sourcing slub yarn products from multiple suppliers.
