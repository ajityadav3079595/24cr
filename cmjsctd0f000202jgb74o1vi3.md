<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Volatile Raw Material Prices Create Supply Chain Uncertainties</strong></p><p>
</p><p>DL-Methionine production relies heavily on petrochemical derivatives, particularly acrolein and methyl mercaptan, whose prices have shown 15-20% annual fluctuations in recent years. These input cost variations create pricing instability in the feed additive market, forcing manufacturers to implement frequent price adjustments. Feed producers consequently face challenges in long-term budgeting and feed formulation consistency, potentially discouraging optimal DL-Methionine incorporation levels.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Stringent Regulatory Landscapes Slow Product Approvals</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Global regulatory frameworks for feed additives continue to tighten, with approval processes for new DL-Methionine formulations now averaging 24-36 months across major markets. The European Union's stringent REACH regulations and recent updates to the U.S. FDA's feed additive approval guidelines have increased documentation requirements significantly. While ensuring product safety, these regulatory hurdles delay market introduction of innovative formulations and strain manufacturer resources with complex compliance procedures.</p><p>
