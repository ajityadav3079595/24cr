</p><p>Crystalline hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) is an advanced ceramic material known for its exceptional thermal conductivity, electrical insulation, and lubricating properties. Often referred to as "white graphite," this synthetic compound features a layered structure similar to graphite but with superior thermal stability and chemical inertness. Its applications span across high-performance ceramics, lubricants, thermal management solutions, and cosmetics.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from the electronics sector for thermal interface materials, particularly in 5G infrastructure and electric vehicle batteries. While automotive applications are expanding rapidly, the aerospace industry presents new opportunities due to h-BN's lightweight properties. </p><p>While h-BN offers exceptional material properties, its commercial adoption faces significant hurdles due to high production costs.
