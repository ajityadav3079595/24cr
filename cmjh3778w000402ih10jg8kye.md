</p><p>PE-RT (Polyethylene of Raised Temperature resistance) pipes are engineered thermoplastic solutions designed for high-temperature applications in plumbing and heating systems. Unlike conventional polyethylene pipes, PE-RT maintains structural integrity at elevated temperatures due to its unique molecular architecture, eliminating the need for chemical cross-linking. These pipes have become the material of choice for underfloor heating systems and domestic water distribution, owing to their flexibility, durability, and corrosion resistance.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by increasing urbanization and infrastructure development, particularly in emerging economies. While residential applications dominate demand, commercial adoption is accelerating due to stricter building codes mandating energy-efficient solutions. Recent developments include Wavin's 2024 launch of next-generation multilayer PE-RT pipes with improved pressure resistance, reflecting the industry's focus on performance enhancement.</p><p>
