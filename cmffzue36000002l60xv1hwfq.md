</p><p>While insulated sandwich panels offer long-term savings, their initial cost remains 20-30% higher than conventional roofing materials. Manufacturers are working to reduce costs through material innovations and production efficiencies, but the price gap remains a challenge for widespread market penetration.</p><p>

</p><p>Proper installation of insulated sandwich panels requires specialized skills and equipment, creating challenges in regions with limited technical expertise. The roofing industry faces a shortage of trained installers, particularly in emerging markets where sandwich panel technology is relatively new. This skills gap has slowed adoption in some regions, as contractors hesitate to take on projects requiring unfamiliar techniques. Training programs and certification initiatives are helping address this issue, but the learning curve continues to impact market expansion in certain geographic areas.</p><p>

Manufacturers are responding with new fire-resistant formulations, but the perception of flammability continues to influence purchasing decisions in some market segments, particularly for high-risk applications like food processing facilities and chemical plants.</p><p>
