</p><p>Isostatically pressed graphite is an ultra-fine grain material produced through equal pressure application from all sides during forming, resulting in isotropic properties. This manufacturing technique enhances mechanical strength and thermal stability compared to conventional graphite, making it ideal for high-performance applications. The material's uniform density and microstructure give it superior properties in thermal management, electrical conductivity, and chemical resistance.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is driven by expanding demand from the photovoltaic and semiconductor industries, where graphite's thermal and electrical properties are critical.
