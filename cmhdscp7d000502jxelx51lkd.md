</p><p>Polyacrylic acid (PAA) scale inhibitors are water-soluble polymers widely used to prevent mineral scale formation in industrial water systems. These inhibitors function by dispersing or sequestering scaling ions such as calcium and magnesium, making them essential for applications like industrial cooling systems, oilfield water injection, and air conditioning. PAA-based inhibitors include acrylic acid homopolymers, acrylic acid/maleic acid copolymers, acrylic acid/sulfonic acid copolymers, and other specialized formulations.</p><p>
</p><p>The market is growing steadily due to rising demand from industries such as power generation, petrochemicals, and manufacturing, where efficient water treatment is critical. Acrylic acid homopolymer dominates the market with a 77% share, owing to its cost-effectiveness and performance efficiency. Meanwhile, North America leads geographically with a 50% market share, followed by Asia-Pacific and Europe. Key players like BASF, DuPont, and Arkema are investing in sustainable formulations to comply with stricter environmental regulations, further driving adoption.</p><p>
