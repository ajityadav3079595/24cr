</p><p><strong>Gypsum-Fiber Board</strong>, also known as paperless drywall or fiber-gypsum board, is a building material composed primarily of gypsum (hemihydrate), fibers (such as wood or glass fibers), and additives mixed with water. Unlike traditional drywall, which uses paper as the outer layer, gypsum-fiber board incorporates fibers that provide superior strength and water resistance. This board is not only used in the same applications as paper drywall, including walls and ceilings but is also increasingly being utilized in furniture making as a sustainable alternative to wood.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>The key properties of gypsum-fiber board include:</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Moisture resistance</strong>: Gypsum-fiber boards are highly resistant to moisture and humidity, making them ideal for use in areas prone to damp conditions.</p><p><strong>Strength</strong>: The addition of fibers enhances the board's structural integrity, allowing it to be used in more demanding applications.</p><p><strong>Sustainability</strong>: The materials used in gypsum-fiber boards are often more eco-friendly than traditional drywall, especially when using recycled fibers.</p><p><strong>Fire resistance</strong>: Like regular gypsum board, gypsum-fiber boards exhibit excellent fire resistance, which adds to their appeal in fire-prone environments.</p><p>
