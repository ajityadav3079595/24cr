</p><p>Anionic dispersants are surface-active substances or non-surface active polymers used to improve particle separation in suspensions, primarily colloids. These chemicals prevent settling or clumping by creating stable dispersions. Characterized by their non-polar, negatively charged hydrocarbon chains and polar hydrophilic groups, they form asymmetric molecular structures that enhance compatibility across applications. Common types include sulfonates (R-SO3Na), sulfates (R-O-SO3Na), and carboxylates, with sulfonates dominating nearly 85% of the product segment.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from paints &amp; coatings (the largest application segment), followed by textile and pulp &amp; paper industries. Regional analysis shows North America and Europe collectively hold 50% market share, while China accounts for approximately 15%. Key players like BASF, Clariant, and DowDuPont are expanding production capacities to meet rising demand, particularly for water-based formulations that align with stringent environmental regulations.</p><p>
