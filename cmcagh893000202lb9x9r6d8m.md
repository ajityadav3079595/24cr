</p><p>Thiocarbamide, also known as thiourea, is an organosulfur compound with the molecular formula CH4N2S. It features a thiocarbonyl (-CS) functional group instead of the carbonyl (-CO) group found in urea. This compound serves as a versatile chemical intermediate with applications spanning pharmaceuticals, agricultural chemicals, and industrial processes. Its unique properties make it valuable in diverse sectors, including electronics for PCB manufacturing and medicine for thyroid disorder treatments.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from end-use industries, particularly in Asia-Pacific where China dominates production.
