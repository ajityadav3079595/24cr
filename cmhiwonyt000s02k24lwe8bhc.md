</p><p>Heat-resistant fabrics are specialized textiles designed to withstand high temperatures while maintaining structural integrity. These fabrics incorporate advanced materials such as aramid fibers, ceramic coatings, and silica-based weaves that provide thermal insulation and flame retardancy. Common applications include protective clothing for industrial workers, firefighting gear, military uniforms, and aerospace components where exposure to extreme heat is a constant risk.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by stringent workplace safety regulations across manufacturing sectors and increasing adoption in energy generation facilities. While traditional materials like asbestos are being phased out due to health concerns, innovations in polymer blends and nanotechnology-enhanced textiles are gaining traction.
