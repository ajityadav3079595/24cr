</p><p>AG Glass, also known as Anti-Glare Glass, is a specialized type of glass treated to reduce glare and reflection on its surface. Unlike regular transparent glass, AG glass undergoes a surface modification process to transform its naturally reflective surface into a matte or rough texture that scatters light. This treatment minimizes visual disturbances caused by light reflection, thereby improving visibility and readability under various lighting conditions. AG glass is widely used in applications where clarity and visibility are crucial, including display panels, instrument covers, electronic screens, solar panels, architectural glazing, and museum displays. The most common techniques used in the production of AG glass include chemical etching, AG coating, and AG spraying. Each method is chosen based on the required durability, visual effect, and cost-efficiency for its intended application.</p><p>
