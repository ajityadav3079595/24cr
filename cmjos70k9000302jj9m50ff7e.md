</p><p>Zinc Laurate is a metal soap compound primarily used as a lubricant and stabilizer in various industrial applications. This white, waxy powder serves as an effective additive in plastics, paints, and personal care products due to its thermal stabilizing properties and water-repellent characteristics. The compound is formed by the reaction of lauric acid with zinc oxide, resulting in a versatile material that improves product performance across multiple industries.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from the plastics industry, where Zinc Laurate acts as a lubricant and release agent during polymer processing. Furthermore, rising consumption in cosmetic formulations as a texture enhancer and opacifying agent contributes to market expansion.
