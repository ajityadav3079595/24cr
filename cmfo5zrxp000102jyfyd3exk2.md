</p><p>Motor lamination materials are specialized electrical steel sheets or alloys used in the construction of motor cores to minimize energy loss through eddy currents. These materials include <strong>steel (non-grain oriented and grain-oriented), nickel alloys, and cobalt alloys</strong>, each offering unique magnetic properties essential for efficient motor performance. The lamination process helps reduce hysteresis loss while improving thermal conductivity in electric motors, transformers, and generators.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is driven by increasing demand from the power infrastructure and industrial automation sectors, where high-efficiency motors are becoming standard. However, price volatility in raw materials like silicon steel presents a key challenge.
