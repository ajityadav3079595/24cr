</p><p>High Strength Steel (Yield Strength ≥700MPa) refers to alloy steels engineered to exhibit superior mechanical properties such as higher tensile strength and enhanced corrosion resistance compared to traditional carbon steels. These steels are categorized into conventional High-Strength Steels and Advanced High-Strength Steels (AHSS).</p><p>
</p><p>Conventional high-strength steels include High Strength Low Alloy Steels, Carbon Manganese Steels, and Bake Hardenable Steels. These are commonly used in applications where cost-effectiveness and decent strength are necessary.</p><p>
</p><p>Advanced High-Strength Steels, on the other hand, are manufactured through complex processes involving precise chemical compositions and controlled thermal treatments, producing multi-phase microstructures that enhance their mechanical properties. These steels are particularly valued in applications requiring excellent formability, crash performance, and fatigue resistance.</p><p>
