</p><p>Caustic paint and coating strippers are chemical formulations containing sodium hydroxide (lye) that dissolve or soften paint layers by breaking polymer bonds. These products come in various forms including gels, liquids, and aerosols, with applications ranging from industrial equipment maintenance to DIY home renovation projects. The strippers work by penetrating paint layers and swelling the coating matrix through saponification reactions.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing infrastructure refurbishment activities and stricter environmental regulations promoting safer paint removal alternatives. While traditional methylene chloride-based strippers face phase-outs in many regions, caustic formulations gain traction as a balance between effectiveness and lower toxicity. Recent product innovations focus on reduced alkalinity formulations and biodegradable additives to meet evolving environmental standards. Key players like 3M and PPG Industries are investing in R&amp;D to develop next-generation stripping solutions that maintain performance while improving workplace safety.</p><p>
