</p><p><strong>Carbon Nanotube Thermal Interface Material (CNT-TIM)</strong> refers to a specialized class of materials used between two surfaces (such as a microprocessor and a heat sink) to enhance thermal transfer and reduce heat buildup. Traditional TIMs, such as thermal pastes or pads, face challenges like low thermal conductivity, aging, and pump-out effects. CNT-TIM addresses these issues by utilizing carbon nanotubes (CNTs) — nanostructures known for <strong>exceptionally high thermal conductivity</strong> (up to 3,000 W/mK), <strong>excellent mechanical flexibility</strong>, and <strong>resistance to thermal stress</strong>.</p><p>
</p><p>In semiconductor packaging and module design, CNT-TIMs are vital for managing heat dissipation, thereby ensuring device reliability and performance. Two primary forms dominate the market: <strong>Single-Walled Carbon Nanotubes (SWNTs)</strong> and <strong>Multi-Walled Carbon Nanotubes (MWNTs)</strong>, both offering distinct advantages for various industrial, automotive, and consumer electronics applications.</p><p>
