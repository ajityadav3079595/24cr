These implants dominate the market due to titanium's exceptional properties including high strength-to-weight ratio, corrosion resistance, and ability to form direct bone attachments without fibrous tissue formation. The product range primarily includes endosteal (root-form) and subperiosteal implants, with endosteal variants accounting for over 80% market share.</p><p>

</p><p>The global burden of dental diseases continues to rise, with over 2.3 billion people suffering from caries of permanent teeth and more than 530 million children affected by primary tooth caries. </p><p>Despite growing demand, the premium pricing of titanium implant systems continues to hinder broader adoption. New ceramic composites demonstrate comparable success rates to titanium for single-tooth replacements, though limitations remain for full-arch reconstructions. The growing preference for metal-free solutions in aesthetic dentistry is prompting some clinicians to reconsider material choices, particularly for front-tooth applications where zirconia's tooth-like appearance provides compelling advantages.</p><p>
