# Reserpine Market, Global Outlook and Forecast 2025-2032

<p>Reserpine is a naturally occurring alkaloid compound primarily sourced from the roots of plants like Rauwolfia serpentina, also known as Indian Snakeroot. Historically, it has been used for its medicinal properties, most notably its ability to lower blood pressure and induce calming effects. Reserpine works by inhibiting the release of norepinephrine and dopamine, neurotransmitters that play a role in regulating blood pressure and mood. As a result, it has been employed in the treatment of hypertension, schizophrenia, and other psychiatric disorders.</p><p>
</p><p>Despite its initial success as a treatment, the side effects of reserpine—such as sedation, depression, and gastrointestinal issues—have limited its usage in modern medicine. Today, it is rarely prescribed in favor of more effective and safer alternatives. However, reserpine is still relevant in specific applications, particularly in research settings and certain niche therapeutic areas.
