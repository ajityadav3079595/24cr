<strong>Drivers</strong></p><p>

	</p><p><strong>Increasing Demand for Lightweight Materials</strong></p><p>
	The automotive industry's shift toward lightweight materials to improve fuel efficiency and reduce emissions is a major growth driver. DLFTs, with their high strength-to-weight ratio, are ideal for automotive components where weight reduction is critical.</p><p><strong>Growing Focus on Performance and Durability</strong></p><p>
	DLFTs offer enhanced mechanical properties such as impact resistance and fatigue strength. These performance advantages are fueling demand, particularly in applications that require high durability under stress.</p><p><strong>Technological Advancements in Manufacturing</strong></p><p>
	Innovations in processing technologies—such as advanced compounding and mold design—have streamlined the production of DLFT components. These advancements have made it easier and more cost-effective for industries to adopt DLFT solutions.</p><p>
