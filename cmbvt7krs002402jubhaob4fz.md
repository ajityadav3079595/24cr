</p><p>High purity calcium carbonate refers to a refined form of calcium carbonate (CaCO3) with minimal impurities, typically exceeding <strong>98% purity</strong>. It is produced through either precipitation or mechanical grinding of natural limestone. The product exists in two primary forms: <strong>uncoated</strong> and <strong>coated precipitated calcium carbonate</strong>, each tailored for specific industrial applications. High purity calcium carbonate is valued for its brightness, low abrasiveness, and chemical stability, making it indispensable in industries requiring high-performance fillers or additives.</p><p>
</p><p>Key products within this market include pharmaceutical-grade calcium carbonate, food-safe variants, and specialty grades for plastics and paints. Its role as a cost-effective functional filler enhances product durability and optical properties, aligning with sustainability trends. The market’s expansion is further supported by innovations in surface modification technologies, enabling tailored solutions for niche applications.</p><p>
