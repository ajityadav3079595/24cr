</p><p>Phosphorus pentasulfide (P2S5 or P4S10) is an inorganic compound known for its greenish-yellow appearance and distinct rotten egg odor. Primarily used in industrial applications, it serves as a key raw material in the production of lubricant additives and pesticides, along with specialized roles in mining flotation agents and pharmaceuticals. The compound's chemical reactivity makes it indispensable for synthesizing organophosphorus compounds.</p><p>

<strong>MARKET DRIVERS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Rising Demand for Lubricant Additives to Fuel Phosphorus Pentasulfide Growth</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The global lubricant additives market, valued at over $16 billion in 2024, is a primary driver for phosphorus pentasulfide demand. </p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Agricultural Sector Expansion Boosts Pesticide Applications</strong></p><p>
