<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Volatile Raw Material Prices to Restrain Market Growth</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The SEPS market faces significant challenges from fluctuating prices of key raw materials including styrene and ethylene, which account for approximately 65% of production costs. </p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Competition from Alternative Thermoplastic Elastomers to Limit Market Penetration</strong></p><p>
</p><p>SEPS faces intense competition from other thermoplastic elastomers like SBS, SIS, and TPU, particularly in cost-sensitive applications. </p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Complex Processing Requirements Pose Adoption Challenges</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The specialized processing requirements of SEPS present a barrier to wider adoption, particularly among smaller manufacturers. SEPS typically requires precise temperature control during extrusion and injection molding, with processing windows often narrower than conventional thermoplastics. This necessitates capital investments in equipment upgrades and operator training that can be prohibitive for some potential users. Additionally, the material's sensitivity to processing conditions can lead to higher scrap rates, increasing effective costs for converters.</p><p>
