</p><p>Antimicrobial growth promoters (AGPs) are veterinary drugs or feed additives used in livestock production to enhance growth performance and feed efficiency while preventing bacterial infections. These compounds - including penicillins, macrolides, and tetracyclines - work by modifying gut microbiota to improve nutrient absorption. While effective for animal productivity, concerns about antibiotic resistance have led to regulatory restrictions and growing demand for alternatives like probiotics and phytogenics.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by expanding meat consumption across Southeast Asia, with countries like Vietnam and Indonesia recording 4-5% annual poultry production increases.
