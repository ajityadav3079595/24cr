 <p><span style="font-size:14px"><strong>Definition</strong></span></p>
 
 
 
 <p><span style="font-size:14px">Soda-lime glass, often referred to as soda-lime-silica glass, is primarily composed of silica (SiO2), soda (Na2O), and lime (CaO). This type of glass is the most widely produced and used in the world, known for its affordability, versatility, and ease of production. It is used extensively in products like containers for beverages and food, as well as in construction materials such as windows. Soda-lime glass is favored for its ability to be easily shaped, molded, and mass-produced, which makes it a preferred material for manufacturers in various industries.</span></p>
