 <p><strong>Secondary Alkane Sulfonates (SAS)</strong>, also known as <strong>paraffin sulfonates</strong>, are anionic surfactants widely used in various industries due to their excellent detergency, wetting, and emulsifying properties. These compounds are produced by the sulfoxidation of n-paraffins with sulfur dioxide and oxygen in the presence of water under ultraviolet light irradiation. SAS are known for their high biodegradability and low toxicity, making them an environmentally friendly alternative to traditional surfactants.</p>
