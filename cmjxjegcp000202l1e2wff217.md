<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Material Consistency and Quality Control Present Technical Hurdles</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The heterogeneous nature of post-consumer plastic waste streams creates challenges in producing tiles with consistent mechanical properties. Variations in polymer composition from batch to batch can affect product performance, requiring sophisticated blending technologies. While leading manufacturers have implemented advanced quality control systems, the industry still faces approximately <strong>15-20% higher production rejection rates</strong> compared to virgin material tiles. This not only increases costs but also creates hesitation among specifiers in critical applications where material consistency is paramount. </p><p>Despite growing recycling rates, the supply of suitable feedstock for tile manufacturing faces limitations.
