</p><p>Textile antistatic agents are chemical additives that reduce or eliminate static electricity accumulation in synthetic fibers and fabrics. These agents work by either increasing surface conductivity (ionic types) or absorbing moisture (non-ionic types) to dissipate electrical charges. The major categories include anion, cation, zwitterion, and nonionic variants, each offering specific performance characteristics for different textile applications.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily fueled by Southeast Asia's expanding textile manufacturing sector, particularly in Vietnam, Indonesia, and Thailand. Rising production of synthetic fabrics like polyester and nylon, which generate significant static electricity, creates substantial demand for antistatic treatments. Additionally, growing quality standards in apparel exports and increasing adoption of technical textiles are accelerating market expansion.</p><p>

<strong>MARKET DRIVERS</strong></p><p>

</p><p> Southeast Asian textile sector has emerged as one of the fastest-growing globally, fueled by increasing urbanization, rising disposable incomes, and shifting manufacturing bases from China.
