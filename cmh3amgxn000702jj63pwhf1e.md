</p><p>Disodium Edetate, also known as disodium ethylenediaminetetraacetate, is a chelating agent widely used to bind metal ions in pharmaceutical formulations and cosmetic products. It plays a critical role in stabilizing solutions by preventing oxidation and degradation caused by metal contaminants. The compound is available in various grades, including BP (British Pharmacopoeia) and USP (United States Pharmacopeia), ensuring compliance with stringent quality standards for medical and industrial applications.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand in pharmaceutical formulations, particularly in chelation therapy and as a preservative in topical medications. While the cosmetic industry's expansion fuels demand for product stabilization, regulatory scrutiny on synthetic additives presents challenges. Furthermore, key players like Daffodil Pharmachem and Oasis Fine Chem are investing in production capacity expansions to meet the rising global demand, particularly in emerging Asian markets.</p><p>
