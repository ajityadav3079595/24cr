</p><p>Methyl glycolate is a chemical compound with the molecular formula C3H6O3. It is the methyl ester of glycolic acid and appears as a clear, colorless liquid with a faint characteristic odor. Methyl glycolate is primarily used as an organic solvent and serves as an important chemical intermediate in several industrial processes. It is derived through the esterification of glycolate or the hydrogenation of dimethyl oxalate (DMO), the latter being the more industrially favored method, particularly in China. Its significance lies in its role in the production of polyglycolic acid (PGA), a biodegradable polymer used in a wide range of eco-friendly plastic applications.</p><p>
</p><p>In industrial applications, methyl glycolate exhibits excellent solvency, low toxicity, and high reactivity, making it suitable for use in coatings, resins, and plastic manufacturing. The increasing demand for biodegradable plastics, driven by environmental regulations and the global movement toward sustainability, has positioned methyl glycolate as a critical building block in green chemistry.</p><p>
