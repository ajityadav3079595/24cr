</p><p>While demand grows, multiaxial carbon fiber fabric faces significant cost barriers that restrain broader adoption. These economic factors currently limit the material's use to high-value applications where performance justifies the premium pricing.</p><p>

</p><p>The industry faces persistent technical challenges in scaling production while maintaining quality standards. </p><p>The developing hydrogen economy offers promising opportunities for multiaxial carbon fiber fabrics. Regulatory frameworks in Europe and North America are increasingly mandating higher recycling rates, forcing manufacturers to invest in alternative processes that could impact production costs.</p><p>
