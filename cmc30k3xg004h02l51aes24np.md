</p><p>Diphenyl Ether (DPE), chemically known as <strong>1,1'-oxybisbenzene</strong>, is an organic compound widely used as an intermediate in chemical synthesis and polymer manufacturing. Characterized by two benzene rings linked by an oxygen atom, it serves as a vital raw material in the production of herbicides, flame retardants, and heat transfer fluids. Its thermal stability and low reactivity make it suitable for industrial applications requiring high-temperature resistance.</p><p>
</p><p>Beyond industrial uses, Diphenyl Ether is also employed in the cosmetics sector as a fragrance fixative, enhancing the longevity of perfumes and personal care products. In pharmaceuticals, it acts as a solvent or reagent for synthesizing active ingredients. The market differentiates between purity levels (<strong>0.99, 0.995, and 0.998</strong>), with higher grades preferred for specialized applications demanding minimal impurities.</p><p>

End User Industry Analysis</p><p>
