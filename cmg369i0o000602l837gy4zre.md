</p><p>Biaxially Oriented Polyethylene Terephthalate (BoPET) is a thermoplastic polymer film produced by stretching polyethylene terephthalate (PET) in both machine and transverse directions. This biaxial orientation enhances mechanical properties, thermal stability, and optical clarity, making it suitable for demanding applications. BoPET films are widely recognized by the brand name Mylar and find extensive use in packaging, electronics, and industrial applications.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by increasing demand for flexible packaging solutions, particularly in food and beverage industries, where BoPET's excellent barrier properties extend product shelf life. Additionally, rising adoption in solar panels and electronic displays is contributing to market expansion. Key players like DuPont Teijin Films, Toray Industries, and Mitsubishi Chemical Corporation continue to invest in production capacity expansions and product innovations to meet growing industry demands.</p><p>
