</p><p>Gypsum board texture refers to the variety of surface treatments applied to gypsum boards to create different finishes that enhance the appearance of walls and ceilings. These textures are used primarily in interior design to hide the joints between sheets of drywall, as well as to add a decorative element to the surface. Gypsum board textures not only improve aesthetics but also provide functionality, such as soundproofing, and help in concealing imperfections caused by construction or wear and tear.</p><p>
</p><p>Common types of gypsum board textures include:</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Comb Texture:</strong> A style that uses a comb-like tool to create a grooved, wavy pattern.</p><p><strong>Popcorn Texture:</strong> Also known as "acoustic" texture, this creates a bumpy, rough finish.</p><p><strong>Orange Peel Texture:</strong> A soft, uneven texture resembling the skin of an orange.</p><p>
</p><p>These textures offer versatility for different interior design styles, from modern to traditional. The surface is often painted after the texture is applied, further enhancing its appeal and creating a lasting impact on the overall room design.</p><p>
