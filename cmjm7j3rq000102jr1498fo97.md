</p><p>Silicon carbide (SiC) nanowires are advanced one-dimensional nanomaterials combining the exceptional properties of bulk SiC with unique quantum confinement effects. These nanostructures exhibit superior thermal conductivity (up to 490 W/mK), extreme hardness (Mohs scale 9.5), and wide bandgap characteristics (2.3-3.3 eV). Their high surface-to-volume ratio enables enhanced performance in semiconductor devices, particularly in high-temperature and high-power applications where traditional silicon-based components fail.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by accelerating adoption in power electronics for electric vehicles, with leading manufacturers like Tesla incorporating SiC components in powertrain systems. While the semiconductor sector dominates current applications, emerging opportunities in quantum computing and 5G infrastructure present new growth avenues.
