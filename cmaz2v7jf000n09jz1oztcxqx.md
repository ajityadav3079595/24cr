</p><p><strong>Polyvinyl chloride (PVC)</strong> is a white, amorphous, odorless powder primarily used to produce both <strong>rigid and flexible PVC resins</strong>. Rigid PVC is strong and inflexible, making it ideal for construction applications, while flexible PVC includes plasticizers that give it a soft and stretchable nature. Due to its exceptional <strong>flame retardancy, abrasion resistance, chemical resistance, and electrical insulation properties</strong>, PVC is one of the five major general-purpose resins alongside PE, PP, PS, and ABS.</p><p>
</p><p>Common PVC products include:</p><p>
</p><p>Pipes and conduits</p><p>Roofing tiles</p><p>Wire coatings</p><p>Flooring</p><p>Films and sheets</p><p>
