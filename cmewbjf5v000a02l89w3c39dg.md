</p><p>Diphenylamine (DPA) is a crystalline organic compound primarily used as an antioxidant in rubber and lubricants, with additional applications in dyes, pharmaceuticals, and gunpowder stabilizers. Its appearance varies from off-white to brown depending on exposure to air and light, and it remains insoluble in water. The compound plays a critical role in extending product lifespans across multiple industries by preventing oxidative degradation.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is driven by increasing demand from the rubber industry, particularly in Asia-Pacific, where automotive and industrial sectors are expanding rapidly. However, stringent environmental regulations in Europe and North America regarding chemical manufacturing pose challenges to market expansion.
