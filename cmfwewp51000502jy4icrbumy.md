</p><p>1-Kestose is a prebiotic fructooligosaccharide (FOS) that selectively stimulates the growth of beneficial gut bacteria like bifidobacteria. As a <strong>non-digestible carbohydrate</strong>, it functions as a dietary fiber and has gained significant traction in the nutraceutical and functional food industries. The compound occurs naturally in small quantities in foods like onions, garlic, and honey, but commercial production typically involves enzymatic synthesis from sucrose.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by rising consumer awareness about gut health and the benefits of prebiotics. However, high production costs remain a challenge for widespread adoption. Recent developments include innovations in enzymatic production methods by key players like Showa Denko and Baolingbao Biology, which have improved yield and purity. The powder form currently dominates the market, accounting for over 65% of sales in 2024, due to its versatility in food applications and longer shelf life compared to syrup formulations.</p><p>
