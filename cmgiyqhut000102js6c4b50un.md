</p><p><strong>Paper Dry Strength Additives (DSAs) are specialized chemical compounds used in the papermaking process to improve the dry strength properties of paper. These additives play a critical role in enhancing the internal bonding of fibers within the paper sheet, thereby increasing parameters such as tensile strength, burst strength, tear resistance, wax pick value, stiffness, and folding endurance. These improvements are crucial for ensuring the paper's structural integrity, especially for packaging and printing applications. Common types of dry strength additives include natural polymers like starch and synthetic polymers such as polyacrylamide (PAM) and polyvinylamine (PVAm).</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>By promoting fiber-to-fiber bonding, DSAs allow manufacturers to reduce fiber usage while still meeting strength requirements, thus optimizing production costs and sustainability.</strong></p><p>
