</p><p>Naphthalene (CAS 91-20-3) is a polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon with the chemical formula C10H8. It is the simplest compound of its class and is primarily derived from coal tar and petroleum refining processes. Naphthalene appears as a white crystalline solid and emits a characteristic odor detectable at very low concentrations (as little as 0.08 ppm by mass). Structurally, it comprises two fused benzene rings, making it an important raw material in the chemical industry. One of its most recognized uses is as the primary active ingredient in traditional mothballs. Its applications span various industrial sectors, including the production of phthalic anhydride, refined naphthalene, and water-reducing admixtures.</p><p>
