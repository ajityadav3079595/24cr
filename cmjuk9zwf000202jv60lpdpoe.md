</p><p>Riboflavin, also known as vitamin B2, is a water-soluble vitamin essential for human and animal health. It plays a critical role in energy metabolism by acting as a precursor for coenzymes flavin mononucleotide (FMN) and flavin adenine dinucleotide (FAD). These coenzymes are involved in various redox reactions across metabolic pathways. While naturally present in foods like dairy, eggs, and leafy greens, riboflavin is also widely used as a dietary supplement to address deficiencies and support overall wellness.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by increasing health consciousness among consumers, rising demand for fortified foods, and expanding applications in animal feed formulations. Key manufacturers like DSM and BASF continue to innovate production processes to enhance efficiency and meet the rising global demand.</p><p>
<strong>MARKET DYNAMICS</strong></p><p>
<strong>MARKET DRIVERS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Growing Health Awareness and Nutritional Supplement Demand Accelerate Market Growth</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The global vitamin B2 (riboflavin) market is experiencing substantial growth due to increasing health consciousness among consumers. With rising prevalence of nutritional deficiencies and metabolic disorders, demand for riboflavin supplements has grown by approximately 7% annually over the past three years. The food fortification segment alone accounts for over 30% of total riboflavin consumption globally, particularly in developed markets where regulatory mandates ensure staple food enrichment.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Expansion of Animal Feed Industry Drives Riboflavin Consumption</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Livestock production growth continues to fuel riboflavin demand, with the animal feed additives segment representing nearly 45% of total market volume.
