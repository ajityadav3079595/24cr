</p><p>Naphthenic acid refers to a mixture of naturally occurring cycloaliphatic carboxylic acids primarily derived from high acid crude oils. These acids, typically found in petroleum, exhibit properties that make them highly useful in a wide range of industrial applications, including metal naphthenate production, wood preservation, lubricants, corrosion inhibitors, and fuel additives. In their crude form, naphthenic acids are characterized by a dark brown, oily appearance. However, after undergoing a refining process, they transform into transparent yellow or orange liquids. </p><p><strong>Restraints</strong></p><p>
