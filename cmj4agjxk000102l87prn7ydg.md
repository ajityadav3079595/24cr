</p><p>Increasing regulatory oversight of antimicrobial compounds presents challenges for chlorhexidine diacetate adoption. While chlorhexidine diacetate remains approved for medical use, its expansion into consumer and industrial applications faces significant documentation requirements, with approval processes often taking 3-5 years and costing manufacturers millions in compliance expenses.</p><p>

While chlorhexidine maintains advantages in cost-effectiveness and proven track record, manufacturers must continuously innovate formulations to maintain competitiveness against these emerging technologies, particularly in price-sensitive market segments.</p><p>
