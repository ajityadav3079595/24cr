</p><p>Thermosetting plastic to fuel is an advanced recycling technology that converts non-recyclable thermoset polymers into usable fuels through processes like pyrolysis and gasification. These polymers, including epoxy resins, phenolic resins, and polyurethanes, are transformed into valuable outputs such as crude oil, diesel, and hydrogen. Unlike mechanical recycling methods, this chemical conversion process can handle contaminated or mixed plastic waste streams.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing environmental regulations on plastic waste, rising demand for alternative fuels, and technological advancements in conversion processes. Major industry players are investing in large-scale facilities to commercialize these technologies, with Europe currently leading in adoption due to stringent waste management policies. However, high initial capital costs and technical challenges in scaling operations remain key restraints for widespread market penetration.</p><p>

While fuel conversion remains the most viable solution for many non-recyclable thermosets, the development of alternative technologies requires continuous innovation to maintain market position.</p><p>
