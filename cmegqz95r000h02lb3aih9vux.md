<strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Indene (CAS 95-13-6)</strong> is a colorless, flammable polycyclic hydrocarbon with the chemical formula <strong>C9H8</strong>. Its molecular structure consists of a benzene ring fused with a cyclopentene ring, creating a distinct aromatic liquid. While indene is typically colorless, it often appears as a pale yellow substance in its pure form. Indene is primarily used as an industrial chemical in the manufacturing of <strong>indene/coumarone thermoplastic resins</strong>, which find applications in various industries, particularly in the production of high-performance adhesives, coatings, and rubber products.</p><p>
</p><p>The compound is produced via the distillation of coal tar and is a key intermediate in the synthesis of several industrial chemicals. Due to its aromatic properties, indene is also used in producing a variety of derivatives, including indene derivatives, which are essential in industries such as automotive, chemicals, and consumer goods.</p><p>
