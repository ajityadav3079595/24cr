</p><p><strong>Industrial Minerals</strong> are naturally occurring, non-metallic minerals that are extracted and processed for their physical and chemical properties, rather than for the extraction of metals. These minerals are integral to a wide range of industrial applications, including construction, ceramics, glassmaking, filtration, electronics, agriculture, and more. Unlike metal ores or fuels, industrial minerals are valued based on their functionality in applications rather than their intrinsic metal content or energy value.</p><p>
</p><p>Common examples of industrial minerals include limestone, gypsum, silica sand, kaolin, bentonite, barite, talc, and phosphate rock. These minerals play a pivotal role in manufacturing, infrastructure development, and environmental applications. As global industries expand and technology evolves, the importance of industrial minerals continues to grow, fueling demand across both traditional and emerging sectors.</p><p>
