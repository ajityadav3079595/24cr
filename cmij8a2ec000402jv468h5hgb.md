</p><p>Collagen peptides are bioactive protein fragments derived from collagen through enzymatic hydrolysis, with molecular weights typically ranging between 500-30,000 Daltons. Unlike intact collagen, these peptides demonstrate superior bioavailability because their smaller molecular structure allows for direct absorption in the human intestine. This makes them particularly valuable in nutritional supplements and functional foods where efficient nutrient delivery is critical. The peptides are classified by source into bovine, porcine, fish, and poultry variants, with porcine collagen currently dominating approximately 35% of the market share.</p><p>
</p><p>The market expansion is further propelled by the growing demand for anti-aging products in cosmetics, with collagen peptides being a key ingredient in topical formulations and nutricosmetics. Europe currently leads consumption with about 30% market share, while North America and Asia-Pacific are experiencing accelerated growth due to rising disposable incomes and health consciousness. Strategic acquisitions, such as Darling Ingredients' 2023 purchase of Gelnex to expand its collagen production capacity, demonstrate industry players' commitment to meeting this surging global demand.</p><p>
