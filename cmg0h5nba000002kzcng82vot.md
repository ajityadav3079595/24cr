</p><p>Microcentrifuge tubes are small, cylindrical containers typically made from ultra-clear polypropylene or polyethylene (PE) resin, designed for efficient sample preparation, storage, and centrifugation in laboratory settings. These tubes feature secure flat-top snap caps that provide airtight sealing, ensuring sample integrity while being easy to handle. They are widely used in life science and biological laboratories for applications such as DNA/RNA extraction, protein analysis, and PCR due to their compatibility with standard microcentrifuge rotors.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing research activities in genomics, proteomics, and drug development, alongside the rising adoption of automated laboratory equipment. <strong>Thermo Fisher, Corning, and Eppendorf</strong> dominate the market, collectively holding over 54% of the global share.
