Chloroacetyl chloride (CAS: 79-04-9), referred to as CAC, is an acid chloride derivative of chloroacetic acid, with the chemical formula C2H2Cl2O. As a colorless or slightly yellow liquid, it is highly irritating. Soluble in benzene, ether, carbon tetrachloride and chloroform. Chloroacetyl chloride is chemically active and decomposes when exposed to water and alcohol, and can be used as an acylation reagent. Also used in organic synthesis to prepare amino acids. In the industrial field, chloroacetyl chloride is used in the production of pesticides, refrigerants, fire extinguishing agents, dyes, lubricant additives, and fluoroacetamide. It is currently the most widely used in pesticides.
