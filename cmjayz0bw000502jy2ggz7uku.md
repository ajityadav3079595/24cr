</p><p>Cooling agents for cosmetics are specialized ingredients designed to provide an instant cooling sensation while offering mild therapeutic benefits. These compounds work by activating thermoreceptors in the skin or mucous membranes, creating a refreshing effect. Common types include menthol derivatives (like menthyl lactate), synthetic coolants (such as WS-3 and WS-23), and natural alternatives (peppermint oil, eucalyptus extracts). They are widely incorporated into skincare, haircare, and oral care formulations.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing consumer demand for sensorial product experiences and multifunctional cosmetic ingredients. Rising preference for post-treatment soothing products in dermatology and the expansion of men's grooming categories are key growth drivers.
