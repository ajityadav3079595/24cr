</p><p>Methylene Diphenyl Diisocyanate is a key chemical precursor primarily used in polyurethane production. As an aromatic diisocyanate, it reacts with polyols to form rigid or flexible foams, adhesives, coatings, and elastomers. The industry classifies MDI into three main types: polymeric MDI (accounting for 75% of demand), pure MDI, and modified MDI variants. These formulations find extensive applications across construction, automotive, furniture, and electronics industries due to their insulation properties and durability.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand for energy-efficient building materials and lightweight automotive components.
