</p><p>Polyisobutylene succinimide, commonly known as PIB succinimide, is a key ashless dispersant used in lubricant and fuel formulations. It is synthesized through the reaction of polyisobutylene (PIB) with succinic anhydride, followed by treatment with an amine to form the imide linkage. This chemical structure provides excellent thermal stability and dispersant properties, making it valuable in various industrial applications.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by increasing demand from the automotive and industrial sectors, where it is used in engine oils and industrial lubricants. The material's ability to prevent sludge formation and maintain engine cleanliness has made it particularly important in modern high-performance lubricants. Additionally, tightening environmental regulations regarding emissions and fuel efficiency are pushing manufacturers to develop advanced lubricant additives, further driving demand for PIB succinimide.</p><p>

The region accounts for approximately 25% of global PIBSI consumption, with increasing investments in bio-based and low-toxicity lubricant formulations. </p><p><strong>South America</strong></p><p>
