</p><p>1,3-Hexafluorobutadiene (C4F6) is an organofluorine compound composed of carbon, fluorine, and hydrogen. Its chemical formula is (CF2=CF)2, and it is a colorless, gaseous compound under standard conditions. It is most commonly used as an etchant in microelectronics, particularly in the semiconductor industry. The compound is a perfluoro-analog of butadiene, which is a conjugated diene known for its applications in synthetic rubber and polymers. 1,3-Hexafluorobutadiene is synthesized by coupling C2 compounds such as chlorotrifluoroethylene or bromotrifluoroethylene, making it a highly specialized compound with unique chemical properties.</p><p>
</p><p>The compound’s primary industrial use lies in its ability to provide controlled etching during the fabrication of semiconductors, especially in the production of microchips and integrated circuits. Additionally, its stability and reactive characteristics make it an essential component in certain types of plasma etching and deposition processes.</p><p>
