</p><p>Insulation Vulcanized Fiber is a cellulose-based material treated with zinc chloride and processed under high pressure to create durable electrical insulation products. These fibers exhibit excellent dielectric strength, mechanical toughness, and thermal resistance, making them essential components in electrical equipment, household appliances, and industrial applications. The material is commercially available in several variants including red, black, and white vulcanized fiber, each with specific insulation properties.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand for reliable insulation materials in electrical applications and expanding renewable energy infrastructure. However, the market faces challenges from substitute materials like plastics and composites.
