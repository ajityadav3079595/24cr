</p><p>Hydrodeoxygenation (HDO) catalysts are specialized chemical agents that facilitate the removal of oxygen from biomass-derived feedstocks under high temperature and pressure conditions. These catalysts play a crucial role in converting bio-oils and other renewable resources into high-quality biofuels, including biodiesel and bio-gasoline. The primary types include conventional catalysts, solid acid supports, metal-based catalysts, and bifunctional metal-acid catalysts.</p><p>

<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>

</p><p>While HDO catalyst performance continues improving, their adoption faces economic hurdles. These economics make small-scale bio-refineries hesitant to adopt HDO technology despite its technical merits.</p><p>
