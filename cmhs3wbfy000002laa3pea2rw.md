</p><p>Cumene Hydroperoxide is an organic peroxide derived from cumene, where the alpha-hydrogen is replaced by a hydroperoxy group. As a key intermediate in chemical synthesis, it serves primarily in the production of acetone and phenol through the cumene process. While relatively stable at room temperature, it requires careful handling due to explosion risks during decomposition, which yields acetophenone, cumyl alcohol, and methylstyrene.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from end-use industries like plastics, where it functions as a polymerization initiator. However, stringent safety regulations may restrain expansion in some regions. Asia-Pacific currently dominates consumption, accounting for over 45% of global demand in 2024, with China leading regional growth. Key players including Merck KGaA and Tokyo Chemical Industry are investing in production capacity expansions to meet rising industrial requirements.</p><p>
