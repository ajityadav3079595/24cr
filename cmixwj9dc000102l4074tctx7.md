</p><p>Fluoride salts are inorganic compounds containing fluorine anions, widely used in applications ranging from nuclear reactors to aluminum production. These salts include sodium fluoride, potassium fluoride, ammonium fluoride, and lithium fluoride, each serving distinct industrial purposes. Their high thermal stability and ionic conductivity make them particularly valuable in molten salt reactors and energy storage systems.</p><p>

<strong>MARKET DRIVERS</strong></p><p>

</p><p>The global push toward clean energy sources is driving substantial growth in the fluoride salt market, particularly in molten salt reactors (MSRs). These advanced nuclear reactors utilize fluoride salts as both coolant and fuel carrier due to their superior heat transfer properties and high-temperature stability. With over 70 MSR projects currently in development worldwide, the demand for high-purity fluoride salts is projected to increase by 12-15% annually through 2032.</p><p>

</p><p>While fluoride salts offer numerous industrial benefits, their production and handling face significant environmental regulations.
