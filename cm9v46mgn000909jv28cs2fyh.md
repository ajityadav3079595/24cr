</p><p>High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) Reagents are specialized chemicals used in HPLC, a powerful analytical technique employed to separate, identify, and quantify components within a sample. These reagents play a crucial role in ensuring the accuracy, reproducibility, and sensitivity of the chromatographic results. They include solvents, buffers, ion-pair reagents, derivatization reagents, and others, each tailored for specific types of analyses and column chemistries.</p><p>
</p><p>The technique relies on a high-pressure pump to pass a liquid solvent containing the sample mixture through a packed column. The interaction between the sample components and the stationary phase enables separation. HPLC reagents must be of ultra-high purity to prevent interferences or background noise that may distort analytical outcomes. Applications span pharmaceutical testing, environmental analysis, food and beverage testing, and more.</p><p>
