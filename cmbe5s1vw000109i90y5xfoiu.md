</p><p>Construction glass sealants are specialized adhesives designed primarily for use in the construction industry, specifically for sealing windows and glass panels. These sealants are used to fill gaps and joints between the glass and the building’s frame to provide waterproofing, insulation, and weather resistance. The sealants are typically made from materials such as silicone, polyurethane, and polysulfide, which offer high durability, flexibility, and adhesion properties.</p><p>
</p><p>The primary function of construction glass sealants is to enhance the structural integrity of the building while preventing water infiltration, air leakage, and thermal expansion or contraction, which can damage the building. They are widely used for both exterior and interior glass applications, providing essential sealing properties to ensure the longevity and functionality of glass in construction projects.</p><p>

	</p><p><strong>Price Volatility of Raw Materials</strong>: The price of raw materials used in the manufacturing of glass sealants, such as silicone and polyurethane, can fluctuate due to supply chain disruptions and the availability of raw materials. <strong>Restraints:</strong></p><p>

	</p><p><strong>Price Volatility of Raw Materials</strong>: The cost of key raw materials, such as silicones, resins, and polymers, can fluctuate due to supply chain disruptions or price changes in petroleum-based products.
