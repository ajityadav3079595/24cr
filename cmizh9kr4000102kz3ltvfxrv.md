</p><p>Glassy carbon plates are a type of advanced carbon material known for their exceptional combination of mechanical, chemical, and thermal properties. These plates are made through the pyrolysis of certain polymeric precursors, resulting in a non-graphitizing carbon with a vitreous (glassy) structure.</p><p>
</p><p>Key attributes of glassy carbon include:</p><p>
</p><p><strong>High purity and chemical inertness</strong>, making them suitable for analytical applications</p><p><strong>Excellent thermal stability</strong>, allowing them to withstand extreme temperatures</p><p><strong>Corrosion resistance</strong>, especially in acidic and alkaline environments</p><p><strong>High electrical conductivity</strong>, which enables their use in electrochemical sensors and electrodes</p><p><strong>Impermeability to gases and liquids</strong>, ideal for high-integrity sealing or structural applications</p><p>
</p><p>Due to these properties, glassy carbon plates find extensive usage in:</p><p>
</p><p>Electrochemistry (as electrodes)</p><p>Metallurgical casting and smelting</p><p>High-temperature crucibles</p><p>Semiconductor manufacturing</p><p>Prosthetic devices</p><p>Solar energy applications</p><p>
