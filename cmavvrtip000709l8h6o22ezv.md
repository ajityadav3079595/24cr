</p><p>Overcharge protection electrolyte additives are a crucial category of functional chemicals integrated into lithium-ion battery systems. These additives are specifically formulated to enhance the safety, reliability, and longevity of batteries by preventing overcharge conditions. When a lithium-ion battery is charged beyond its recommended voltage threshold, it becomes vulnerable to thermal runaway, electrolyte decomposition, and even fire or explosion. Overcharge protection additives intervene chemically or electrochemically to mitigate these risks by initiating voltage-dependent reactions that suppress further charge accumulation, thus stabilizing the battery system. These additives are widely utilized in electric vehicles (EVs), energy storage systems (ESS), consumer electronics, and aerospace applications.</p><p>
</p><p>Their mechanisms may include redox shuttle reactions or other reversible chemical pathways that help absorb excess charge, thereby ensuring battery safety without compromising performance. The development and integration of these additives are driven by the growing demand for safe and durable battery systems, especially as global electrification trends accelerate.</p><p>
