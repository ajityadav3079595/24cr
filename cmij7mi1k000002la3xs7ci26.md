These pipes are engineered to withstand internal stresses and external environmental factors, coming in multiple material types including steel, ductile iron, concrete, and advanced composites. Key applications span oil &amp; gas transmission, water distribution, industrial processing, and construction infrastructure.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is being driven by increasing urbanization and subsequent infrastructure development, particularly in emerging economies. <strong>MARKET DRIVERS</strong></p><p>

</p><p>Global infrastructure development remains a primary driver for the pressure pipe market, with governments allocating over $2 trillion annually for water, oil &amp; gas, and urban development projects. </p><p>Furthermore, composite pipe technologies combining steel reinforcement with polymer liners are gaining traction. These hybrid solutions offer the structural integrity of metallic pipes with the chemical resistance of plastics, making them ideal for harsh industrial applications. The oil &amp; gas sector has particularly embraced these innovations, with composite pipes representing over 18% of all new midstream pipeline installations as of 2024.</p><p>
