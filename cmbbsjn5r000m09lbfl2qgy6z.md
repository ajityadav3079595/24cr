</p><p><strong>3D NAND KrF Photoresist</strong> is a specialized light-sensitive material used in the photolithography process during the fabrication of 3D NAND flash memory chips. KrF stands for <strong>Krypton Fluoride</strong>, which emits deep ultraviolet (DUV) light at a wavelength of 248 nanometers, allowing for precise pattern transfer onto silicon wafers. Unlike traditional planar NAND memory, 3D NAND flash is built vertically, stacking memory cells in multiple layers. This vertical stacking increases storage density while maintaining a smaller footprint. The KrF photoresist plays a critical role in enabling this complex structure by providing the high resolution, pattern fidelity, and etch resistance needed for accurate layer alignment and definition.</p><p>
</p><p>The photoresist material undergoes chemical changes when exposed to KrF light during the photolithography process. It allows selective etching of the underlying material to form intricate micro-patterns, a necessity for forming memory cells and interconnects. Given the complexity of 3D NAND architecture, photoresists used in this process must also withstand multi-step etching and deposition procedures, making KrF resists essential for reliable manufacturing.</p><p>
