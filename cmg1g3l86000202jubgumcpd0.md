</p><p>Hydrogenation petroleum resins are synthetic polymers derived from petroleum feedstocks through catalytic hydrogenation, which enhances their thermal stability and color properties. These resins are widely used as tackifiers in adhesives, modifiers in coatings, and additives in packaging materials due to their excellent compatibility with various polymers and solvents. The market is segmented into hydrogenated C9, C5, and DCPD petroleum resins, each serving distinct industrial applications.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from the adhesive and packaging industries, particularly in North America and Asia-Pacific. <strong>MARKET DRIVERS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Growing Demand from Adhesive &amp; Sealant Industry to Propel Market Expansion</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The adhesive and sealant industry remains a key driver for hydrogenation petroleum resins, accounting for over 40% of global consumption. With the global adhesives market projected to grow at a CAGR of 4.8% through 2030, this demand is directly translating into increased resin consumption. <strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
