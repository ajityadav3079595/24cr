</p><p>Riboflavin, commonly known as vitamin B2, is a water-soluble vitamin essential for human nutrition and animal health. This micronutrient plays a critical role in energy metabolism by acting as a precursor for flavin mononucleotide (FMN) and flavin adenine dinucleotide (FAD), coenzymes involved in numerous redox reactions. While naturally present in foods like dairy, eggs and green vegetables, industrially produced riboflavin serves as a vital additive in pharmaceuticals, food fortification and animal feed.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is being driven by increasing awareness about nutritional deficiencies, expansion of fortified food products and rising demand in livestock nutrition.
