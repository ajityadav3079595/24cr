</p><p>Fluorescent sterols are lipid molecules derived from cholesterol or plant sterols that exhibit fluorescence properties, making them valuable tools for biological research, diagnostics, and industrial applications. These compounds are widely used in membrane studies, drug discovery, and cellular imaging due to their ability to mimic natural sterols while providing enhanced detection capabilities. Primary types include BODIPY-cholesterol, NBD-cholesterol, and dansyl-labeled sterols, among others.</p><p>
