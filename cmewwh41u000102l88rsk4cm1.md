</p><p>Sodium Heparin is a highly effective anticoagulant that works by preventing the formation of blood clots. It is widely used in the treatment of conditions like venous thromboembolism, deep vein thrombosis, and pulmonary embolism, as well as in the management of clotting risks in various surgeries. Heparin works by inhibiting thrombin and other clotting factors, thus preventing blood clots from forming. Sodium Heparin is administered either orally or via injection, depending on the treatment scenario, and is essential in critical care units, dialysis centers, and during surgical procedures where the risk of clot formation is high.</p><p>

</p><p><strong>Rising Incidence of Cardiovascular Diseases and Thromboembolic Disorders:</strong> As the global population ages, the prevalence of cardiovascular diseases (CVD) and other thromboembolic conditions has risen sharply. Blood clot-related diseases, such as venous thromboembolism and atrial fibrillation, are leading drivers of demand for Sodium Heparin. These conditions are common among the elderly, and the global population’s aging trend continues to support market growth.</p><p><strong>Increasing Surgical Procedures and Dialysis Treatments:</strong> With the growing number of surgical procedures performed globally and the increasing demand for dialysis treatments, there is a heightened need for Sodium Heparin. <strong> Restraints:</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Side Effects and Safety Concerns:</strong> While Sodium Heparin is an essential therapeutic agent, it can have adverse effects like bleeding complications, thrombocytopenia, and allergic reactions. This price barrier may limit the growth potential of the market in regions with lower healthcare budgets.</p><p>
<strong>Opportunities:</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Growing Awareness of Blood Clotting Disorders:</strong> Increased awareness regarding the prevention and treatment of blood clots is expected to contribute to the growth of the market. <strong>Challenges:</strong></p><p>
