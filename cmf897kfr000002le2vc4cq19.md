</p><p>Amphoteric polymers are specialized chemical additives that enhance the dry strength of paper by improving fiber-to-fiber bonding. These polymers contain both cationic and anionic functional groups, allowing them to interact effectively with cellulose fibers. They are widely used in paperboard, packaging materials, and specialty papers where superior tensile strength and durability are required. While traditional dry strength agents remain prevalent, amphoteric polymers are gaining traction due to their <strong>environmental compatibility</strong> and <strong>higher efficiency</strong> compared to conventional alternatives.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is further supported by stringent environmental regulations promoting sustainable paper production. Key industry players like <strong>BASF SE</strong> and <strong>Kemira</strong> are investing in R&amp;D to develop next-generation amphoteric polymers with improved performance characteristics.
