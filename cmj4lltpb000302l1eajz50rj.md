</p><p>Hydrogels are three-dimensional, hydrophilic polymer networks capable of absorbing and retaining significant amounts of water or biological fluids. These smart materials can be derived from natural sources (like collagen or alginate) or synthetically produced (such as polyacrylamide or polyethylene glycol). Their unique water-holding capacity, biocompatibility, and responsiveness to environmental stimuli make them invaluable across medical applications including wound care, drug delivery, and tissue engineering, as well as industrial uses in agriculture, cosmetics, and hygiene products.</p><p>
