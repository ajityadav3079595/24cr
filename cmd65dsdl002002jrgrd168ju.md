<p>The global fish paper market was valued at <strong>US$ 92 million in 2024</strong> and is projected to reach <strong>US$ 128 million by 2032</strong>, growing at a <strong>Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 4.2%</strong> during the forecast period (2025–2032). The market has shown consistent expansion over the years, driven by technological advancements and the rising need for durable, high-performance materials in industrial applications.</p><p>
</p><p>Fish paper, also known as vulcanized fiber, is a durable, flexible material made from cellulose fibers treated with zinc chloride and sulfuric acid. It is widely used in electrical and mechanical industries for insulation, gaskets, and protective layers due to its high dielectric strength, thermal resistance, and mechanical durability. The material is available in two primary forms: <strong>sheet</strong> and <strong>roll</strong>, catering to different industrial needs. Fish paper is particularly valued for its ability to withstand high temperature and resist moisture, making it ideal for applications in transformers, motors, and other electrical components.
