<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>High Production Costs Limit Widespread Commercial Adoption</strong></p><p>
</p><p>While CVD SiC offers superior performance characteristics, its production remains significantly more expensive than silicon-based alternatives. The specialized equipment required for high-temperature deposition combined with complex quality control processes results in per-unit costs approximately 3-5 times higher than conventional semiconductors. This cost premium creates adoption barriers in price-sensitive applications, particularly for consumer electronics and automotive auxiliary systems where cost-per-watt remains the primary purchasing criterion.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Material Processing Challenges Impact Yield Rates</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The CVD process for SiC involves precise control of temperature gradients, gas flows, and substrate preparation to prevent defects. These technical challenges in scaling production while maintaining material consistency create supply chain bottlenecks, particularly for applications requiring large-area single crystals. The mismatch between material potential and manufacturing readiness continues to constrain market expansion.</p><p>

</p><p>Patents covering foundational CVD SiC production methods create fragmented technology landscapes.
