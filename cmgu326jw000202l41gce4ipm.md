</p><p>Thermosetting powder coatings are dry finishing processes where polymer resins are combined with curatives, pigments, and other additives to form a powder. When heated, these coatings chemically crosslink to form durable, heat-resistant finishes that won't melt or reflow. Major resin types include epoxy, polyester, acrylic, and polyurethane hybrids, each offering distinct performance characteristics for different applications.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from automotive and construction sectors, where powder coatings provide superior corrosion resistance and environmental benefits compared to liquid paints. Strict VOC emission regulations worldwide are accelerating adoption, as powder coatings contain no solvents. Recent developments include bio-based resin formulations and low-temperature cure technologies expanding application possibilities. Key players like PPG, AkzoNobel, and Axalta are investing in sustainable product innovations to capitalize on growing demand for eco-friendly coating solutions.</p><p>
