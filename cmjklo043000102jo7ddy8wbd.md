This specialized hydrocarbon polymer finds extensive application in roofing systems (accounting for 60% of consumption), waterproofing membranes, pipe coatings, and industrial adhesives. The material's unique properties stem from increased asphaltene content (35-40%) and higher softening points (85-140Â°C) compared to conventional bitumen.</p><p>
