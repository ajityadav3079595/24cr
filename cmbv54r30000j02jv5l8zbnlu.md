# Global Liquid Sodium Aluminate Market Research Report 2025-2032

<p>The global liquid sodium aluminate market was valued at <strong>US$ 382 million in 2024</strong> and is projected to reach <strong>US$ 567 million by 2032</strong>, exhibiting a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of <strong>5.1%</strong> during the forecast period (2025–2032). This growth trajectory is supported by increasing demand from key industries such as water treatment, construction, and paper manufacturing. The market has demonstrated steady historical growth, supported by technological advancements and expanding industrial applications. The Asia-Pacific region continues to drive substantial demand due to rapid industrialization, while North America and Europe maintain steady consumption backed by stringent environmental regulations and high-performance applications in water treatment and construction industries.</p><p>
</p><p>Liquid sodium aluminate is an inorganic compound primarily used as an additive in industrial applications due to its high reactivity and alkaline properties. It is manufactured by dissolving alumina trihydrate or aluminum hydroxide in sodium hydroxide solutions, resulting in varying concentrations such as 38%, 43%, and 45% solutions. Key applications of liquid sodium aluminate include water treatment processes, where it acts as a coagulant aid, and in the paper industry as a sizing agent. Additionally, it serves as an accelerator in cement formulations within the construction sector and finds utility in chemical manufacturing for specialized reactions. Regional Analysis</p><p>

Additionally, the oil and chemical industries in the region contribute to demand, leveraging sodium aluminate for specialized reactions and corrosion resistance.</p><p>
Europe</p><p>

Latin America &amp; Middle East &amp; Africa</p><p>

End User Industry Analysis</p><p>
</p><p>The liquid sodium aluminate market is significantly influenced by industries such as <strong>water treatment, construction, paper manufacturing, and oil &amp; chemicals</strong>. The water treatment sector accounts for the largest share, owing to widespread use in coagulation and pH adjustment. Regulatory mandates to improve water quality amplify demand. In construction, sodium aluminate accelerates concrete setting, enhancing productivity in infrastructure projects. The paper industry relies on it for pulp processing, while the oil sector employs it for catalysis and corrosion inhibition. Quantitative analysis indicates that water treatment applications contribute over 35% of total consumption, followed by construction (~25%) and paper (~20%). Expanding industrial activities in emerging economies further diversify end-user reliance.</p><p>
