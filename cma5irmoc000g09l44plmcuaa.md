</p><p>High-alloy powder refers to materials produced using a high-pressure water atomization process, such as iron-based, nickel-based, cobalt-based alloys, among others. These powders offer exceptional wear and corrosion resistance and can be customized for specific particle size distribution and chemical composition. They are primarily used in the production of powder metallurgy parts like automobile and motorcycle valve guides and rings.</p><p>

</p><p style="margin-left:40px">3. Growing adoption of powder metallurgy parts, particularly in automotive and manufacturing industries, is fueling the demand for High Alloy Powder.</p><p>
