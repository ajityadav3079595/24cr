</p><p>Clad Plate refers to a composite steel material created by bonding two or more metals together, typically using processes such as explosion bonding or roll bonding. The main objective of cladding is to combine the properties of various metals to enhance the performance and characteristics of the final product. The core material is usually steel, while the cladding materials could include stainless steel, nickel, copper, high nickel alloys, and copper-nickel alloys. </p><p>The most common types of Clad Plates are:</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Explosion Bonded Clad Plate</strong>: This involves using an explosive charge to bond metals at high pressure, ensuring a durable connection between the cladding and base material.</p><p><strong>Roll Bonded Clad Plate</strong>: This type is produced by passing the metal plates through rollers under high pressure, ensuring a strong bond between the cladding and base material.</p><p>
</p><p>Clad Plates are widely used in industries such as oil and gas, chemical manufacturing, and water treatment, where equipment must endure harsh conditions, making these materials vital in applications where performance, longevity, and safety are critical.</p><p>
