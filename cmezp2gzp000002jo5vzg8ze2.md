</p><p>Methyl vinyl ether is an organic compound with the chemical formula CH3OCH=CH2. It is a colorless gas with a sweet odor and is classified as the simplest enol ether. Methyl vinyl ether is highly reactive and prone to polymerization, leading to the formation of polyvinyl ethers. It has various industrial uses, such as in the production of copolymers for coatings and lacquers, as a modifier for resins, and as a synthetic building block and monomer. Additionally, methyl vinyl ether can be used in alcohol protection reactions and other organic syntheses. However, due to its reactivity, it must be handled with caution and appropriate safety measures.</p><p>
