</p><p>Electric motor cores are essential components that provide structural support and magnetic flux pathways in electric motors. These laminated steel cores, consisting of silicon steel sheets, minimize energy loss through eddy currents while enhancing motor efficiency. The market includes various types such as welding motor cores, riveting motor cores, and brushless motor cores, each catering to specific applications in industrial and automotive sectors.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by accelerating EV adoption, with battery electric vehicles accounting for 58% of motor core demand in 2024. While North America shows steady growth, Asia Pacific dominates with 62% market share due to China's booming EV production. Furthermore, technological advancements in high-performance silicon steel and automated manufacturing processes are creating new opportunities. Leading manufacturers like Mitsui High-tec and POSCO are expanding production capacities to meet the surging demand from automotive OEMs transitioning to electrification.</p><p>

</p><p>Alternative motor technologies, particularly advanced axial flux designs and solid core configurations, are emerging as competitive threats to traditional laminated core designs. While these technologies currently represent a small market share, their development trajectory suggests potential market disruption within specific application segments by 2028-2030.</p><p>

The development of new alloy compositions and lamination techniques is enabling the production of lighter, more compact, and more powerful motor cores.</p><p>
