</p><p>Pozzolans are a class of siliceous or aluminosilicate materials that, when finely ground and mixed with calcium hydroxide in the presence of water, form cementitious compounds. These materials play a crucial role in enhancing the durability and sustainability of concrete while reducing its carbon footprint. The two primary types are natural pozzolans (such as volcanic ash and diatomaceous earth) and artificial pozzolans (including fly ash and silica fume).</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing infrastructure development, stricter environmental regulations promoting sustainable construction materials, and the rising demand for high-performance concrete. While natural pozzolans face supply chain challenges, artificial variants are gaining traction due to consistent quality and availability. Key industry players like LafargeHolcim and CEMEX are investing in pozzolan-based sustainable cement solutions to capitalize on green building trends.</p><p>
