</p><p><strong>Hydrochar</strong> is a carbon-rich material produced through hydrothermal carbonization (HTC), a thermochemical process that converts biomass into a solid fuel or soil amendment under elevated temperature and pressure. It serves as a sustainable alternative to conventional fossil fuels and chemical fertilizers.</p><p>
</p><p>The market encompasses hydrochar derived from various feedstocks, including sewage sludge, food waste, and animal manure. Key applications range from fuel generation to water purification, emphasizing its versatility. The product’s ability to sequester carbon and enhance soil fertility positions it as a critical component in circular economy initiatives.</p><p>
