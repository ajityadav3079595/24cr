</p><p>Fullerenes are carbon-based molecules characterized by their hollow, cage-like structures, including spherical (buckyballs) and cylindrical (carbon nanotubes) forms. These nanomaterials exhibit unique properties such as high conductivity, strength, and reactivity due to their molecular arrangement of hexagonal and pentagonal carbon rings. The most commercially prevalent types include C60 and C70 fullerenes, which find applications across diverse industries.</p><p>
