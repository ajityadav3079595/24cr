</p><p>Heat Treated Glass refers to glass that has been subjected to a thermal treatment process, such as tempering or heat strengthening, to enhance its mechanical strength and thermal resistance. The process involves heating the glass to a high temperature and then rapidly cooling it to create a toughened or strengthened surface. This makes the glass safer, as it is less likely to break into sharp shards when it fractures. Instead, heat treated glass shatters into small, rounded pieces, significantly reducing the risk of injury.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>The primary forms of heat treated glass include:</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Heat Strengthened Glass</strong>: This type of glass is heated to a lower temperature compared to fully tempered glass, making it stronger than ordinary glass but not as strong as tempered glass. It is typically used in applications where impact resistance is important but full tempering is not required.</p><p><strong>Fully Tempered Glass</strong>: This glass undergoes a higher temperature heating process and rapid cooling, resulting in a glass that is significantly stronger and more resistant to thermal stress. It is commonly used in applications such as doors, windows, and automotive glass, where strength and safety are paramount.</p><p>
</p><p>Heat treated glass finds applications across various industries, including residential and commercial construction, automotive manufacturing, and even the creation of durable and aesthetic furniture pieces.</p><p>
