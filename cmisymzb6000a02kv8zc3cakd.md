<strong>MARKET CHALLENGES</strong></p><p>

</p><p>While biodegradable adhesives offer environmental benefits, their technical limitations present substantial commercialization challenges. Current formulations often exhibit weaker bonding strength (typically 30-50% lower than synthetic adhesives) and reduced temperature resistance, making them unsuitable for demanding applications like automotive assembly or construction. The shelf life of water-based biodegradable adhesives averages just 6-12 monthsâ€”considerably shorter than conventional productsâ€”leading to supply chain complications. </p><p><strong>Other Challenges</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>High Production Costs</strong></p><p>
Biodegradable adhesives currently cost 60-80% more to manufacture than petrochemical-based alternatives. The premium stems from expensive raw materials (e.g., polylactic acid prices hover around $2.50/kg) and complex enzymatic production processes requiring sterile conditions. This price disparity makes budget-conscious industries hesitant to switch, despite environmental benefits.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Limited Feedstock Availability</strong></p><p>

The 2024 global maize shortage highlighted this risk, causing biodegradable adhesive prices to spike by 22% in Q2. Such volatility discourages long-term procurement commitments from OEMs.</p><p>

Without harmonized global standards, market growth will remain constrained by logistical complexities.</p><p>
