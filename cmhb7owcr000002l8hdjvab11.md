</p><p>Phosphorescent pigments are specialized luminescent materials that absorb and store light energy, then re-emit it gradually over time. These pigments differ from fluorescent materials by their delayed emission property, known as afterglow. The two primary types are <strong>short persistence</strong> pigments (using zinc sulfide and copper activators) and <strong>long persistence</strong> variants (based on rare-earth-doped alkaline earth aluminates or silicates).</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand across multiple applications including safety signage, textiles, and automotive coatings. Europe currently dominates with nearly 35% market share, while the top five manufacturers collectively control over 75% of global production.
