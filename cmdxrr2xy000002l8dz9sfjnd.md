</p><p>Electromagnetic (EMI) Shielding refers to the practice of reducing the electromagnetic field in a space by blocking the field with barriers made of conductive or magnetic materials. These barriers, typically composed of metal sheets, conductive coatings, foams, or enclosures, prevent electromagnetic interference (EMI) from disrupting the performance of electronic devices. EMI can be both radiated and conducted, affecting signal quality, data integrity, and device functionality. EMI shielding materials are essential in environments where multiple electronic systems operate in close proximity, such as smartphones, medical equipment, automotive systems, and aerospace technologies.</p><p>
</p><p>The need for effective EMI shielding has increased dramatically due to the proliferation of electronic devices, higher operating frequencies, and the trend toward miniaturization. Modern shielding solutions include a variety of materials and technologies, such as metal-filled polymers, conductive textiles, laminated foils, and EMI gaskets, tailored to meet the unique requirements of different industries and applications.</p><p>
