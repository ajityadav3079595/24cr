</p><p>Cyclic Olefin Polymer (COP) refers to a class of amorphous polymers derived from cyclic olefins, typically through ring-opening metathesis polymerization (ROMP) or other proprietary polymerization methods. These polymers are characterized by their excellent optical clarity, high chemical resistance, low moisture absorption, and good thermal stability. Unlike traditional polyolefins, COP exhibits a unique molecular structure that allows for customized properties based on the monomers and polymerization techniques used.</p><p>
</p><p>COPs are thermoplastics, making them suitable for processes like injection molding, extrusion, and blow molding. Their glass-like transparency and low birefringence make them ideal for optical applications, such as lenses and light guides. In the medical and pharmaceutical industries, COP is favored for drug delivery systems, diagnostic devices, and packaging due to its excellent barrier properties and biocompatibility. Its ability to withstand sterilization techniques such as gamma radiation, electron beam, and ethylene oxide further enhances its suitability in healthcare applications.</p><p>

</p><p>Cyclic Olefin Polymer (COP) refers to a class of amorphous polymers derived from cyclic olefins, typically through ring-opening metathesis polymerization (ROMP) or other proprietary polymerization methods. These polymers are characterized by their excellent optical clarity, high chemical resistance, low moisture absorption, and good thermal stability. Unlike traditional polyolefins, COP exhibits a unique molecular structure that allows for customized properties based on the monomers and polymerization techniques used.</p><p>
</p><p>COPs are thermoplastics, making them suitable for processes like injection molding, extrusion, and blow molding. Their glass-like transparency and low birefringence make them ideal for optical applications, such as lenses and light guides. In the medical and pharmaceutical industries, COP is favored for drug delivery systems, diagnostic devices, and packaging due to its excellent barrier properties and biocompatibility. Its ability to withstand sterilization techniques such as gamma radiation, electron beam, and ethylene oxide further enhances its suitability in healthcare applications.</p><p>
