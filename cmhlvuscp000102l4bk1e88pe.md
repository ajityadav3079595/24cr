</p><p>Dehydrated agar is a gelatinous substance extracted from red algae, widely used as a gelling agent across multiple industries. This natural hydrocolloid possesses unique thermoreversible properties - forming gels at low temperatures while remaining soluble in hot water. The product is commercially available in two primary forms: powder and tablets, with powder dominating market share due to easier handling and faster dissolution properties.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by expanding applications in food processing, particularly in bakery and confectionery products, where it serves as a vegetarian alternative to gelatin. Furthermore, increasing pharmaceutical R&amp;D activities and microbiology testing requirements are creating sustained demand, as agar remains the gold standard medium for microbial culture. The cosmetic industry's shift toward natural ingredients has also opened new growth avenues for agar-based formulations in skincare products. While Asia-Pacific currently leads production and consumption, North America and Europe are witnessing accelerated adoption rates, particularly in organic and clean-label food products.</p><p>
