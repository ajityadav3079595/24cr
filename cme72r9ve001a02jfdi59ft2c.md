<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Environmental Concerns and Regulatory Phase-outs to Constrain Market Growth</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The R23 refrigerant market faces significant challenges due to its classification as a high global warming potential (GWP) substance. With a GWP of 14,800, R23 is considered one of the most potent greenhouse gases, making it a target for international environmental agreements. The Kigali Amendment to the Montreal Protocol specifically targets the gradual reduction of HFCs, including R23. While exemptions exist for certain medical and industrial applications, the overall trend toward phase-down creates uncertainty in the market.</p><p>
</p><p>Compliance with these environmental regulations requires substantial investment in alternative technologies and process modifications. Many end-users are beginning to transition to lower-GWP alternatives, which may reduce long-term demand for R23 despite its superior performance in specific applications. This regulatory pressure represents a persistent challenge to market growth.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Supply Chain Volatility and Raw Material Constraints</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The R23 market is experiencing supply chain challenges stemming from raw material availability and geopolitical factors. Fluorspar, a key raw material for R23 production, has seen fluctuating prices and supply disruptions. Recent trade policies and mining restrictions in major producing countries have created uncertainties in the supply chain, affecting production costs and lead times.</p><p>
</p><p>Additionally, the specialized equipment required for R23 handling and storage presents another market restraint. The need for trained technicians and certified handling procedures adds to operational costs, making R23 systems more expensive to install and maintain compared to conventional refrigerants. These factors collectively restrain market expansion despite strong demand from niche applications.</p><p>
