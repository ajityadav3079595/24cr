</p><p>Green petroleum coke is a high-carbon byproduct of oil refining, primarily used as a fuel source and raw material in industrial applications. It contains 80-95% carbon content and is categorized into anode grade (used in aluminum production), fuel grade (for power generation), and specialty grades. The material's high calorific value and low ash content make it particularly valuable for energy-intensive industries across Latin America.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is being driven by increasing aluminum production in Brazil, expansion of steel manufacturing in Mexico, and growing energy demands across the region.
