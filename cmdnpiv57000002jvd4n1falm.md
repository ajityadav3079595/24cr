</p><p>Closed cell polyurethane foam is a versatile material characterized by its cellular structure where individual cells are completely enclosed, making it impermeable to water and air. This lightweight yet durable material offers excellent thermal insulation, buoyancy, and structural support properties. The two primary types are flexible foam (used in cushioning applications) and rigid foam (used in insulation and structural components).</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is being driven by increasing construction activities, where polyurethane foam is extensively used for building insulation to meet energy efficiency standards. The material's superior R-value (thermal resistance) of 6.5 per inch makes it ideal for reducing energy consumption in residential and commercial buildings. Furthermore, rising demand from automotive manufacturers for lightweight components and growing applications in refrigeration and cold chain logistics are contributing to market expansion. Key players like BASF SE, Dow Chemical Company, and Huntsman Corporation are investing in sustainable formulations to meet stringent environmental regulations.</p><p>
