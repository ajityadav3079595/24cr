</p><p>Hafnium(IV) oxide, also known as hafnia, is an inorganic compound with the chemical formula HfO2. It appears as a colorless, odorless solid and is considered one of the most stable and prevalent compounds of hafnium. Notably, hafnium oxide is recognized for its high dielectric constant and large band gap (approximately 6 eV), making it an ideal material for insulating layers in high-k dielectric applications in semiconductors.</p><p>
</p><p>Due to its robust thermal and chemical stability, HfO2 is extensively used in nuclear reactors as a control material, as well as in refractory coatings that must endure extreme temperatures and corrosive environments. Moreover, its resistance to radiation and high-temperature degradation has expanded its use in aerospace coatings and other technologically demanding fields.</p><p>
