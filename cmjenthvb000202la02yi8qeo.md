</p><p>ADCA (Azodicarbonamide) is a chemical blowing agent primarily used in polymer processing to create lightweight cellular structures in plastics, rubber, and synthetic leather applications. It decomposes at high temperatures to release nitrogen, carbon monoxide, and other gases that form uniform foam structures. The material finds extensive use in industries such as footwear, automotive, packaging, and construction for producing expanded PVC, EVA foam, and synthetic leather products.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand for lightweight materials in automotive components and rising consumption of foamed plastics in construction insulation. However, regulatory restrictions in some countries due to potential health concerns pose challenges to market expansion. Key manufacturers are focusing on developing modified ADCA formulations with improved thermal stability and reduced decomposition byproducts to address these concerns while maintaining performance characteristics.</p><p>
