</p><p>While micro copper coated steel fibers offer superior performance, their adoption faces notable barriers. The production process - involving precision copper plating of micro steel fibers - results in costs 40-50% higher than conventional steel reinforcement. This premium pricing limits use primarily to high-value projects despite the long-term cost savings. </p><p>The technical complexity extends to material handling as well. Micro fibers require careful batching procedures to prevent clumping, with improper mixing reducing performance by up to 30%. These operational challenges have slowed adoption among smaller contractors and in price-sensitive developing markets where cost considerations often outweigh long-term durability benefits.</p><p>
