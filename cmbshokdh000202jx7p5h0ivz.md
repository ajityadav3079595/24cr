</p><p>Synthetic activated carbon is a highly porous material produced from carbon-rich sources such as coal, coconut shells, and wood. It is widely used for adsorption, purification, and filtration applications due to its large surface area and high adsorption capacity. The material is processed through thermal or chemical activation to enhance its porosity, making it effective in removing impurities from gases, liquids, and industrial effluents. Key applications include <strong>gas adsorption, decolorization, and removal of contaminants</strong> in water and air purification systems.
