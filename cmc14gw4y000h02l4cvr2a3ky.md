</p><p><strong>Heat Dissipation Paste (HD Paste)</strong>, also referred to as <strong>thermal interface material (TIM)</strong>, is a highly conductive compound applied between heat-generating components (such as CPUs, GPUs, LED chips) and their cooling systems (like heat sinks or spreaders). Its primary purpose is to <strong>fill microscopic air gaps</strong> that naturally occur between two solid surfaces, which would otherwise act as insulators, preventing efficient heat transfer.</p><p>
</p><p>HD pastes are engineered to possess <strong>high thermal conductivity</strong> while offering low thermal resistance, ensuring that heat flows efficiently from the device into the cooling hardware. They are crucial in environments where reliable thermal management is mandatory to maintain operational efficiency and avoid component failure. There are two main types of HD paste: <strong>Silicon-based</strong> and <strong>Silicon-free</strong>, each offering specific performance benefits depending on the application requirements.</p><p>
