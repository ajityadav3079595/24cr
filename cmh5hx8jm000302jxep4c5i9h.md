</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Supply Chain Vulnerabilities and Raw Material Price Volatility to Limit Growth</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Xanthohumol production faces significant challenges due to its dependence on hops cultivation, which is concentrated in specific geographic regions. The compound's low natural concentration in hops (typically 0.1-1% of dry weight) necessitates large-scale processing, increasing production costs substantially. These factors contribute to price volatility, with xanthohumol prices ranging between $1,500-$3,000 per kilogram in 2024.</p><p>

</p><p>Despite xanthohumol's therapeutic potential, regulatory barriers in major markets present significant hurdles. The FDA's Generally Recognized as Safe (GRAS) status for xanthohumol remains pending, affecting product formulation strategies for U.S. food and beverage manufacturers.</p><p>
