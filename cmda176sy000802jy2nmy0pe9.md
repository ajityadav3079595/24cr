</p><p>Oxidized Polyethylene Wax (OPE Wax) is a type of modified polyethylene wax that features polar functional groups, such as hydroxyl and carboxyl groups, introduced through an oxidation process. This chemical alteration imparts unique properties to the wax, such as improved compatibility with polar materials, enhanced dispersibility in various matrices, and better adhesion characteristics. Unlike traditional polyethylene wax, which is non-polar, OPE wax can interact effectively with polar substances, making it a valuable additive in industries like plastics, coatings, inks, textiles, rubber, and adhesives.</p><p>
</p><p>The oxidation process involves exposing polyethylene wax to oxidizing agents like oxygen or air at elevated temperatures, which results in a product with varied acid numbers and melt viscosities depending on the degree of oxidation. The result is a wax that provides better lubrication, slip, and anti-blocking properties, while also acting as a dispersing agent and coupling agent. These traits make oxidized polyethylene wax particularly important in applications such as PVC processing, masterbatches, and emulsion formulations.</p><p>
