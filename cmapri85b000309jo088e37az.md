<strong>Carbon electrodes</strong> are conductive materials made primarily from raw materials such as <strong>anthracite, petroleum coke, and pitch</strong>, used in a range of high-temperature industrial processes. Unlike graphite electrodes, carbon electrodes <strong>do not require graphitization</strong>, making their <strong>production more cost-effective</strong>. These electrodes are essential components in <strong>electric arc furnaces (EAF)</strong> for steelmaking and are also increasingly used in <strong>renewable energy applications</strong>, especially in energy storage systems.</p><p>
