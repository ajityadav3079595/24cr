 <p><span style="font-size:14px">Polyethylene Imine (PEI) is a synthetic polymer characterized by repeating units of amine groups and aliphatic ethylene spacers. It is commercially available in both linear and branched forms. Linear PEI consists exclusively of secondary amines, while branched PEI contains a mix of primary, secondary, and tertiary amines. Dendrimeric forms, which are highly branched and exhibit well-defined molecular structures, have also been developed.</span></p>
