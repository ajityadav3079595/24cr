</p><p>Electrically heated glass is an advanced glazing solution that incorporates conductive coatings or wires between glass layers to generate uniform heat when powered. This smart glass technology consists of two tempered glass panels with a transparent conductive oxide (TCO) coating, typically indium tin oxide (ITO), sandwiched between them. When connected to a power source, electrical resistance heating occurs, making it ideal for applications requiring both transparency and thermal control.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by increasing demand from the construction sector for energy-efficient solutions, particularly in cold climates where traditional heating systems struggle with heat loss through windows. Furthermore, stringent building energy codes and the rising adoption of smart glass technologies in automotive applications are accelerating market expansion. Recent innovations include self-regulating glass systems that adjust heating based on external temperatures, with major manufacturers like Saint-Gobain and NSG Group investing heavily in R&amp;D to improve efficiency and reduce power consumption.</p><p>
