</p><p>Buffered lactic acid is a pH-stabilized form of lactic acid, commonly used as an acidity regulator and preservative across multiple industries. This compound is created by neutralizing lactic acid with buffering agents like sodium lactate or potassium lactate, making it less corrosive while maintaining its antimicrobial properties. The product finds extensive applications in food &amp; beverages, pharmaceuticals, and personal care formulations due to its ability to enhance shelf life and product stability.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by increasing demand for natural preservatives in processed foods and the expanding pharmaceutical industry where buffered lactic acid serves as an excipient. While North America currently dominates the market, Asia-Pacific is witnessing accelerated growth due to rapid industrialization of food processing sectors. Major manufacturers including Jungbunzlauer and Corbion are expanding their production capacities to meet this rising demand, particularly for high-purity (over 99%) grades used in pharmaceutical applications.</p><p>
