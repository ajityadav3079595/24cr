</p><p>Cannabidiol (CBD) is a non-psychoactive phytocannabinoid derived from the Cannabis sativa plant, increasingly recognized for its therapeutic potential across multiple industries. Unlike THC (tetrahydrocannabinol), CBD does not induce intoxication, making it suitable for pharmaceutical formulations, wellness products, and consumer goods. The compound interacts with the endocannabinoid system, influencing physiological processes like pain perception, inflammation, and anxiety regulation.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by expanding legalization of hemp-derived products, rising consumer demand for natural remedies, and increasing clinical validation of CBD's medical benefits. While North America currently dominates market share due to progressive regulatory frameworks, Europe and Asia-Pacific are emerging as high-growth regions. Key product segments include oils, tinctures, capsules, and topical formulations, with applications spanning pharmaceuticals, food &amp; beverages, cosmetics, and pet care.</p><p>
