</p><p>Deuterated solvents, also known as heavy solvents, are chemical compounds in which one or more hydrogen atoms have been replaced with deuterium, a stable isotope of hydrogen. These solvents are primarily used in nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) spectroscopy due to their low background interference in proton NMR experiments. Common deuterated solvents include deuterated chloroform (CDCl3), deuterated dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO-d6), deuterated methanol (CD3OD), and deuterated ethanol (C2D5OD). The demand for deuterated chloroform dominates the market, accounting for over half of global usage.</p><p>
</p><p>Deuterated solvents are indispensable in analytical chemistry, pharmaceuticals, and academic research due to their unique isotopic properties. Their importance in spectroscopic techniques makes them a critical component in qualitative and quantitative chemical analysis. In recent years, the market has seen increasing demand driven by advancements in drug discovery, metabolomics, and structural biology.</p><p>
