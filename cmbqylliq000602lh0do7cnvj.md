</p><p>Graft Polyols, also known as polymer polyols, are specialized polyether polyols created through a process called grafting. In this process, acrylonitrile and styrene monomers are grafted onto conventional polyether polyols. The result is a polymer that exhibits enhanced properties such as improved stability, durability, and resistance to environmental factors.</p><p>
</p><p>These modified polyols are primarily used in the production of rigid and flexible foams, adhesives, sealants, coatings, and other applications where enhanced material performance is required. Graft Polyols offer significant advantages over traditional polyols, such as improved processing, better mechanical properties, and enhanced compatibility with other materials.</p><p>
</p><p>The key applications of Graft Polyols include the production of polyurethane foams, which are used in a wide variety of industries, including automotive, construction, and consumer goods. These polyols are also used in the manufacturing of coatings and adhesives that require high strength, flexibility, and resistance to harsh conditions.</p><p>
