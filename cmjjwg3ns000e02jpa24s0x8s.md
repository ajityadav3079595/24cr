</p><p>High purity tellurium oxide (TeO2) is a highly refined chemical compound composed of tellurium and oxygen, with purity levels typically exceeding 99.99%. It appears as a white or yellowish crystalline powder with a tetragonal crystal structure. This compound is classified as a semiconductor, featuring a wide bandgap of 3.6 eV and a high refractive index of 2.26, making it particularly valuable in optical and electronic applications.</p><p>
</p><p>Due to its distinctive physical and chemical properties, high purity tellurium oxide is extensively used in high-precision domains, including semiconductors, acousto-optic devices, and laser systems. Its ability to serve as a transparent conductive oxide (TCO) layer and as a material for preserving certain chemical formulations further broadens its scope of application.</p><p>
</p><p>As industries increasingly demand purer and more reliable raw materials for high-performance products, tellurium oxide of high purity has become a crucial component in the global materials market.</p><p>
