</p><p>Geopolymers are inorganic polymers developed nearly three decades ago by Joseph Davidovits, formed through the chemical reaction of aluminosilicate materials in alkaline environments at ambient temperatures. These materials utilize precursors like metakaolin or fly ash along with alkali activators to create binders that can replace ordinary Portland cement (OPC). Geopolymers offer significant environmental advantages, including an 80-90% reduction in CO2 emissions compared to traditional cement, along with enhanced resistance to fire and corrosive chemicals.</p><p>
