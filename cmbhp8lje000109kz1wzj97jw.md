</p><p><strong>Gypenosides</strong> are a group of dammarane-type saponins primarily found in the Gynostemma pentaphyllum plant, also known as Jiaogulan or "Southern Ginseng." Belonging to the saponin family, gypenosides exhibit numerous pharmacological activities, including <strong>antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, hepatoprotective, neuroprotective, anti-cancer, and cholesterol-lowering</strong> effects.</p><p>
</p><p>Used extensively in traditional Chinese medicine, gypenosides are recognized for their adaptogenic properties, which help the body resist physical, chemical, and biological stress. In modern pharmacological research, these compounds are being explored for their potential in managing chronic illnesses such as cardiovascular diseases, metabolic disorders, and even certain cancers.</p><p>
</p><p>Gypenosides are typically extracted and purified in concentrations ranging from 30% to 98%, and are formulated into powders, capsules, or liquid extracts. They are used in pharmaceuticals, research studies, dietary supplements, and functional beverages across various global markets.</p><p>
