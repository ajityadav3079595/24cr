<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Health Concerns Related to Fiber Inhalation to Limit Market Expansion</strong></p><p>
</p><p>While aluminum silicate fibers offer excellent thermal properties, potential health risks associated with fiber inhalation during installation and handling present significant market challenges. Studies have shown that prolonged exposure to certain high-temperature fibers may pose respiratory risks, leading to stringent workplace safety regulations in developed markets. The OSHA permissible exposure limit (PEL) of 1 fiber per cubic centimeter has increased compliance costs for end-users, particularly in North America and Europe.</p><p>

</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Technical Limitations in Extreme Conditions to Constrain Applications</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Although aluminum silicate fibers perform exceptionally well in most high-temperature environments, their application faces limitations in extreme conditions. The material shows reduced durability when exposed to temperatures exceeding 1600°C or in highly corrosive atmospheres found in certain chemical processes. This restricts adoption in advanced aerospace applications and specialized chemical reactors where temperatures can surpass material limitations. The ceramic fiber industry reports approximately 20% replacement rate within 3-5 years in such extreme environments.</p><p>
