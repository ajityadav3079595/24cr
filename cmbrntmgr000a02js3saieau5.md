</p><p>High Purity Oxygen refers to oxygen gas with a purity level typically exceeding 99.5%, and often reaching levels as high as 99.999% depending on the application. It is produced through advanced separation techniques such as cryogenic distillation, pressure swing adsorption (PSA), or vacuum pressure swing adsorption (VPSA). High purity oxygen plays a vital role in industries requiring stringent quality standards such as electronics manufacturing, metallurgy, healthcare, semiconductors, and chemical processing.</p><p>
</p><p>Its ultra-pure composition ensures that impurities like nitrogen, carbon dioxide, and hydrocarbons are present in negligible quantities. This level of purity is essential in processes where even minor contamination can lead to adverse outcomes, such as oxidation defects in semiconductor wafers or impurities in metallurgical refinement. Thus, high purity oxygen serves as a critical enabler in high-precision industrial applications.</p><p>
