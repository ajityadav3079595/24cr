</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>High Production Costs and Complex Formulation Challenges Limit Market Penetration</strong></p><p>
</p><p>While electric conductivity dyes offer significant performance advantages, their widespread adoption faces obstacles due to complex manufacturing processes and premium pricing. These high costs make conductivity dyes economically unfeasible for many price-sensitive applications, particularly in developing markets. Additionally, achieving consistent batch-to-batch performance requires precise control over molecular structure and doping levels, creating technical barriers for new market entrants.</p><p>
