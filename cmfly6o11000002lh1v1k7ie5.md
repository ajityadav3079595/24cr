These structural variations give rise to different subtypes, most notably <strong>α-carboline</strong> and <strong>β-carboline</strong>, which exhibit distinct biological activities. The α-carboline subtype is formed through a 3-2 linkage, while β-carboline results from a 2-3 fusion pattern. This structural diversity enables broad applications in pharmaceuticals, particularly for neurological and anticancer therapies, as well as specialized chemical synthesis.</p><p>
