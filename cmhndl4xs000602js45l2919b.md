</p><p><strong>High Purity Fused Quartz</strong> is a specialized form of glass made almost entirely of silica (SiO2), offering exceptional purity levels. It is produced by fusing (melting) high-purity silica sand, which contains quartz crystals. Unlike traditional commercial glasses, which include various additives, fused quartz has an amorphous structure. This provides distinct characteristics, such as high optical clarity, high melting point, and excellent resistance to thermal shock and chemical corrosion.</p><p>
</p><p>The material is primarily used in demanding applications that require transparency, high thermal resistance, and minimal contamination. These include industries such as semiconductor manufacturing, solar power, optical systems, and fiber optics. Its unique properties distinguish it from other forms of glass, particularly in the ability to withstand high temperatures and maintain performance in extreme conditions.</p><p>
