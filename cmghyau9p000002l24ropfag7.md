</p><p><strong>Sanitizers and Disinfectants for Food Contact Surfaces</strong> refer to chemical agents specifically formulated to reduce or eliminate microbial contamination on surfaces that come into direct contact with food products. These surfaces include kitchen countertops, cutting boards, industrial food processing equipment, utensils, and storage areas. The primary goal of sanitization and disinfection in food-related environments is to uphold hygiene standards that prevent foodborne illnesses caused by harmful microorganisms such as E. coli, Salmonella, and Listeria monocytogenes.</p><p>
</p><p>Sanitizers are typically used to reduce bacteria to safe levels, while disinfectants go a step further by destroying a broader range of pathogens, including viruses and fungi. Both types of products must be approved for food contact by relevant health authorities (e.g., U.S. EPA, FDA, EFSA) and must not leave harmful residues. Effective sanitization and disinfection protocols are an integral part of Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP) systems widely adopted across the food industry.</p><p>
