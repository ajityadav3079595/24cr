These tubes leverage quartz's unique properties including exceptional thermal shock resistance (withstanding temperatures up to 1200°C), ultra-low thermal expansion (0.55 × 10-6/K), and superior chemical inertness against most acids and halogens. They serve as critical components in semiconductor wafer processing, optical fiber production, and specialized metallurgical applications.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by multiple factors, particularly the <strong>semiconductor industry expansion</strong> which accounted for 42% of total demand in 2024. However, supply chain constraints for high-purity quartz sand and the complex manufacturing process (requiring 99.99% SiO2 content) present challenges.
