</p><p>Toluene Diisocyanate (TDI) is an aromatic organic compound with the chemical formula CH3C6H3(NCO)2, primarily used in polyurethane production. When its isocyanate groups react with polyols, they form durable urethane linkages, making TDI essential for manufacturing flexible foams, coatings, adhesives, and elastomers. The compound exists in two isomeric forms: 2,4-TDI and 2,6-TDI, with the former being more commercially prevalent.</p><p>
