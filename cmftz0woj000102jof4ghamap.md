</p><p>Tetrabromophthalic Anhydride (TBPA) is a brominated flame retardant chemical widely used in polymer applications. This white crystalline powder serves as a reactive intermediate in epoxy resins, unsaturated polyesters, and other high-temperature polymers, providing exceptional flame retardancy while maintaining material performance. The compound's effectiveness stems from its ability to release bromine radicals that interrupt combustion reactions.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by stringent fire safety regulations across construction and electronics sectors, particularly in Brazil where building codes mandate flame-retardant materials. However, environmental concerns regarding brominated compounds have prompted manufacturers to invest in eco-friendly alternatives, creating both challenges and opportunities.
