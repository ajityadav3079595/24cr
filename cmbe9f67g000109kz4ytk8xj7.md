<strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Enanthic acid, also referred to as heptanoic acid, is an organic compound characterized by a seven-carbon chain ending in a carboxylic acid functional group. It is an oily liquid with a distinct, rancid odor and is slightly soluble in water but highly soluble in ethanol and ether. The compound plays a crucial role in various industries due to its unique properties and applications.</p><p>
