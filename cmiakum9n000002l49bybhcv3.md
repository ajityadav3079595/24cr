</p><p>The corrugated fanfold market faces significant pressure from fluctuating pulp and kraft paper prices, which accounted for 60-65% of total production costs in 2024. After reaching record highs in 2022, pulp prices remain unstable due to geopolitical factors, transportation constraints, and energy cost volatility. Many manufacturers have been forced to implement 5-7 price increases since 2020, with some regional markets seeing cumulative price hikes exceeding 45%. This pricing pressure has made it particularly challenging for small and medium packaging converters to maintain profitability while meeting customer pricing expectations.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Other Challenges</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Limited Automation Compatibility</strong></p><p>
While fanfold offers processing efficiencies, many existing packaging lines require significant retrofitting (costing $250,000-$500,000 per line) to handle continuous fanfold feeds rather than single sheets. This capital investment requirement has slowed adoption among smaller manufacturers.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Moisture Sensitivity</strong></p><p>
Standard fanfold boards lose up to 50% of their compression strength when exposed to high humidity, limiting use in certain climates without specialized coatings that add 20-30% to material costs.</p><p>
