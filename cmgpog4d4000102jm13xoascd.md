</p><p>Melamine faced panels are engineered wood products consisting of a decorative melamine-impregnated paper layer bonded to a substrate (typically MDF or particleboard). These panels are widely used in interior applications due to their durability, aesthetic versatility, and cost-effectiveness. Key product segments include <strong>below 18 mm</strong>, <strong>18-25 mm</strong>, and <strong>above 25 mm</strong> thickness variants, catering to diverse construction and furniture requirements.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by rising demand in residential and commercial construction, particularly for applications like <strong>furniture</strong>, <strong>kitchen cabinetry</strong>, and <strong>wall panels</strong>. Leading manufacturers including <strong>Kronospan</strong>, <strong>Egger Group</strong>, and <strong>Swiss Krono</strong> are expanding production capacities to meet the growing global demand, particularly for fire-resistant and eco-friendly variants.</p><p>
