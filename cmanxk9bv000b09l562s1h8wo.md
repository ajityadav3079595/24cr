<strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Fluorinated lubricants are special grade synthetic lubricants with superior properties compared to petroleum-based lubricants. They can operate within a wide temperature range from -90°C to 300°C and offer significant endurance under high load conditions. Key properties include hydrophobic nature, chemical inertness, and oxidation resistance. Fluorinated lubricants exist in both oil and grease forms and are widely used in various industries due to their exceptional performance.</p><p>
