</p><p>Air detraining admixtures are chemical additives used in concrete mixtures to reduce or eliminate unwanted air entrainment. These specialized formulations enhance the durability and workability of concrete by controlling pore structure, which is critical for applications requiring high strength and resistance to freeze-thaw cycles. The two primary types include dry powdered and liquid air detraining admixtures, each tailored for specific construction requirements.</p><p>
</p><p>Market growth is driven by increasing infrastructure development, particularly in emerging economies, and rising demand for high-performance concrete in commercial and residential construction.
