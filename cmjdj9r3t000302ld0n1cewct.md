</p><p>Self-heating food packaging refers to innovative containers that utilize exothermic chemical reactions to warm food without external heat sources. These packages typically incorporate compartments for water and heating agents like calcium oxide or magnesium, which activate upon mixing. The technology finds applications across diverse food categories including soups, noodles, ready-to-eat meals, and beverages like coffee and tea.</p><p>
</p><p>While the market shows promising growth, challenges exist in developing cost-effective and environmentally sustainable heating mechanisms. However, recent innovations in biodegradable materials and smart packaging integration are creating new opportunities.
