</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Expansion of Pharmaceutical Applications to Accelerate Adoption</strong></p><p>

<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Stringent Environmental Regulations to Limit Market Expansion</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The market faces significant challenges due to increasing regulatory scrutiny of mercury compounds worldwide. Over 128 countries have now ratified the Minamata Convention on Mercury, which imposes strict controls on mercury-containing products. In the European Union, the use of phenylmercuric acetate has been banned in most consumer applications since 2020, with similar restrictions being implemented in North America. These regulatory developments are compelling manufacturers to invest heavily in reformulation efforts, with estimated R&amp;D costs for mercury-free alternatives ranging between $2-5 million per product line.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Growing Health Concerns to Impact Consumer Acceptance</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Mounting evidence regarding mercury toxicity is creating substantial barriers to market growth. Studies have shown that chronic exposure to mercury compounds can lead to neurological disorders, with the WHO estimating that over 1 million workers globally face occupational mercury exposure risks. This has led to increasing consumer preference for mercury-free products across all applications. In the pharmaceutical sector alone, over 65% of new ophthalmic formulations launched in 2024 were preservative-free or used alternative antimicrobial systems.</p><p>
