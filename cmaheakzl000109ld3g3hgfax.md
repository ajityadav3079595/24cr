</p><p>High Purity Zinc Oxide (HPZO) refers to a highly refined form of zinc oxide that contains minimal impurities. Typically classified in purity grades such as 4N (99.99%) and 5N (99.999%), HPZO is distinguished by its high transparency, elevated refractive index, and excellent electrical and thermal conductivity. These attributes make it particularly valuable in applications that demand precision and performance.</p><p>
</p><p>Unlike standard zinc oxide, HPZO undergoes stringent purification processes such as sublimation, hydrothermal synthesis, or vapor-phase transport to achieve these high purity levels. The result is a product suitable for use in sensitive and high-precision industries such as:</p><p>
</p><p>Electronic semiconductors</p><p>Optical coatings</p><p>Pharmaceuticals</p><p>High-grade ceramics</p><p>Specialty chemicals</p><p>
</p><p>This enhanced performance profile has led HPZO to become an essential material in technological innovations, especially where defect-free and high-performance materials are mandatory.</p><p>
