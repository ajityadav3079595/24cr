</p><p>High Purity Hafnium refers to hafnium metal with exceptionally low impurity levels, typically above 99.9% purity (0.999) and in some cases even 99.99% (0.9999). Hafnium, with the atomic number 72 and atomic weight 178.49, is a transition metal known for its lustrous silver-gray appearance and outstanding corrosion resistance.</p><p>
</p><p>The element is chemically inert to most acids except hydrofluoric acid and aqua regia, making it ideal for critical industrial applications. High purity hafnium is used primarily in control rods of nuclear reactors due to its excellent neutron absorption capability. Moreover, its high melting point, robust mechanical strength, and resistance to oxidation make it indispensable in aerospace turbine blades, high-temperature ceramics, and advanced semiconductor manufacturing.</p><p>
</p><p>This market is characterized by a limited number of suppliers due to the complex and costly purification processes required. The demand for high purity hafnium is growing as industries increasingly favor high-performance materials that deliver long-term reliability in extreme environments.</p><p>
