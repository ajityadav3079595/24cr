</p><p>Croscarmellose sodium is an internally cross-linked sodium carboxymethylcellulose primarily used as a superdisintegrant in pharmaceutical formulations. Classified as E468 in food applications, this modified cellulose derivative accelerates tablet disintegration through rapid water absorption and swelling mechanisms. Its superior performance over traditional disintegrants has made it increasingly popular in solid dosage formulations.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by expanding pharmaceutical production, particularly in generic drugs, coupled with rising demand for fast-acting oral medications.
