</p><p>Methyl orthoformate, also known as trimethoxymethane (CAS Number 149-73-5), is the simplest orthoester primarily used as a reagent in organic synthesis. It serves as a protecting group for aldehydes, forming acetals that can later be deprotected using hydrochloric acid. Industrially, it is synthesized from hydrogen cyanide and methanol. This versatile chemical finds significant applications across pesticides, pharmaceuticals, and specialty chemicals.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing demand from the agricultural sector, where methyl orthoformate is a key intermediate in pesticide production, accounting for 64% of total consumption. China dominates global production with over 95% market share, with Yingkou Cynovate Chemical leading as the largest producer (24% market share). While the hydrocyanic acid method remains the predominant manufacturing process (73% share), technological advancements in production efficiency continue to shape the competitive landscape.</p><p>
