<strong>Market Size </strong></p><p>
</p><p>Chlortetracycline is a broad-spectrum antibiotic that belongs to the tetracycline class of antimicrobials. It is derived from Streptomyces aureofaciens and is widely used in veterinary medicine and agriculture to prevent and treat bacterial infections in animals. This compound functions by inhibiting protein synthesis in bacteria, thereby halting their growth and proliferation. Chlortetracycline is utilized extensively in animal feed as a growth promoter and for disease prevention in livestock, especially in poultry, swine, and cattle industries. Additionally, while its application in human medicine has reduced due to concerns over antibiotic resistance, it remains relevant in niche pharmaceutical uses.</p><p>
</p><p>Chlortetracycline is available in various forms including powder, granules, tablets, and injectables. The product's effectiveness, low cost, and ease of incorporation into animal feed make it a staple in animal husbandry globally. However, regulatory scrutiny and evolving resistance patterns have led to varying levels of adoption and restrictions across different regions.</p><p>
