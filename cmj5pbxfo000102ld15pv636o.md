</p><p>Cement boards are composite materials made from cement and reinforcing fibers, engineered into sheets of varying thicknesses. Primarily used as tile backing boards in construction, these versatile products also find applications in flooring, wall systems, and even high-temperature industrial settings like foundries and metal processing plants. The boards offer superior durability, moisture resistance, and fireproofing capabilities compared to traditional building materials.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by increasing construction activities worldwide, particularly in commercial building projects which account for 63% of total demand. While fiber cement boards dominate the product segment with 70% market share, innovations in lightweight cement-bonded particle boards are gaining traction. North America currently leads regional adoption with 35% market share, though Asia-Pacific is expected to witness accelerated growth due to rapid urbanization and infrastructure development. <strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>High Production Costs and Energy-Intensive Manufacturing to Limit Market Expansion</strong></p><p>

Regional shortages of key raw materials have led to extended lead times, with some manufacturers reporting 6-8 week delivery delays in 2023. These disruptions are particularly challenging for large-scale commercial projects with tight construction schedules.</p><p>

</p><p>Emerging engineered wood products and advanced gypsum board formulations are gaining ground in the construction market. The gypsum board market, valued at nearly triple the cement board sector, continues to improve its moisture-resistant formulations, challenging one of cement board's key advantages.</p><p>
