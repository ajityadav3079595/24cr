The material finds extensive applications in thermal management solutions across industries including petrochemical, mechanical instrumentation, and communication equipment.</p><p>

Nearly 12-18% of material is wasted during typical fabrication processes due to these handling difficulties. </p><p>The market faces growing competition from emerging thermal interface materials including graphene-based films and advanced thermal pads. While artificial graphite paper maintains advantages in anisotropic heat spreading, newer materials offer benefits in ease of handling and isotropic thermal transfer. Synthetic diamond films are emerging as particularly disruptive competitors in high-end applications, offering thermal conductivity exceeding 2000 W/mK.
