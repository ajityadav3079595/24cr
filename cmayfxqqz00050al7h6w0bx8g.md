</p><p>Graphite Carbon Bricks are high-temperature resistant neutral refractory products primarily composed of carbon. These bricks are manufactured by using raw materials such as anthracite, coke, graphite, along with binders like asphalt, tar, and anthracene oil. Anthracite, with its dense structure and low volatile matter, is frequently used as an aggregate in the production process. The binders provide the necessary bonding to form the brick structure and improve its heat resistance.</p><p>
</p><p>Graphite Carbon Bricks are primarily utilized in industries that require materials capable of withstanding high temperatures without degrading. Key applications include steelmaking furnaces, petrochemical industries, and chemical reactors. These bricks are particularly effective in environments where high thermal stability, corrosion resistance, and mechanical strength are critical.</p><p>
