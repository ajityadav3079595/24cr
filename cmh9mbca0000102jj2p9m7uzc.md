</p><p>n-Butyl Chloride, also known as 1-Chlorobutane, is a colorless liquid with a characteristic odor, primarily used as an intermediate in organic synthesis. It is widely employed in the pharmaceutical industry for the production of active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs) and as a solvent in various chemical reactions. Additionally, it serves as a catalyst in industrial applications, including the synthesis of rubber and plastic additives. The compound is produced through the chlorination of n-butane or by reacting butanol with hydrochloric acid. Its high reactivity and versatility make it a crucial component in multiple industrial and pharmaceutical processes.</p><p>
