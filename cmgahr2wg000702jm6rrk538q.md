</p><p>Sodium paratungstate is a high-density inorganic compound composed of tungsten, oxygen, sodium, and hydrogen. Its unique polyoxometalate structure features a cluster of 12 tungsten atoms and 40 oxygen atoms combined with sodium ions, giving it exceptional water solubility with densities reaching 2.9 g/cm³. This characteristic makes it valuable across industrial applications where high-density solutions are required. The compound maintains stability at room temperature and demonstrates lower toxicity compared to alternatives like cesium chloride, expanding its suitability for specialized applications including biomedical uses.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is primarily driven by increasing demand from catalyst manufacturing and electroplating industries, where sodium paratungstate's properties enhance process efficiency.
