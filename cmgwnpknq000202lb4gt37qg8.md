<strong>MARKET CHALLENGES</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Technological Lag in Processing Efficiency Limits Yield Improvements</strong></p><p>
</p><p>While sweet potato starch offers superior functional properties, its extraction efficiency trails competing starches by 18-22% due to technical limitations in processing methodologies. Conventional wet milling techniques recover only about 75% of available starch from sweet potatoes, compared to 90-93% recovery rates for corn starch. This yield gap substantially impacts production economics, with energy costs per ton of starch produced running 30% higher than corn starch operations. The industry has yet to adopt advanced separation technologies like three-phase decanter centrifuges that could potentially increase yields by 10-12 percentage points.</p><p>
</p><p>Furthermore, residual protein removal remains problematic, requiring multiple washing cycles that increase water usage to approximately 15 cubic meters per ton of starch - nearly triple that of potato starch processing.</p><p>
