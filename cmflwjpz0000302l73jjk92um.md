</p><p>Detonation synthesis nanodiamond powder, also known as ultradisperse diamonds (UDD), are carbon nanoparticles produced through explosive detonation processes. These materials exhibit unique properties including <strong>high thermal conductivity, exceptional hardness, and large specific surface area</strong>, making them valuable for applications in polishing compositions, lubricants, and composite materials. The particles typically range between 10-100nm in size, with varying grades optimized for different industrial uses.</p><p>

</p><p>The global detonation synthesis nanodiamond powder market is experiencing significant growth due to rising demand from precision polishing applications across multiple industries. </p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Standardization Issues Hindering Market Development</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The lack of universally accepted quality standards for nanodiamond powder creates significant challenges for both suppliers and end-users. </p><p>While laboratory-scale applications demonstrate nanodiamonds' impressive capabilities, scaling these solutions for industrial production remains challenging. These technical hurdles have delayed commercialization of several promising nanodiamond applications, particularly in the polymer composites sector where uniform dispersion is critical for mechanical performance.</p><p>
