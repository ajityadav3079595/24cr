</p><p>High Carbon Chromium Bearing Steel is a high carbon alloy steel that contains chromium in the range of 1.30-1.65%. This type of steel is designed to provide enhanced wear resistance, durability, and high fatigue strength. It is primarily used in the manufacture of rolling bearings that operate under high-stress conditions. The presence of chromium in the alloy imparts improved resistance to corrosion and oxidation, making it suitable for use in challenging environments. This steel is also heat-treated to achieve high hardness, which is crucial for its performance in high-load applications.</p><p>
</p><p>Key products made from high carbon chromium bearing steel include precision ball bearings, roller bearings, and tapered bearings, which are integral components in machines that require precise motion control and load-bearing capacities.</p><p>
