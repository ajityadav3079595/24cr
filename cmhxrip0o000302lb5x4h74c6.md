</p><p>Pentamethyldisiloxane (CAS 1438-82-0) is a colorless organosilicon liquid with the molecular formula C5H16OSi2. This compound serves as a key intermediate in silicone chemistry, primarily used to manufacture modified silicone oils through hydrosilylation processes that introduce functional groups like polyether, epoxy, amino, or long-chain alkyl moieties. These derivatives find applications across multiple high-growth industries.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by expanding applications in cosmetics, agriculture, and polyurethane foam production, where pentamethyldisiloxane's low surface tension and thermal stability are highly valued. While purity grades (98% and 99%) constitute the majority of production, emerging applications in electronics and medical devices present new opportunities for market expansion.</p><p>
