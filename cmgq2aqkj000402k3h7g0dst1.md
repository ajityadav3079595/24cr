</p><p>High temperature superconductors (HTS) are materials that exhibit superconductivity at relatively high temperatures compared to traditional superconductors. Superconductivity refers to a phenomenon where a material can conduct electricity without resistance when cooled below a certain critical temperature.</p><p>
</p><p>G1 High Temperature Superconductors, specifically, fall under the first generation of HTS and include Bismuth-based compounds such as BSCCO-2212 and BSCCO-2223. These materials are known for their ability to operate at liquid nitrogen temperatures (~77K), which makes them more feasible for practical applications than earlier low-temperature superconductors that require expensive and complex helium cooling systems.</p><p>
</p><p>Applications of G1 HTS span across multiple domains including electric power equipment (such as fault current limiters and transformers), medical imaging (MRI systems), and industrial machinery requiring high magnetic fields or low energy losses.</p><p>
