<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>

</p><p>The PEM electrolysis catalyst market faces significant constraints from its reliance on platinum group metals (PGMs), which account for <strong>60-70%</strong> of current catalyst formulations. With global PGM prices experiencing <strong>25-35%</strong> volatility annually and South Korea importing <strong>95%</strong> of its PGM requirements, the market remains exposed to supply disruptions and price shocks. Recent geopolitical tensions have further exacerbated these vulnerabilities, prompting concerns about stable catalyst supply for Korea's expanding electrolyzer manufacturing base.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Durability Challenges in Industrial-Scale Applications Limit Market Penetration</strong></p><p>
</p><p>While PEM electrolysis catalysts demonstrate adequate performance in laboratory conditions, industrial deployment reveals persistent durability issues. This performance gap has led to warranty claims exceeding <strong>$15 million</strong> annually in the Korean market, creating reluctance among project financiers to support large-scale PEM-based hydrogen projects without substantial performance guarantees.</p><p>
