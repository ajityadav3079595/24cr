<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Environmental Regulations Limiting Industrial Applications</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Stringent environmental policies regarding bromine compounds are significantly impacting market growth potential. Potassium bromide faces regulatory scrutiny in many regions due to concerns about bromine accumulation in ecosystems. Several countries have implemented strict discharge limits for bromide compounds in industrial wastewater, increasing production compliance costs by an estimated 15-20% over the past five years.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Other Restraints</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Competition from Alternatives</strong></p><p>
The development of alternative anticonvulsant medications with fewer side effects is gradually reducing pharmaceutical applications. In water treatment applications, ozone and UV technologies are displacing bromide-based systems in many municipalities.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Safety Concerns</strong></p><p>
Handling and transportation regulations for potassium bromide have become more stringent due to its classification as an irritant. These requirements increase operational costs for manufacturers and end-users alike.</p><p>

</p><p>The potassium bromide market faces significant challenges in securing stable bromine supplies, as over 70% of global production originates from just three countries. Recent geopolitical tensions have exposed vulnerabilities in this concentrated supply chain, leading to price volatility that sometimes exceeds 30% year-over-year fluctuations. Manufacturers are struggling to maintain consistent pricing while meeting purity requirements.</p><p>

</p><p><strong>Quality Consistency Requirements</strong></p><p>
Pharmaceutical and electronics grade potassium bromide must meet exacting purity standards (typically 99.9% or higher), creating significant production challenges. Even minor impurities can render batches unsuitable for high-value applications.</p><p>
