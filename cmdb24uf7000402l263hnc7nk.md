</p><p>Di-Ammonium Phosphate (DAP) is one of the most widely used phosphorus fertilizers in the world, known for its high nutrient content and excellent physical properties. Chemically composed of ammonium and phosphate ions (NH4)2HPO4, DAP provides essential nutrients—nitrogen (18%) and phosphorus (46%)—that promote plant growth, especially in early development stages. It is especially valued in agriculture for its ability to enhance root establishment and crop yield. The substance appears either in granulated or crystalline form and is applied across various crop types, including cereals, grains, oilseeds, and fruits.</p><p>
</p><p>DAP also plays a role in industrial applications, particularly in fire retardants, metal finishing, and yeast cultivation, though its primary use remains in the agricultural sector. The product's effectiveness, ease of storage, and compatibility with various soil types make it an indispensable component in global agricultural practices.</p><p>
