</p><p>Trypsin is a <strong>serine protease enzyme</strong> that plays a critical role in protein digestion and cell culture applications. Naturally found in the digestive systems of vertebrates, this enzyme specifically cleaves peptide bonds at the carboxyl side of lysine and arginine amino acids. While it occurs naturally in animals, commercial production primarily utilizes porcine and bovine sources due to their high yield and stability.</p><p>
</p><p>The market growth is driven by expanding applications in <strong>biopharmaceutical manufacturing</strong> and research laboratories, particularly for cell dissociation and protein digestion processes.
