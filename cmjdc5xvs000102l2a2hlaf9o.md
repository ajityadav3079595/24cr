<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Limited Raw Material Availability and Seasonal Variability</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The glucosinolates market faces significant supply chain challenges due to the seasonal nature and geographic concentration of cruciferous vegetable production. This creates pricing volatility, with costs fluctuating up to 30% between peak and off-peak harvest seasons. The perishable nature of these raw materials further complicates supply chain logistics and increases production costs for glucosinolate extracts.</p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>High Extraction and Purification Costs</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The complex extraction and stabilization processes required for glucosinolates present another barrier to market growth. </p><p>Market penetration is further constrained by the technical challenges of standardizing glucosinolate concentrations across product batches, particularly for applications requiring consistent dosing in pharmaceutical and nutraceutical formulations. These factors collectively limit the broader adoption of glucosinolate ingredients in price-sensitive market segments.</p><p>
