</p><p>Low Soda Alumina is defined as a form of high-purity alumina (Al2O3) that has a sodium oxide (Na2O) content of less than 0.1%. This product is valued for its high temperature resistance, excellent electrical insulation properties, high hardness, and wear resistance. These characteristics make it an essential material for industries where performance and durability are critical, such as in electronic ceramics (e.g., semiconductors and insulating components), wear-resistant ceramics (e.g., cutting tools and industrial machinery), and refractory materials (e.g., heat-resistant linings for industrial furnaces). The market for Low Soda Alumina is primarily divided into two categories based on sodium content: <strong>Low Sodium</strong> (Na2O: 0.05%-0.1%) and <strong>Ultra Low Sodium</strong> (Na2O: ≤0.05%).</p><p>
