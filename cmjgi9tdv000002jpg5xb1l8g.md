<strong>MARKET RESTRAINTS</strong></p><p>
</p><p style="text-align:center"><strong>Supply Chain Volatility and Raw Material Shortages Pose Significant Challenges</strong></p><p>
</p><p>The litsea oil market faces substantial production challenges due to the limited geographical distribution of Litsea cubeba trees, primarily concentrated in Southern China. </p><p><strong>Other Constraints</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Regulatory Complexity</strong></p><p>
Divergent international regulations regarding essential oil composition specifications create compliance hurdles for exporters. The EU's recent revision of maximum permissible citral levels in food products (down to 0.5% from 1.0%) requires costly reformulations.</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Extraction Limitations</strong></p><p>
Traditional steam distillation methods yield only 2-3% oil from raw fruit, necessitating large quantities of raw material. Emerging supercritical CO2 extraction methods, while more efficient (5-6% yield), require capital investments exceeding $2 million per facility.</p><p>

</p><p>The absence of ISO or USP-grade specifications forces buyers to conduct extensive in-house testing, adding 15-20% to procurement costs.
